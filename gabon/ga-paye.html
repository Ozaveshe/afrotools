Gabon Tax FAQ

Common Gabon PAYE Questions

What are the Gabon IRPP tax bands for 2025/26?

Eight annual bands: 0% on first XAF 1,500,000; 5% on XAF 1.5M–2M; 10% on XAF 2M–3M; 15% on XAF 3M–5M; 20% on XAF 5M–7M; 25% on XAF 7M–10M; 30% on XAF 10M–15M; 35% above XAF 15,000,000. Monthly PAYE withholding is 1/12 of the annual IRPP. Applied to taxable income (gross minus CNSS 2.5%).

Is CNSS contribution deductible from IRPP taxable income?

Yes — the employee's mandatory 2.5% CNSS contribution is deductible from gross income before IRPP is calculated. This reduces your taxable income and therefore your IRPP. The employer CNSS contribution (16.6%) is a separate employer cost and does not affect employee take-home pay.

What is CNSS in Gabon?

The Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale (CNSS) is Gabon's mandatory social security body covering pension, family allowances, and occupational risk. Employees contribute 2.5% of gross salary and employers contribute 16.6% — one of the highest employer rates in francophone Africa. There is no ceiling on CNSS contributions.

How is secondary employment taxed in Gabon?

Secondary employment income in Gabon is subject to IRPP at a flat withholding rate of 30% at source with no deductions or tax-free threshold. The secondary employer withholds 30% of the full gross amount and remits it to the DGI. This prevents progressive bands from being claimed twice.

What is the total employer cost in Gabon?

Employers in Gabon pay the gross salary plus CNSS employer contribution of 16.6%. On a monthly gross of XAF 1,000,000, the total employer cost is XAF 1,166,000 — a significant payroll cost that makes Gabon one of the more expensive hiring environments in Central Africa.

When must employers remit IRPP and CNSS in Gabon?

Employers must remit IRPP withholding to the DGI and CNSS contributions to the Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale on a monthly basis. Declarations are typically due by the 15th of the following month. Late filing attracts penalties and interest. The DGI can be contacted at dgi.gouv.ga.

How Gabon IRPP Tax Is Calculated 2025/26

Gabon's employment income tax (Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques — IRPP) is administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts (DGI) and assessed on an annual basis. Employers withhold monthly. The employee's mandatory CNSS social security contribution (2.5%) is deducted from gross salary before IRPP is applied. Gabon uses progressive annual IRPP bands from 0% up to 35% at the top bracket. The CNSS employer contribution (16.6%) is among the highest in Central Africa, making Gabon's total employment cost significantly above the regional average.

Social security in Gabon is managed by the Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale (CNSS). Employees pay 2.5% of gross salary, covering pensions, healthcare, and disability benefits. This 2.5% is fully deductible from gross before IRPP tax is calculated. The employer pays a significantly higher 16.6% of gross salary — making total CNSS 19.1% of gross. This is one of the highest employer contribution rates in francophone Africa, reflecting Gabon's oil-wealth-funded social systems.

Gabon's progressive IRPP bands start with a 0% threshold, then step up gradually to 35% at the highest income levels. Monthly IRPP withholding and CNSS remittances are due by the 15th of the following month to the DGI and CNSS respectively. Gabon uses the XAF (Central African CFA franc), fixed to the euro at 655.957 — the same currency as Cameroon, Congo, and other CEMAC member states.

Gabon is a middle-income country with significant oil and natural resource revenues. The DGI has been modernising tax administration. Employers hiring both nationals and expatriates should note that work permit requirements and social security treaties may affect contribution obligations. Verify current IRPP bands and CNSS rates at the DGI Gabon.

Gabon IRPP: How Income Tax Works

Gabon's Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques (IRPP) is a progressive income tax administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts (DGI) under the Code Général des Impôts. Tax is calculated on annual taxable income — which equals gross salary minus any deductible CNSS employee contributions (2.5%). The annual IRPP is then divided by 12 for monthly withholding. All employees working in Gabon are subject to IRPP, regardless of nationality.

Social security in Gabon is managed by the Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale (CNSS). Employees contribute 2.5% of gross salary, while employers contribute 16.6%. The CNSS employee contribution is deductible from taxable income before IRPP is calculated. There is no Skills Development Levy or Workers Compensation Fund equivalent deducted via payroll in Gabon.

IRPP is filed and remitted monthly by the employer on behalf of employees. Employers must submit payroll declarations and pay IRPP by the 15th of the following month via the DGI portal. Failure to withhold and remit IRPP correctly can result in penalties. The tax year in Gabon follows the calendar year (January–December), and annual reconciliation is required for employees with multiple income sources.

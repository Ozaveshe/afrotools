Namibia Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Namibia PAYE tax bands?

Seven annual bands from 0% on first N$100,000 up to 37% above N$1,500,000. Rates increase progressively with income. SSC contribution (N$972/year) is deductible from taxable income.

What is SSC in Namibia?

Social Security Commission contribution: N$81/month flat (N$972/year) for both employee and employer. Employee contribution is deductible from taxable income for PAYE purposes.

Is SSC deductible from taxable income?

Yes, the employee SSC contribution (N$81/month) is deductible from gross pay before calculating PAYE. This reduces your taxable income and PAYE liability.

When must employers remit PAYE and SSC?

PAYE must be filed and paid by the 20th of the month following payroll. SSC contributions are due by the same date. Late payments attract penalties and interest.

What is the tax-free threshold in Namibia?

The first N$100,000 of annual taxable income (after SSC deduction) is taxed at 0%. Employees earning below this threshold pay no income tax — only the flat N$81/month SSC contribution.

How is Namibia PAYE calculated?

Annually: first deduct the N$972/year SSC employee contribution from gross salary. The resulting taxable income is then run through seven progressive NamRA bands (0%–37%). Take-home pay equals gross minus SSC minus PAYE tax.

What is the total employer cost in Namibia?

Employers pay the gross salary plus a flat N$81/month SSC contribution (N$972/year). Unlike percentage-based systems, the employer SSC cost is the same regardless of salary level. Employers must also withhold and remit employee SSC and PAYE tax to NamRA.

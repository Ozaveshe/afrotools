Madagascar Tax FAQ

Common Questions

What is Madagascar's PAYE structure?

Two bands: 0% on first MGA 350,000/month, and 20% on income above that. CNAPS pension is 1% employee + 13% employer, capped at MGA 1.61M/month.

Is CNAPS deductible from taxable income?

Yes. The 1% CNAPS employee contribution is deductible from gross before calculating PAYE tax. Only 1% is deductible; above-cap amounts are not.

What is the CNAPS cap?

CNAPS contributions are capped at MGA 1.61M per month. Once your gross salary reaches this contribution ceiling, no further CNAPS is deducted.

How is Madagascar PAYE calculated?

Monthly: deduct CNAPS (1%, capped), then apply 0% band (first 350k) and 20% band (above 350k) to the remaining taxable income.

What is the tax-free threshold in Madagascar?

The first MGA 350,000 of monthly taxable income (after CNAPS deduction) is taxed at 0%. Employees earning below this threshold pay no income tax — only the CNAPS pension contribution.

What is the total employer cost in Madagascar?

Employers pay the gross salary plus a 13% CNAPS contribution (capped at MGA 1.61M/month). For example, on a monthly salary of MGA 2,000,000, the employer CNAPS is MGA 209,300 (13% of the cap), making the total employer cost MGA 2,209,300 per month.

What does CNAPS cover in Madagascar?

The Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale (CNAPS) covers retirement pensions, disability benefits, and survivor benefits. The combined rate is 14% of gross salary (capped) — 1% from the employee and 13% from the employer.

Madagascar PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Madagascar uses a two-band income tax system administered by DGID (Direction Générale des Impôts et des Domaines). The first MGA 350,000 of monthly taxable income is exempt at 0%, and all income above that threshold is taxed at a flat 20%. CNAPS pension (1% employee, capped) is deductible before tax calculation.

Madagascar uses the Malagasy ariary (MGA). CNAPS (Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale) employee contribution is capped at MGA 1,610,000/month gross — meaning the maximum CNAPS deduction is MGA 16,100/month. The employer CNAPS is 13% up to the same cap. Salaries above the cap incur no additional CNAPS.

The simplicity of Madagascar's binary system (0% / 20%) means compliance is straightforward. Effective tax rates range from 0% (for those below MGA 350k/month) up to just under 20% for very high earners. Madagascar is an island nation east of mainland Africa, not part of WAEMU or CEMAC, using the independent ariary since 2005.

Compared to most African countries with 4–9 progressive bands, Madagascar's two-band system is among the simplest on the continent. The 20% flat rate above threshold compares to Mauritius (up to 20%), Namibia (up to 37%), and South Africa (up to 45%), making Madagascar relatively competitive for higher-income earners.

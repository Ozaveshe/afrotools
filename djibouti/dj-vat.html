Current legal picture

Three treatments, kept separate

  • 10% current standard rate.
  • 0% only for exact Article 19 export and international-trade cases.
  • Exempt only for an exact Article 8 item; exemption does not preserve input-tax deduction the same way as 0%.
  • Monthly declaration and payment are due by the 20th under Articles 38–39.

Frequently asked

Djibouti VAT answers

Why does the old law page say 7%?

That page preserves the original 2008 wording. Later official finance laws explicitly use 10% VAT, which this reviewed calculator applies.

Is every export automatically 0%?

No. Article 19 requires the defined transaction and supporting customs or other official evidence.

Calculation methodology

The calculator applies the current 10% standard rate and rounds VAT to the nearest Djibouti franc. It exposes 0% only after exact export or international-trade evidence and exposes an exempt path only after exact Article 8 confirmation. The FDJ 80 million and FDJ 120 million rules are review screens and do not independently decide liability.

Known limitations

  • Informational estimate only. It is not professional tax, legal, payroll, or filing advice.
  • The public 2008 law page preserves the original 7% wording; later official finance laws evidence the current 10% rate, so users should confirm the consolidated CGI before filing.
  • Zero-rating and exemptions require transaction-level legal, customs and invoice evidence.
  • Sector-specific, regional, treaty, relief, and special-regime rules may not be fully modeled.
  • Confirm filing, registration, and remittance duties with the official authority or a qualified adviser before submission.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to send money to Africa?
There is no permanent cheapest provider. The best route depends on amount, corridor, FX spread, payout method, speed, cash-out cost, and recipient access. Compare the live quote before sending.
Which African country has the highest fixed deposit rates?
Nominal deposit rates can change quickly and high rates may come with inflation, tax, liquidity, currency, or provider risk. Compare real return and verify current bank terms before placing funds.
Are digital lending apps (Branch, Tala, FairMoney) safe?
Some digital lenders are licensed or supervised in specific markets, but terms, fees, data practices, and collection behaviour vary. Verify licensing, APR, penalties, privacy terms, and repayment timing before borrowing.
How do T-bills compare to fixed deposits in Africa?
T-bills and fixed deposits differ on issuer risk, access route, tax, liquidity, discount pricing, early exit, and currency exposure. Verify the current auction, bank, broker, or treasury terms before investing.
What payment gateway should I use for my African business?
The best gateway depends on country, customer channel, settlement currency, payment method, dispute handling, integrations, settlement timing, and monthly volume. Compare total cost and test provider availability before committing.

From mobile wallets to bank transfers and payment gateways, fees can include fixed charges, percentage charges, cash-out costs, settlement delays, FX spreads, and provider limits. Use the tools to compare scenarios before checking the live provider quote.

Digital Lending & Credit Access

Short-term loans, digital lending apps, BNPL products, and microfinance offers can look affordable until fees, rollovers, penalties, and APR are compared. Use the lending calculators to estimate cost and then verify the lender's current disclosures.

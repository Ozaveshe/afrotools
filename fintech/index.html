Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to send money to Africa?
Wise typically offers the best exchange rates (lowest FX markup) while Sendwave charges 0% fees for transfers to Kenya and Ghana. For larger amounts, Lemfi and WorldRemit often beat Western Union and MoneyGram on both fees and exchange rates. Always compare using our Remittance Fee Comparator before sending.
Which African country has the highest fixed deposit rates?
Nigeria and Ghana currently offer the highest fixed deposit rates in Africa — often 18-30% per annum — but these are in high-inflation currencies. Your real return (after inflation) may be lower. Use our Real Return Calculator to compare properly.
Are digital lending apps (Branch, Tala, FairMoney) safe?
Yes, major apps like Branch, Tala, Carbon, and FairMoney are licensed by their respective central banks. However, their effective APRs can be very high (100-300% annualized). Always use our Digital Lending Comparator to understand the true cost before borrowing.
How do T-bills compare to fixed deposits in Africa?
Treasury bills are generally safer (government-backed) and often offer competitive rates, especially in Nigeria (22-28%) and Ghana (28-35%). They are more liquid than fixed deposits (can be sold on secondary market) but require a brokerage account or bank access to purchase.
What payment gateway should I use for my African business?
It depends on your country. Paystack and Flutterwave are the top choices for Nigeria, Kenya, and Ghana. Peach Payments leads in South Africa. DPO Group has broad pan-African coverage. Use our Payment Gateway Comparator to see costs for your specific transaction volume.

How the estimate is built

  1. Use taxable monthly cash and in-kind employment remuneration.
  2. Apply the eight marginal Schedule A bands.
  3. If selected, calculate public-sector pension on user-entered basic salary.
  4. Do not subtract pension from the employment-tax base without authority supporting that deduction.
  5. Show annual figures as twelve repeated monthly cases.

Private-sector, special-service, daily-worker, diplomatic and other exclusions require separate review.

Calculation methodology

The calculator applies the located eight-band monthly marginal schedule to taxable cash and in-kind employment remuneration. It models no generic private-sector contribution. When the user selects the regular public-sector scenario, employee pension at 5% and government/state-employer pension at 7% apply to user-entered pensionable basic salary and remain separate from the employment-tax base.

Known limitations

  • Informational estimate only. It is not professional tax, legal, payroll, or filing advice.
  • No newer consolidated primary schedule was located, so the result is a statutory reference rather than a claim of newly enacted 2026 rates.
  • Irregular daily-worker, diplomatic, short-presence, multiple-employer, benefit and special-regime treatment requires separate review.
  • No universal private-sector pension or social-security rate is modeled.
  • Confirm filing, registration, and remittance duties with the official authority or a qualified adviser before submission.

Test-case examples

Input: Monthly taxable employment income: ERN 10,000; tax-only scenario.
Expected: Employment income tax is ERN 2,795 and estimated monthly cash after tax is ERN 7,205.
Why: Covers all eight marginal bands without inventing a private-sector contribution.

Input: Monthly taxable income ERN 10,000; public-sector pensionable basic salary ERN 8,000.
Expected: Tax is ERN 2,795; employee pension ERN 400; employer pension ERN 560; estimated cash is ERN 6,805; employer cost is ERN 10,560.
Why: Protects the public-sector scope, pensionable-basic base and non-deductibility from employment tax.

What this calculator deliberately does not do

It does not decide classification, current legal force, registration, refunds, input credits, invoicing, filing or payment. It also does not convert this sales tax into VAT.

Why the old calculator was unsafe

It simultaneously claimed 0% VAT and described a 5% sales tax. It also offered reverse VAT, withholding and an AI adviser without a defensible Eritrean legal basis.

Sources and freshness

What the public evidence supports

  • IMF summary of the tax system (as of 1999): Proclamation 64/1994 and Legal Notice 22/1995, with goods and service schedules.
  • IMF 2003 Article IV staff report: reports the later three-band 0%, 5%, 12% goods structure.

Conflict: the detailed 1999 summary includes a 3% goods band; the later report says that band was removed. Neither source confirms 2026 treatment.

Calculation methodology

This is an evidence-gated historical sales-tax planning reference, not a current VAT calculator. It performs add/extract arithmetic only after the user confirms an exact listed-goods or listed-services evidence match. Every result remains marked as not current-rate-confirmed.

Known limitations

  • Informational estimate only. It is not professional tax, legal, payroll, or filing advice.
  • The located authoritative public summary is dated December 2002 and does not establish the rates or legal regime in force in 2026.
  • The evidence describes a single-stage sales tax rather than a conventional input-credit VAT system.
  • Confirm current classification, registration, invoicing, filing, payment, exemptions, and legal force with a current Eritrean authority or qualified adviser.

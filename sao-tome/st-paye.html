Why the IRS result is paused

A serious payroll tool should show uncertainty instead of turning an old rule into a current claim. The evidence trail does not yet establish a safe 2026 IRS withholding table.

2009The IRS code published an annual table in the former dobra denomination.
2016An official decree amended the table's deduction amounts.
2023A high-income tier and IERS were explicitly temporary until the next State Budget.
2026Law 3/2026 does not re-enact that overlay and authorizes a future IRS revision.

Until the tax authority publishes or confirms the operative employee schedule, AfroTools reports only the current INSS calculation. The amount labelled “pay after INSS” must not be treated as final net salary.

Calculation methodology

The checker applies the verified 4% employee and 6% employer INSS rates to monthly gross remuneration. It reports pay after employee INSS and employer cost. It deliberately returns no IRS or final-net value while the current official IRS schedule is unconfirmed.

Known limitations

  • IRS withholding and final take-home pay are intentionally not calculated.
  • No claim is made that employee INSS reduces the IRS taxable base.
  • Special remuneration, exemptions, contribution coverage and filing treatment require confirmation with the authorities.
  • Planning aid only; it is not professional tax, legal, payroll or filing advice.

Benin PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Benin's PAYE system is administered by the DGI (Direction Générale des Impôts) using seven progressive annual bands from 0% to 25%. The tax-free threshold is XOF 300,000 per year (XOF 25,000 per month). Benin uses the West African CFA Franc (XOF), shared with other UEMOA member states including Senegal and Côte d'Ivoire.

Employees contribute to CNPS (Caisse Nationale de Protection Sociale) at 2.5% of gross salary, with employers contributing 9.5%. CNPS contributions are deductible from taxable income before DGI rates are applied. PAYE is remitted monthly to the DGI.

Benin Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Benin DGI progressive tax bands for 2025/26?

Seven annual bands: 0% on first XOF 300,000; 2% on XOF 300,001–600,000; 5% on XOF 600,001–1,200,000; 10% on XOF 1,200,001–2,400,000; 15% on XOF 2,400,001–4,800,000; 20% on XOF 4,800,001–9,600,000; 25% above XOF 9,600,000. All rates apply to taxable income after CNPS deduction.

Is CNPS contribution deductible from DGI taxable income?

Yes — the employee's mandatory 2.5% CNPS contribution is fully deductible from gross pay before calculating DGI. This reduces your taxable income and therefore your DGI tax. The employer's 9.5% contribution is a separate employer cost, not deducted from your pay.

When are Benin PAYE taxes calculated and paid?

Benin PAYE is calculated annually based on total employment income for the fiscal year. Employers withhold PAYE monthly from salaries and remit to DGI by the 15th of the following month. Annual reconciliation occurs at year-end.

What is the total CNPS contribution rate in Benin?

The total CNPS (Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale) contribution is 12% of gross salary: 2.5% paid by the employee (deductible from DGI taxable income) and 9.5% paid by the employer as an additional cost. There is no upper cap on CNPS contributions.

Are there any other mandatory deductions in Benin?

Beyond DGI income tax and CNPS, Benin does not have separate levies equivalent to Tanzania's SDL or WCF. Employees may have deductions for pension contributions, health insurance, or trade union dues depending on their employment contract.

What is the salary in the calculator equivalent to in monthly terms?

The annual gross salary you enter is divided by 12 to show a monthly equivalent salary. However, Benin income tax is calculated on annual total income, not monthly bands like some other countries. Use the monthly equivalent for salary planning purposes only.

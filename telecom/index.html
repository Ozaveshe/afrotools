Frequently Asked Questions

Which African country has the cheapest mobile data?

Egypt, Morocco, and South Africa consistently rank among the cheapest for mobile data in Africa. Egypt averages around 15 EGP/GB while South Africa averages R60/GB. Use our Data Plan Comparator to find the best deals in your country.

Can I keep my phone number when switching operators?

Yes, in most African countries! Mobile Number Portability (MNP) is available in Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, Egypt, and many others. The process typically takes 24-72 hours. Check our Number Portability Guide for your country's specific process.

Is SIM registration mandatory in Africa?

Yes, virtually all African countries now require SIM registration linked to a national ID. In Nigeria it's NIN-SIM linkage, in Kenya it's National ID/Huduma Namba, and in South Africa it's RICA. Unregistered SIMs face disconnection.

Is Starlink available in Africa?

Starlink is available in Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Mozambique, and several other African countries as of 2026. Pricing varies by country. Use our Starlink vs Local ISP tool to compare costs with local alternatives.

How much does roaming cost in Africa?

Roaming costs vary widely. Within regional blocs (like EAC or ECOWAS), rates are lower due to roaming agreements. Generally expect 2-5x your home rates. Our Roaming Cost Calculator gives you exact estimates per operator and destination.

Eswatini — formerly Swaziland — taxes employment income through a progressive PAYE system with four bands rising to 33%, administered by the SRA. The tax year runs April to March, aligned with South Africa. Employers deduct PAYE monthly and remit to the SRA.

Pension provision is managed through the SNPF — Swaziland National Provident Fund. Employees contribute 5% of gross salary; employers contribute 10%. The SNPF operates as a defined contribution fund accumulating individual member balances for retirement.

Eswatini is a small landlocked kingdom surrounded by South Africa on three sides and Mozambique on the fourth. The Swazi Lilangeni (SZL) is pegged 1:1 to the South African Rand. Sugar and textiles are major export industries.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tax year in Eswatini?
Eswatini's tax year runs April to March, aligned with South Africa's fiscal year. PAYE returns follow this cycle.
What are SNPF rates in Eswatini?
Employees contribute 5%; employers contribute 10% to SNPF. SNPF is a defined contribution fund accumulating balances for retirement.
Is the SZL the same as the South African Rand?
The Swazi Lilangeni (SZL) is pegged 1:1 to the South African Rand and interchangeable within Eswatini. ZAR is also accepted, but SZL is not accepted in South Africa.

About Cabo Verde's Tax System

Cabo Verde applies income tax through the IUR — Imposto Único sobre o Rendimento — a unified tax covering employment income, business profits and investment returns. Employment income is taxed at progressive rates to 35%. Employers withhold IUR monthly and remit to the DNRE. Cabo Verde has one of West Africa's most advanced tax administration systems.

Social security is administered by INPS — Instituto Nacional de Previdência Social. Employees contribute 8.5% of gross salary; employers contribute 16%. INPS provides retirement pension, sickness, maternity and unemployment coverage — a broader social safety net than most African nations.

Cabo Verde is a small Atlantic island nation whose Escudo is pegged to the Euro. Tourism, remittances and financial services are the primary economic drivers, and the country's business-friendly environment attracts significant foreign investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the IUR in Cabo Verde?
IUR (Imposto Único sobre o Rendimento) is a unified income tax covering employment, business and investment income. Progressive rates rise to 35%. Employers withhold monthly and remit to the DNRE.
What are INPS rates in Cabo Verde?
Employees contribute 8.5%; employers contribute 16% to INPS. Benefits include pension, sickness, maternity and unemployment coverage.
What currency does Cabo Verde use?
The Cape Verdean Escudo (CVE), pegged to the Euro, providing exchange rate stability for this Atlantic island economy.

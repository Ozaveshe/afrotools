Togo PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Togo's salary tax is administered by OTR (Office Togolais des Recettes). For employment income, OTR applies the annual salary barème to net taxable pay after deductible employee social contributions, a 28% professional deduction on the first XOF 10,000,000, and family relief.

Togo uses the West African CFA franc (XOF), shared with other WAEMU countries and fixed to the euro. Under the 2023 salary barème, annual net taxable salary is exempt up to XOF 900,000 and then taxed progressively from 3% to 35% above XOF 20,000,000.

For salaried workers, CNSS (Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale) pension contributions are 4% employee and 17.5% employer. Before applying the OTR barème, salary income also benefits from a 28% professional deduction on the first XOF 10,000,000 and XOF 10,000 of relief per month for each dependent, capped at six dependents.

This calculator rounds annual net taxable salary down to the nearest XOF 1,000 before applying the barème, then rounds the resulting tax down to the nearest XOF 10, matching the wording of the OTR code. Monthly figures shown here are annual equivalents divided by 12 for planning purposes.

Togo Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Togo OTR progressive tax bands for 2025/26?

For salary income, OTR applies the annual barème to net taxable pay: 0% up to XOF 900,000; 3% from XOF 900,001 to 3,000,000; 10% from XOF 3,000,001 to 6,000,000; 15% from XOF 6,000,001 to 9,000,000; 20% from XOF 9,000,001 to 12,000,000; 25% from XOF 12,000,001 to 15,000,000; 30% from XOF 15,000,001 to 20,000,000; and 35% above XOF 20,000,000.

Is CNSS contribution deductible from OTR taxable income?

Yes. The employee CNSS pension contribution of 4% is deducted before OTR salary tax is computed. The employer's 17.5% contribution is a separate employer cost and is not deducted from the employee's pay.

When are Togo PAYE taxes calculated and paid?

Togo PAYE is calculated annually based on total employment income for the fiscal year. Employers withhold PAYE monthly from salaries and remit to OTR by the 15th of the following month. Annual reconciliation occurs at year-end.

What is the total CNSS contribution rate in Togo?

For salaried workers, the CNSS pension branch is 21.5% of salary in total: 4% paid by the employee and 17.5% by the employer.

Are there any other mandatory deductions in Togo?

The OTR salary rules also recognise a 28% professional deduction on the first XOF 10,000,000 of income and XOF 10,000 of relief per month for each dependent, up to six dependents. Some employers may also operate mandatory health-insurance payroll deductions, which are not separately requested on this page.

What is the salary in the calculator equivalent to in monthly terms?

The annual gross salary you enter is divided by 12 to show a monthly equivalent salary. However, Togo income tax is calculated on annual total income, not monthly bands like some other countries. Use the monthly equivalent for salary planning purposes only.

How the estimate is built

  1. Annualise the monthly gross salary.
  2. Deduct the modeled 4% employee CNSS contribution.
  3. Apply the 28% professional deduction to the eligible first XOF 10 million.
  4. Deduct family relief for up to six qualifying dependents.
  5. Round taxable income down to XOF 1,000, apply progressive bands, then round tax down to XOF 10.

Other deductions listed by the CGI are not assumed because this calculator does not collect the evidence needed to validate them.

Calculation methodology

The shared engine annualizes gross employment income, deducts the modeled 4% employee CNSS contribution, applies the 28% professional deduction to the eligible first XOF 10 million, subtracts XOF 120,000 annual relief per qualifying dependent up to six, rounds taxable income down to XOF 1,000, applies the eight progressive OTR bands, and rounds tax down to XOF 10. Employer CNSS at 17.5% is shown separately and is never deducted from employee take-home.

Known limitations

  • Planning estimate only; it is not an official assessment, payslip, tax return, filing service, or professional advice.
  • Benefits in kind, other mandatory insurance, voluntary retirement, life-insurance, housing-interest and case-specific deductions are not modeled.
  • Special regimes, exemptions, non-resident treatment and irregular remuneration require individual review.
  • Confirm payroll filing and remittance treatment with OTR, CNSS or a qualified payroll professional.

Test-case examples

Input: Annual gross salary: 0.
Expected: PAYE, CNSS and net pay are all zero.
Why: Establishes the zero-income baseline and catches negative liability regressions.

Input: Annual gross salary XOF 6,000,000 with two qualifying dependents.
Expected: Employee CNSS XOF 240,000; professional deduction XOF 1,612,800; dependent relief XOF 240,000; taxable income XOF 3,907,000; PAYE XOF 153,700; net XOF 5,606,300.
Why: Protects the complete standard-case calculation and statutory rounding stages.

Input: Annual gross salary XOF 24,000,000 with more than six dependents.
Expected: Dependent count is capped at six, professional deduction is capped at XOF 2,800,000 and the upper progressive bands are applied.
Why: Protects both statutory caps and high-income band progression.

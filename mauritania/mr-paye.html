Mauritania PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Mauritania's payroll income tax (ITS — Impôt sur les Traitements et Salaires) is administered by DGI (Direction Générale des Impôts). The official doctrine note exempts the first MRU 6,000 of monthly salary, then taxes the remaining taxable remuneration at 15%, 25%, and 40% using the published offset formulas. Employers withhold ITS monthly and remit it by the 15th of the following month.

Mauritania uses the ouguiya (MRU), introduced in 2018 as the "new ouguiya" replacing the old currency at 10:1. Unlike neighbouring Senegal and Mali which use XOF (WAEMU CFA franc), Mauritania is not a WAEMU member and MRU is independently managed. In practice, taxable remuneration is reduced by deductible worker CNSS and the monthly MRU 6,000 exemption before ITS is calculated.

The official CNSS declaration form shows a 1% worker contribution plus employer-side charges of about 15% once the 13% employer line and 2% occupational-medicine line are included. This calculator therefore deducts the worker contribution before ITS and shows employer-side charges separately in employer cost.

The ITS structure differs from many neighbouring payroll systems because the doctrine note combines a fixed monthly exemption with formula-based tax bands rather than a simple visible ladder. That makes the taxable-remuneration step especially important for accurate payroll estimates.

Mauritania Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Mauritania PAYE tax bands for 2025/26?

DGI exempts the first MRU 6,000 of monthly salary. The remaining taxable remuneration is then taxed at 15% up to MRU 9,000, 25% from MRU 9,001 to MRU 21,000, and 40% above MRU 21,000, using the published formulas 15% of RI, 25% of RI minus MRU 900, and 40% of RI minus MRU 4,050.

Is CNSS contribution deductible from ITS taxable income?

Yes. The DGI ITS doctrine allows mandatory worker social-security contributions to be deducted before tax is calculated. This page applies the worker CNSS contribution at 1% of pay, then subtracts the statutory MRU 6,000 monthly exemption before computing ITS.

What is ITS in Mauritania?

ITS (Impôt sur les Traitements et Salaires) is Mauritania's payroll income tax, administered by DGI (Direction Générale des Impôts). After the monthly MRU 6,000 exemption and deductible worker CNSS, the DGI doctrine applies 15%, 25%, and 40% formulas to the remaining taxable remuneration.

What currency does Mauritania use?

Mauritania uses the ouguiya (MRU). Following a 2018 currency reform, the "new ouguiya" (MRU) replaced the old ouguiya at 10:1. Unlike its WAEMU neighbours using XOF, Mauritania is not part of the franc zone and MRU floats on the open market.

What is the top ITS rate in Mauritania?

Mauritania's top ITS rate is 40%, applying once monthly taxable remuneration after deductions exceeds MRU 21,000. Effective rates are still lower because the worker CNSS deduction and the first MRU 6,000 of salary are removed before the formula is applied.

When must employers remit PAYE to DGI Mauritania?

ITS (PAYE) must be filed and paid to DGI by the 15th day of the following month. CNSS is remitted separately under its own timetable. Late payment attracts penalties under the Code Général des Impôts. Employers must file monthly returns even for nil-tax months.

Calculation methodology

The calculator annualizes salary where needed, applies modeled employee statutory deductions, runs taxable income through the country PAYE bands, and derives net pay from gross pay minus modeled PAYE and statutory deductions. Employer-cost lines are informational where the page exposes them.

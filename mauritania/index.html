Mauritania taxes employment income through the ITS — Impôt sur les Traitements et Salaires — with progressive rates rising to 33%. The Mauritanian Ouguiya (MRU) was redenominated in January 2018, replacing the old Ouguiya (MRO) at a rate of 1 MRU = 10 MRO. Employers withhold ITS monthly and remit to the DGI.

Social security is managed by CNSS — Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale. Employees contribute 1% of gross salary; employers contribute 14%. CNSS provides retirement pension, family allowances and work injury compensation for formally employed workers.

Mauritania is a large West African country bordering the Sahara Desert with Atlantic Ocean coastline providing significant fishing resources. Iron ore, gold and oil are important exports. The country bridges Arab North Africa and Sub-Saharan West Africa, being a member of both the Arab League and ECOWAS.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the MRU in Mauritania?
The MRU (new Ouguiya) was introduced in January 2018, replacing the old Ouguiya (MRO) at 1 MRU = 10 MRO. All tax thresholds are now denominated in MRU.
How is ITS calculated in Mauritania?
Mauritania's ITS applies progressive rates up to 33% on employment income. Employers withhold monthly and remit to the DGI.
What are Mauritania's main exports?
Iron ore from the Zouerate mines is Mauritania's primary export, followed by fishing revenues, gold and oil. The SNIM mining company is one of the largest formal sector employers.

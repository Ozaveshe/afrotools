# Swahili Beta Release Notes Draft

Date: 2026-05-16

Status: internal/product draft only. Not published live.

## One-Line Summary

The Swahili beta is now a coherent AfroTools product surface for salary, PAYE, VAT, PDF, business, country, construction, language, communications, career, and developer workflows, with strong registry-backed discovery and carried specialist/bridge debt clearly separated.

## What Is Strong Now

### Route Stability

The Swahili route surface is stable enough for beta review. The strategy checkpoint records `852` `/sw/` HTML routes, `850` indexable Swahili routes, and `465` direct one-level `/sw/zana/` routes. Later promotion reports show the registry grew without broken Swahili hrefs.

### Registry Discovery

Wave 8 raised direct `/sw/zana/` registry coverage from `367/465` to `400/465`. Wave 9 raised it again to `415/465`, or `89.25%`, with `700` unique Swahili registry hrefs and `0` broken Swahili registry hrefs at the promotion checkpoint.

### Search

Search is now beta-usable for Swahili intent buckets. The final tuning pass keeps strong results for `PAYE`, `VAT`, `PDF`, `CV`, `TIN`, `zakat`, `forodha`, `bima`, `afya`, `biashara`, `kilimo`, and `serikali`, and improves hub-first results for `mshahara`, `kazi`, `ujenzi`, `internet`, `tafsiri`, `API`, `mkopo`, and `sarafu`.

### Hreflang

Swahili hreflang is not carrying a fatal blocker. Recent validation passes complete successfully. The current carried warnings are reciprocal cleanup items involving a French business-name route and the intentional `/sw/blogu/` bridge to `/fr/blog/`.

### Core Lanes

Salary/PAYE and VAT are the strongest Swahili entry points. The Swahili surface now has practical hubs, country routes, registry discovery, and search behavior that match how users look for salary, deductions, VAT, TIN, and business compliance tools.

### PDF And Documents

The PDF and document lanes have strong Swahili hubs and search exposure. PDF bridge and tool routes are discoverable, and the beta messaging keeps local/browser utility expectations clear.

### Business And Country Hubs

Country hubs and business-support routes are route-honest. Nigeria, Ghana, South Africa, Egypt, Morocco, Malawi, and Zambia received bounded hub/PAYE and business route review. Country HR pages were not over-promoted unless reports marked them safe.

### Construction

Construction, property, and engineering pages were polished before promotion. Wave 7/9 coverage now exposes stronger construction routes without using the registry as a raw count game.

### Language And Translation

The language lane is now discoverable through `/sw/lugha-na-tafsiri/`, translator tools, transliteration, PDF translation, and search tuning. The copy avoids certified/legal/medical translation claims.

### Communications

The communications lane has usable Swahili surfaces for data, internet, ISP, LTE, 5G, Starlink, USSD, SIM, SMS, roaming, WhatsApp Business, and mobile-money-adjacent routing without inventing telco prices or coverage.

### Career

The career and employment-document lane is now easier to find from `kazi`, `CV`, and `mshahara` searches. HR and legal language stays cautious where labor-law certainty would be risky.

## What Remains Carried Debt

### Specialist Review

Health, legal, religious/cultural, government-admin, finance, education, and HR-risk routes remain in the specialist queue. They are generally safe to remain discoverable with disclaimers, but most are not registry-promotion candidates without domain or human review.

### Blog And API Bridges

`/sw/blogu/` and `/sw/api/` are honest bridges, not full localization. Blog article links and API docs/pricing still open English-only destinations, but Swahili fallback routes are now attached and visible.

### Source Layer

The source-layer pilot is still design-only. The dry-run planner can report alias-aware Swahili output paths, but the real writer is not yet safe to create Swahili page packs without risking curated pages. No Swahili page-pack pilot was created.

### Non-Swahili Dirty-Tree Work

French JSON-LD candidates, French hreflang reciprocity warnings, Hausa/cars/generated churn, and other unrelated dirty-tree work should not be treated as Swahili beta blockers unless they break shared assets used by Swahili pages.

## Beta Positioning

Public beta should mean:

- Swahili users can enter from `/sw/`, `/sw/zana-zote/`, `/sw/tools/`, salary/PAYE, country hubs, PDF, business, construction, language, communications, career, and developer hubs.
- Shared search returns Swahili-first results for the major beta query buckets.
- Registry-backed discovery exposes only pages that are good enough for users.
- Known bridge-only and specialist-risk pages are clearly labeled as carried debt, not hidden surprises.

## Not In This Beta

- Full translation of all English blog articles.
- Full translation of API docs and pricing docs.
- Full specialist certification for health, legal, religious, finance, or government pages.
- Durable Swahili `lang/pages/**/sw.json` source migration.
- Cleanup of unrelated French, Hausa, cars, or generated-output dirty-tree work.

## Release Note Candidate

Swahili beta is ready for internal release-candidate review: the surface is broad, search is useful, registry discovery is quality-screened, core salary/PAYE and business lanes are strong, and remaining debt is mostly specialist review, bridge-only documentation, and future source-layer durability.

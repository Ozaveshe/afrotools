FAQ

PAYE Questions

What are the DGI tax bands?

Six annual bands: 0% on XAF 0–200k; 10% on 200k–500k; 15% on 500k–1M; 25% on 1M–3M; 40% on 3M–8M; 50% above 8M annually. Applied to taxable income after CNSS deduction.

Is CNSS deductible?

Yes — employee's 3% CNSS is fully deductible from gross before tax. Reduces taxable income. Employer's 18% is a separate cost.

Central African Republic PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

The Central African Republic levies IRPP (Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques) on employment income under a six-band progressive schedule administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts (DGI). Taxable income is annual gross salary minus CNSS contributions. The top marginal rate of 50% applies to annual income above XAF 8,000,000.

Social security contributions are split: employees pay 3% CNSS on gross wages, while employers contribute 18% — totalling 21% of payroll. CNSS covers pensions, family allowances, and occupational risk.

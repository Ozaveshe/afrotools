The Comoros applies income tax through a progressive IRPP with rates rising to 30% on employment income. Employers withhold income tax monthly from salaries and remit to the DGI. The Comoros comprises three main islands — Grande Comore, Anjouan and Mohéli — with federal tax policy set at the national level.

Social security is managed by the CNPS. Employees contribute approximately 4% of gross salary; employers contribute around 8%. CNPS provides pension and occupational risk coverage, though reach is limited given the small formal employment sector.

The Comoros is a small island nation in the Indian Ocean. Vanilla, ylang-ylang and cloves are key agricultural exports. The Comorian Franc (KMF) is pegged to the Euro via France's treasury guarantee mechanism, providing monetary stability.

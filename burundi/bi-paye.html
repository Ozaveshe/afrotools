Burundi PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Burundi's employment income tax (IPR — Impôt sur les Revenus) is administered by OBR (Office Burundais des Recettes). The system uses four monthly progressive bands from 0% to 35%, calculated on taxable income after deducting INSS (Institut National de Sécurité Sociale) employee contributions at 4%. Employers withhold IPR monthly and remit to OBR.

Burundi uses the Burundian franc (BIF). The tax-free threshold is BIF 150,000/month (BIF 1,800,000/year). Uniquely among East African nations, the first taxable band is immediately 20% — there are no low-rate transitional bands. This means a worker earning BIF 200,000/month pays 20% on the BIF 50,000 above the threshold.

INSS employee contribution of 4% of gross salary is deductible before IPR calculation. Compared to Tanzania's NSSF (10% employee), Burundi's INSS rate is significantly lower. There is no SDL (Skills Development Levy) or WCF equivalent in Burundi. The top rate of 35% applies above BIF 500,000/month — one of the lower top-rate thresholds in the region.

Unlike WAEMU countries using XOF, Burundi uses BIF and is part of the East African Community (EAC) alongside Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and Rwanda. Despite this regional integration, Burundi maintains its own distinct tax structure. The country's tax-free threshold in USD terms is relatively modest, reflecting the lower income levels.

Burundi Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Burundi PAYE tax bands for 2025/26?

Four monthly bands under OBR IPR: 0% on first BIF 150,000; 20% on BIF 150,001–300,000; 30% on BIF 300,001–500,000; 35% above BIF 500,000. Annual tax-free threshold: BIF 1,800,000. Bands applied against taxable income after INSS deduction (4%).

Is INSS contribution deductible from IPR taxable income?

Yes — the employee's INSS contribution (4% of gross) is deductible from gross pay before calculating IPR. This reduces taxable income and therefore the tax. INSS is administered by the Institut National de Sécurité Sociale of Burundi.

What is IPR in Burundi?

IPR (Impôt sur les Revenus) is Burundi's income tax on employment, similar to PAYE. It is administered by OBR (Office Burundais des Recettes). The four progressive monthly bands range from 0% to 35%. Burundi has one of Africa's higher starting rates (20%) after the tax-free threshold with no intermediate lower bands.

What currency does Burundi use?

Burundi uses the Burundian franc (BIF). Unlike East African neighbours Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda which use their own shillings, Burundi uses a franc-based currency. BIF is not pegged to any fixed rate and trades on the open market.

What is the top PAYE rate in Burundi?

Burundi's top IPR rate is 35%, applying to monthly taxable income above BIF 500,000. Uniquely, the first taxable band immediately above the threshold is 20% — there are no low intermediate rates. This creates a significant tax step when income crosses BIF 150,000/month.

When must employers remit PAYE to OBR?

IPR (PAYE) must be filed and paid to OBR by the 7th day of the following month. INSS contributions are remitted separately. Late payment attracts penalties under the Code Général des Impôts. Employers must file monthly returns even for nil-tax months.

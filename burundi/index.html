About Burundi

Burundi Tax & Finance Overview

Burundi's tax system is overseen by the Office Burundais des Recettes (OBR) and applies a progressive personal income tax known as the Impot Professionnel sur les Remunerations (IPR) with rates from 0% to 35%, denominated in the Burundian Franc (BIF). The progressive structure provides a tax-free threshold for the lowest earners, with rates increasing through intermediate bands before reaching the top rate of 35% on higher earnings. Employees in Burundi contribute to the Institut National de Securite Sociale (INSS), with the employee share typically at around 4% of gross salary and the employer contributing approximately 6%, covering old-age pension and occupational risk benefits. The formal social security system primarily covers workers in the structured private sector and public service. Burundi applies a standard VAT rate of 18% on most goods and services, with exemptions for essential foodstuffs, medical supplies, agricultural inputs, and educational services. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers must withhold PAYE from employee salaries and remit it to the OBR within the prescribed monthly deadlines. Burundi's economy is predominantly agricultural, with coffee and tea as primary exports, and the government has been working to broaden the tax base beyond the formal sector. Registration with the OBR is required for all businesses meeting the turnover threshold for VAT.

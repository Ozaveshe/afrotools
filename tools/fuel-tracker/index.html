Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about fuel prices in Africa.

Use the Price Comparison Table for the latest available petrol snapshot across African countries. AfroFuel shows local currency first and USD only for comparison, so always verify the local pump price before buying or quoting transport.
Sort the table by Petrol from low to high to see the cheapest country in the current snapshot. Avoid relying on a fixed country name because pump prices, subsidies, exchange rates, and update dates can change.
Choose petrol, diesel, or LPG, then sort the Price Comparison Table from high to low. The answer can differ by fuel type, so the live table is the safest source for the current comparison.
Open the Generator Cost Calculator, choose a country and fuel type, then enter generator size, litres per hour, hours per day, and days per month. AfroFuel estimates daily cost, monthly cost, litres used, annual cost, and USD comparison.
Diesel can cost more because of import costs, taxes, supply pressure from transport and industry, exchange rates, or how each country sets fuel prices. Compare petrol and diesel side by side in the table before planning fleet, shop, or generator costs.
A practical planning estimate is about 1.5 litres per hour for many 5 KVA petrol generators, but the real number depends on load, engine condition, fuel quality, and appliance use. Adjust the litres-per-hour field in the calculator if you know your generator's actual consumption.

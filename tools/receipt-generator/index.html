Professional Receipts & Invoices for African Businesses

Every business transaction should be documented with a proper receipt or invoice. Whether you're running a small shop in Lagos, a service business in Nairobi, or an online store in Johannesburg, providing professional documentation builds trust with customers and helps you maintain accurate financial records for tax purposes.

In Nigeria, FIRS requires businesses to issue receipts for all transactions. In South Africa, SARS expects tax invoices for VAT-registered businesses. Kenya's KRA similarly requires proper documentation. This generator supports VAT/TIN numbers to help meet these requirements.

This free tool creates clean, professional receipts and invoices that you can download as PDF or print directly. It supports 16 currencies including NGN, KES, ZAR, GHS, EGP, XOF, XAF, TZS, UGX, and more. Features include logo upload, QR code for payment links, status watermarks (PAID, DRAFT, etc.), and automatic tax/discount calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this receipt/invoice legally valid?

This generates a basic commercial receipt or invoice suitable for most small business transactions. For VAT invoices (required by FIRS, SARS, KRA, GRA), add your VAT/TIN number in the business details. Consult your accountant for full compliance with your specific tax authority.

How do I save receipts?

Click "Download PDF" for a direct PDF file, or use "Print" and select "Save as PDF" in your browser's print dialog. Your business details can be saved for future visits using the "Save" button.

What is the QR code for?

The QR code can encode any payment link (Paystack, Flutterwave, bank transfer details) or reference text. When your customer scans it, they'll be taken directly to the payment page or see the reference information.

What currencies are supported?

16 currencies: Nigerian Naira (NGN), Kenyan Shilling (KES), South African Rand (ZAR), Ghanaian Cedi (GHS), US Dollar (USD), Egyptian Pound (EGP), West African CFA (XOF), Central African CFA (XAF), Tanzanian Shilling (TZS), Ugandan Shilling (UGX), Rwandan Franc (RWF), Botswana Pula (BWP), Ethiopian Birr (ETB), Moroccan Dirham (MAD), British Pound (GBP), and Euro (EUR).

🌍 African Market Context

In most African cities, buying beats renting over 7+ years due to high rent inflation (8–15%/year), strong property appreciation, and the wealth-building effect of equity.

However, high mortgage rates (Nigeria 18–26%, Ghana 28%) make buying very expensive in the short term. If you plan to move within 3 years, renting is almost always cheaper.

South Africa, Egypt and Morocco have more moderate mortgage rates (10–14%) where the break-even point is typically 4–6 years.

❓ Common Questions

When does buying make more sense? +
Buying makes sense when: you plan to stay 5+ years, you have a stable income, local mortgage rates are below 15%, and rent inflation is high (above 8%/year). In SA, Kenya and Egypt this threshold is often 4–6 years.
When should I keep renting? +
Keep renting if: you may move within 3 years, you're in a city with very high mortgage rates (Nigeria, Ghana), your down payment capital can earn returns above the home's appreciation rate, or you need flexibility for career/family reasons.
What is the "break-even" point? +
The break-even point is the year at which total buying costs become lower than total renting costs. Before this point, renting is cheaper. After it, owning is cheaper. This tool shows you that point on the timeline.
Does this account for home equity? +
Yes. The calculator subtracts your estimated home equity (appreciated home value minus remaining mortgage balance) from buying costs, so you see the real net cost of owning, not just the payments made.

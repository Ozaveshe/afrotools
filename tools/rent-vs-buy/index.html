What the comparison excludes

No appreciation, rent growth, interest rate, tax rate, investment return, opportunity cost, country preset or break-even forecast is added. A lower entered cash cost is not advice to choose that path.

Define final cash carefully

For rent, use only cash expected back at the horizon. For buying, use your own net sale-cash figure after remaining loan principal, tax and selling costs. This tool does not calculate either amount.

What this never decides

  • Whether you qualify for a mortgage.
  • What a property will be worth.
  • How rent, tax or interest will change.
  • Whether flexibility or ownership suits you.
  • When either path reaches break-even.

Questions before relying on the comparison

Does the lower figure mean I should choose that option?

No. It means only that the entered net cash cost is lower over the entered horizon. Affordability, mobility, risk, title, condition and personal needs are outside this arithmetic.

Where do interest and appreciation go?

They are not modelled here. Enter a quoted mortgage payment, and if you want to include sale proceeds, enter one final net cash figure that already reflects your own separately documented assumptions.

Why is there no break-even year?

A break-even forecast would require time-varying rent, rates, property values, expenses and investment returns. This tool compares one equal horizon without inventing those paths.

Are my amounts uploaded or saved?

No. Calculation and exports run locally without storage or transmission.

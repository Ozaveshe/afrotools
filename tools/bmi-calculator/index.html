    BMI Categories

    • ● Underweight: < 18.5
    • ● Normal: 18.5 - 25
    • ● Overweight: 25 - 30
    • ● Obese: > 30

    What is BMI?

    BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. Calculated as weight(kg) ÷ height(m)².

    It's a screening tool for weight categories that may increase health risks, but doesn't measure body fat directly.

    BMR & TDEE

    BMR: Basal Metabolic Rate—calories your body burns at rest (Mifflin-St Jeor formula).

    TDEE: Total Daily Energy Expenditure—total calories burned including activity.

    Use TDEE as your baseline for weight goals.

    BMI & African Bodies

    Research suggests that the relationship between BMI, body fat, and health risks can vary across ethnic groups. Some studies indicate that people of African descent may have a lower body fat percentage at the same BMI compared to European populations.

    However, abdominal fat (measured by waist circumference) remains a strong predictor of metabolic risk. Use BMI alongside waist measurements for a fuller picture of your health.

    Health Tips

    • Protein at every meal aids muscle retention
    • Drink water before meals to aid appetite control
    • Sleep 7-9 hours for optimal metabolism
    • Reduce added sugars, increase fiber

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is BMI accurate for everyone?
    BMI is a useful screening tool but has limitations. It does not distinguish between muscle and fat mass, so athletes or muscular individuals may have a high BMI despite low body fat. It also does not account for age, sex, bone density, or fat distribution. The AfroTools BMI Calculator includes additional metrics like body fat estimation and waist-to-hip ratio for a more complete picture.
    What is considered a healthy BMI range?
    According to the WHO, a healthy BMI is between 18.5 and 24.9. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is classified as obese. However, these ranges are general guidelines and should be considered alongside other health indicators.
    Is my health data stored when I use the BMI calculator?
    No. All calculations run entirely in your browser. Your height, weight, and other measurements are never sent to any server or stored remotely. Your data stays private on your device.
    Does the BMI calculator support both metric and imperial units?
    Yes. You can toggle between metric (kg/cm) and imperial (lbs/inches) units. The calculator automatically converts between systems so you can use whichever you are most comfortable with.

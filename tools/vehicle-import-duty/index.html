Frequently Asked Questions

What is CIF value for vehicle imports?
CIF stands for Cost + Insurance + Freight. It is the price of the vehicle at the origin port, plus insurance during shipping, plus the shipping freight cost to the destination port. Customs duty is calculated on the CIF value, not just the purchase price. Always get a formal CIF valuation from your freight forwarder.
Which African country has the highest vehicle import duty?
Nigeria has the highest effective vehicle import duties in Africa — up to 75% total (35% import levy + 35% surcharge + 5% ECOWAS levy) on most vehicle types, making car imports very expensive. Ethiopia also has high rates with 100% excise on gasoline vehicles. Electric vehicles often get preferential rates across many African countries.
Can I import a vehicle older than 8 years into Kenya?
No. Kenya enforces an 8-year age limit on imported used vehicles (measured from year of manufacture). Rwanda enforces a strict 5-year limit. Uganda also has an 8-year limit. Violating these rules results in the vehicle being impounded or denied entry. Always check the manufacture date, not the registration year.
Are electric vehicles exempt from import duty in Africa?
Many African countries offer reduced or zero duty on electric vehicles to encourage adoption. Rwanda charges 0% duty on EVs (vs 25% for ICE vehicles). Ethiopia charges 5% excise on EVs vs 100% on gasoline vehicles. Nigeria charges 5% duty on EVs vs up to 75% on conventional cars. Always confirm current rates with your customs agent.
What other costs are involved besides duty?
Beyond duty and taxes, budget for: clearing agent fees (1-3% of CIF), port handling and demurrage fees (if delayed), vehicle inspection fees, NAFDAC/KEBS/standards authority fees, transport from port to destination, and registration costs. Total clearance costs can add 5-10% beyond the stated duty rates.

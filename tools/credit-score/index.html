What this index means

The five selected factor values are averaged equally into a 0–100 self-check index. The values and categories are explanatory prompts, not a reverse-engineered scoring model.

  • No report access: nothing is fetched from a lender or bureau.
  • No eligibility prediction: the result cannot tell whether credit will be approved or priced.
  • Current truth: obtain your report and dispute process from an authorised source in your jurisdiction.
Is this my real credit score?
No. It is an educational profile index calculated only from the five answers on this page. Actual models, data ranges and lender decisions vary.
What should I do if a report is wrong?
Use the current dispute procedure published by the authorised credit-report provider or regulator in your jurisdiction. Keep copies of the report, supporting records and correspondence.
Does a strong index guarantee approval?
No. A lender may consider verified report data, affordability, income, existing obligations, collateral, product policy and other factors.

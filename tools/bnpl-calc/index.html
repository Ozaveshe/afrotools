Is BNPL really 0% like PayFlex?
PayFlex (South Africa) and some other providers offer 0% if you pay within the agreed period (typically 4 weekly installments). However, late payment fees can be very high (5-15% of the purchase). The 0% is only truly free if you pay on time — the merchant typically subsidizes the fee. Always read the terms carefully.
Can BNPL affect my credit score?
Yes. Late BNPL payments are increasingly reported to credit bureaus. In South Africa, Zip reports to credit bureaus. In Kenya and Nigeria, BNPL defaults can affect your access to digital lending. On-time BNPL payments can positively build credit history for first-time borrowers.
When does BNPL make sense?
BNPL makes sense when: (1) The total fee is small relative to your cash flow benefit, (2) You absolutely need the item now and can repay on schedule, (3) The provider charges 0% with no conditions (like some PayFlex plans), (4) You're building credit history with on-time payments. Never use BNPL for discretionary wants — only needs.

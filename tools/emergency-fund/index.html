Frequently Asked Questions

User-set resilience target

How the target is calculated

The base target is monthly essential expenses multiplied by the coverage months you choose. Current savings reduce the gap but do not reduce the displayed target.

  • Timeline: gap divided by your monthly saving, rounded up to a whole month.
  • 12-month amount: gap divided by 12 without rounding away cents.
  • Inflation scenario: base target compounded by your entered annual rate for the selected years.

Revisit essential expenses after rent, debt, household or health changes. This is a planning tool, not a universal recommendation or a guarantee that a chosen product will remain accessible.

How many coverage months should I enter?
Choose a target based on how long replacing income may take, the stability of household income, dependants, essential obligations, insurance and other support available. Test more than one scenario.
What belongs in essential monthly expenses?
Include costs you would still need during an income interruption, such as housing, food, utilities, medicine, transport, minimum debt payments and essential support commitments.
Does the calculator recommend a savings product?
No. Compare current access rules, capital risk, protections, fees, taxes and withdrawal timing. Verify the institution and product through official sources in your jurisdiction.

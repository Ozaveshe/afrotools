TIN Registration in Congo (Brazzaville) — Complete Guide

The NIU (Numéro d'Identifiant Unique) is issued by the Direction Générale des Impôts et des Domaines (DGID) in Congo (Brazzaville). Registration is free and typically takes 3-7 business days. Registration is currently available in person at DGID offices. The TIN is governed by the Code Général des Impôts du Congo and administered by the Ministère des Finances.

All businesses operating in Congo (Brazzaville) and individuals in formal employment are required to register for a NIU. The NIU is a Variable alphanumeric identifier needed for tax filing, business bank accounts, government contracts, and customs clearance. Failure to register carries penalties: fines per CGI.

⚠️ Disclaimer This tool provides general information only. Tax laws and registration requirements change frequently. Always verify with DGID or a qualified tax advisor for the latest requirements.

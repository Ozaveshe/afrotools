    Tax identity setup

    A tax ID is the connector between registration, banking, invoices, payroll, tenders, tax filing, and cross-border payments. Treat it as an operating credential, not a one-time number.

    • Mismatched spelling between registry certificate, bank account and tax record
    • Assuming a tax ID alone covers VAT, PAYE, pension or sector taxes

    Tax Identification Numbers in Africa — What You Need to Know

    A TIN (Tax Identification Number) is a unique identifier issued by the national revenue authority. It is required to open a business bank account, file tax returns, pay employees, and in many countries, to complete government procurement contracts.

    • Most African countries now offer online TIN registration through the tax authority's web portal
    • TIN is typically free of charge — be wary of agents charging excessive fees
    • Corporate TIN is different from individual/personal TIN — businesses need both in some countries
    • TIN is linked to VAT registration in countries where turnover thresholds are met
    • Some countries (Kenya PIN, Nigeria TIN) integrate TIN with broader e-government platforms
    • Mauritius uses a Business Registration Number (BRN) which doubles as the tax identifier
    • OHADA countries have a RCCM number (Registre du Commerce et du Crédit Mobilier) which is used alongside the fiscal identifier (NIF/IFU)

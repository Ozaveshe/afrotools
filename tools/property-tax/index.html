Property Tax Rates

Nigeria (Lagos): Land Use Charge = 0.0394% (owner-occupied) to 0.132% (commercial) of assessed value

Kenya: Land rates = County-set rate on unimproved site value. Nairobi ~25% of annual rental value

South Africa: Municipal rates ~0.5%-1.5% of market value. Rebates for pensioners.

Ghana: Property rate ~0.5%-1% of rateable value (assessed by Lands Commission)

Lagos LUC Relief

  • Owner-occupied: lowest rate (0.0394%)
  • Pensioner discount: 50% relief
  • Property under NGN 4M: exempt
  • Pay by Jan 15 for early payment discount

Understanding Property Tax in Africa

Property taxes in Africa vary significantly by country and municipality. In Nigeria, the Land Use Charge (LUC) in Lagos is the most well-known property tax, calculated as a percentage of the assessed property value. Kenya charges land rates based on the unimproved site value. South Africa has municipal rates based on market value, and Ghana charges a property rate set by district assemblies.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Lagos Land Use Charge calculated?

Lagos LUC = Assessed Value x Rate. The assessed value combines land value and building improvement value. Rates range from 0.0394% for owner-occupied residential to 0.132% for commercial properties.

Is property tax deductible from income tax?

In Nigeria, property tax is not directly deductible from personal income tax. However, for rental properties, it may be deductible as a business expense. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

African Regulatory Change Monitoring — All 54 Countries

AfroTools tracks minimum wage adjustments, PAYE band changes, pension contribution rate updates, social security amendments, new levies, and filing deadline changes across every African country. When governments gazette a change, our team verifies it and alerts subscribers within hours — not weeks.

Common changes we track: Nigeria's National Minimum Wage Act amendments, South Africa's National Minimum Wage annual reviews, Kenya's NSSF Act updates and Housing Levy changes, Ghana's National Daily Minimum Wage (set by the National Tripartite Committee), and pension rate adjustments from PENCOM, RSSB, NSSF, SSNIT, and GEPF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does AfroTools update its regulatory data?
The AfroTools team monitors government gazettes, official press releases, and finance act publications continuously. Verified changes are published within 24–48 hours of gazette publication. Crowdsourced reports from users can appear within hours, pending team verification.
What types of regulatory changes are tracked?
We track: minimum wage changes, PAYE / income tax band adjustments, pension contribution rate changes (both employee and employer), social security rate amendments, new levies and charges (like the Kenya Housing Levy), changes to tax thresholds, corporate income tax rates, and filing deadline changes.
What qualifies for AfroPoints when I report a change?
Any regulatory change to payroll, tax, or social security that has been officially announced or gazetted qualifies. You earn 150 AfroPoints for being the first to submit a verified change, +50 for including an official source URL (gazette or government website), and +25 for including the correct effective date. Rumours and unconfirmed changes do not qualify.
How are alert subscriptions delivered?
You can receive alerts via email or WhatsApp. Email alerts include full change details, old vs. new values, affected tools, and a link to the official source. WhatsApp alerts are concise summary messages with a link to the full details page.
Which payroll tools get updated when a regulation changes?
Each regulatory change record includes a list of affected tools. For example, a minimum wage change flags the Minimum Wage Checker, Payslip Generator, and Staff Cost Calculator. A pension rate change flags the country-specific pension calculator (e.g. Nigeria Pension, Kenya NSSF, Ghana SSNIT) and the Payslip Generator.

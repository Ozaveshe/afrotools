About This Tool

Tipping Guide for Africa

Tipping customs vary significantly across Africa. In South Africa, tipping 10-15% is standard at restaurants and is an important part of service workers' income. In Nigeria, 5-10% is typical, though many upscale restaurants add a service charge. In Kenya, 10% is the norm, and in Ghana, service charges are often included but an additional 5% is appreciated.

How to Use

  1. Enter your total bill amount
  2. Select your currency (NGN, KES, ZAR, GHS, etc.)
  3. Choose a tip percentage using presets or enter a custom amount
  4. Set the number of people splitting the bill
  5. Results update instantly — total, tip, and per-person amounts

According to international tipping norms, Africa generally follows the European model where tips supplement (rather than replace) wages, unlike the US where tips form a major part of server income.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I tip in Africa?
In Nigeria, 5-10% is typical. In South Africa, 10-15% is standard. In Kenya, 10% is common. In Ghana, service charges are often included but an extra 5% is appreciated.
Does the calculator support African currencies?
Yes. Select from Nigerian Naira, Kenyan Shilling, South African Rand, Ghanaian Cedi, and more. The currency symbol updates throughout the results.
Can I round up the tip to a nice number?
The calculator shows exact amounts. In practice, many people round up to the nearest convenient number. For example, if your tip comes to ₦487, you might round to ₦500.

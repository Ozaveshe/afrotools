Solar ROI FAQs

How do I calculate solar payback?

Enter your monthly grid bill, generator spend, outage hours, system size, battery choice, and financing assumptions. The calculator compares current energy spend with a solar scenario and estimates how long savings may take to recover the system cost.

Should I include generator fuel?

Yes, include generator fuel if you use a petrol or diesel generator for backup power. Generator savings can materially change payback, especially for shops, clinics, schools, churches, cold rooms, and SMEs with frequent outages.

Do I need a battery?

Not always. A battery is mainly for backup and night-time essential loads. Solar without a large battery can pay back faster, but it will not keep appliances running during outages.

What size solar system do I need?

Start from daily kWh, peak load, outage hours, and essential appliances. A small home may start around 1-2 kW, a family home around 3-5 kW, and businesses should do a load estimate before choosing a size.

Why is my payback different from installer quotes?

Quotes change with panel wattage, inverter brand, battery chemistry, usable battery capacity, roof work, protection devices, wiring distance, labour, warranty, maintenance, and finance charges.

What assumptions can I edit?

You can edit tariff, generator fuel price, solar yield, performance ratio, system size, install cost, battery cost, maintenance, deposit, interest, finance term, and outage or backup assumptions.

Is this a replacement for PVWatts or installer design?

No. Use this for early planning and quote comparison. PVWatts, PVGIS, Global Solar Atlas, and installer design tools can model location, roof, shading, inverter limits, and production more precisely.

How accurate is this calculator?

It is a planning estimate, not a quote. Accuracy improves when you use recent bills, real fuel receipts, a load profile, local solar yield, current equipment quotes, and finance terms from the provider.

Solar ROI methodology

The country pages are planning calculators that combine user-entered bills, generator spend, system size, battery choice, solar yield, performance ratio, maintenance, and financing assumptions. The root page routes you to the right local calculator and exports a quote-checklist brief.

Planning estimate

Dataset reviewed 2026-03-01. Tariffs, fuel prices, equipment quotes, finance fees, warranty terms, and installer availability can change quickly.

Data freshness

  • The bundled solar ROI country dataset carries per-country freshness labels for tariff, fuel, install-cost, battery, yield, performance-ratio, and emissions assumptions.
  • Root-page briefs are local planning checklists. Replace static assumptions with a current bill, fuel receipt, installer quote, and finance terms before comparing payback.

Before acting

  • Use recent grid bills, fuel receipts, outage hours, and written installer quotes before relying on a payback result.
  • Verify solar yield, shading, roof condition, battery chemistry, usable capacity, protection devices, and warranty terms with a qualified installer.
  • Confirm current tariffs, fuel prices, permitting, safety rules, and finance charges from providers or official sources.

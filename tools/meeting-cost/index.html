About This Tool

The Hidden Cost of Meetings

Meetings are the #1 time killer in the modern workplace. A Harvard Business Review study found that executives spend an average of 23 hours per week in meetings, up from less than 10 hours in the 1960s. When you factor in preparation and follow-up, meetings consume even more productivity.

How the Calculator Works

  1. Enter the number of meeting attendees
  2. Set the average annual salary for attendees
  3. Choose meeting duration and overhead multiplier
  4. Select how often this meeting recurs for annual impact

The formula: Cost = Attendees x (Annual Salary / Working Hours) x Overhead x (Duration / 60). According to Harvard Business Review, many organizations can recover 20-30% of meeting time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is meeting cost calculated?
Cost = (sum of hourly rates) x duration. Hourly rate = annual salary / working hours per year, multiplied by overhead.
Does it include overhead costs?
Yes, you can choose an overhead multiplier (1x-2x) to account for benefits, office space, and equipment.
How can I reduce meeting costs?
Reduce duration, invite fewer people, have clear agendas, use async communication, and eliminate recurring meetings that lost their purpose.

What is FIRE?

FIRE = Financial Independence, Retire Early. The goal is to save and invest enough that your investment returns cover your living expenses permanently.

The 4% Rule: You need 25x your annual expenses invested. Withdraw 4% per year and your money should last 30+ years.

Example: If you spend NGN 3.6M/year (300K/month), your FIRE number = NGN 90M.

FIRE Types

  • Lean FIRE: Minimal expenses, frugal lifestyle
  • Regular FIRE: Comfortable middle-class lifestyle
  • Fat FIRE: Luxurious lifestyle, higher target
  • Coast FIRE: Stop saving, let compound growth do the work

African FIRE Considerations

  • Higher inflation = use real returns
  • Extended family obligations
  • Healthcare costs (no universal cover)
  • Rental income as passive income stream
  • Agriculture / farmland investments

Financial Independence in Africa

The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement is gaining traction among young African professionals. While the concept originated in the West, it applies equally to Africans — with some unique considerations like higher inflation rates, extended family obligations, and different investment landscapes.

Your FIRE number is simply your annual expenses multiplied by 25 (based on the 4% safe withdrawal rate). Once your invested assets reach this number, you can live off investment returns without working. In Africa, consider using a more conservative 3-3.5% withdrawal rate due to higher inflation volatility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FIRE realistic in Africa with high inflation?

Yes, but you need to use real (inflation-adjusted) returns. If your investments return 12% and inflation is 7%, your real return is ~5%. This means it takes longer to reach FIRE, but it's still achievable with discipline and the right investment mix.

What should I invest in for FIRE in Nigeria?

A diversified portfolio: FGN bonds (stable returns), mutual funds, REITs, index-linked bonds (inflation protection), and potentially some USD-denominated assets to hedge against naira depreciation. Real estate rental income is also popular.

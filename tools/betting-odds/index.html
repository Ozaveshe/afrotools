Betting Odds — Explained for African Bettors

Decimal odds (common in Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana) show your total return per unit staked, including your original stake. Odds of 2.50 mean you receive ₦2.50 for every ₦1 staked. Profit = (odds − 1) × stake. Implied probability = 1 ÷ decimal odds × 100.
Fractional odds (e.g., 6/4 or 3/2) show profit relative to stake. 6/4 means ₦6 profit for every ₦4 staked. Decimal equivalent = (numerator ÷ denominator) + 1. So 6/4 = 1.5 + 1 = 2.50 decimal.
Positive American odds (+150) show profit on a $100 stake. Negative American odds (-200) show the stake needed to win $100. To convert: +150 → decimal = (150/100) + 1 = 2.50. −200 → decimal = (100/200) + 1 = 1.50.
A "good bet" is when your estimated probability of the outcome is higher than the implied probability from the odds. E.g., if odds imply 40% chance but you believe the team has 55% chance — that's a positive expected value bet. The house edge (bookmaker margin) is typically 5–10% on football markets.

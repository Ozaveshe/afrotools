How the MVP estimate works

The calculator starts from country and student-level tutoring benchmarks, then adjusts for subject demand, tutor experience, and delivery mode. Use the market range as a quote band, not a fixed rule: exam urgency, transport, online platform fees, group size, and parent budgets can move the final price.

How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how the result is produced, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before you rely on it.

Methodology & assumptions
We calculate your hourly rate with the formula rate = base × experience multiplier × mode multiplier. The base comes from a country / student-level / subject benchmark table (for example Nigeria secondary sciences = ₦4,000/hr). Experience multipliers are 0.8 (student tutor), 1.0 (graduate), 1.3 (qualified teacher) and 1.6 (subject specialist); mode multipliers are 1.0 in-person, 0.85 online and 0.5 per student for group sessions. The market range shown is 0.8× to 1.3× that rate, weekly income is rate × hours, monthly is weekly × 4 weeks, and package prices apply a 10% discount (5 sessions) or 15% discount (10 sessions). Assumptions: the benchmarks are editorial market estimates and a flat four-week month.
Limitations & what to verify
This is a pricing estimate only, not a regulated wage, official tariff, employment contract, or guaranteed market rate. Real rates depend on local parent budgets, transport, online platform fees, lesson-prep time, subject demand, exam urgency, group size and cancellation rules. Treat the market range as a quote band and verify it against actual local quotes and nearby tutors before you commit to a price.
Sources & references
Base rates are AfroTools editorial estimates compiled as a market reference from advertised private-tutoring rates across Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa and Ghana. There is no single official source for private tutoring fees, so cross-check current local rates on tutoring marketplaces and with tutors in your area. Last verified 2026.

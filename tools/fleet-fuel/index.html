Frequently Asked Questions

How can I reduce fleet fuel costs in Africa?
Key strategies: (1) Route optimization — avoid unnecessary detours and idling in traffic. (2) Driver training — smooth acceleration and braking improves fuel efficiency by 10-15%. (3) Vehicle maintenance — properly inflated tyres and clean air filters reduce consumption by 5-10%. (4) Fuel management system — track per-vehicle consumption to catch theft or inefficient drivers. (5) Off-peak scheduling — avoid peak traffic hours especially in Lagos, Nairobi, and Accra.
What is the average fuel consumption for trucks in Africa?
Heavy trucks in Africa typically consume 28-35 litres per 100km, compared to 25-30 L/100km in developed markets. The higher consumption is due to road conditions, frequent stops at checkpoints, older fleet, and overloading. Poor road surfaces alone can increase fuel consumption by 15-25% compared to paved highways.
How much of logistics cost is fuel in Africa?
Fuel typically represents 35-45% of total fleet operating costs in Africa, compared to 25-30% globally. This is due to lower fuel efficiency from poor roads, higher fuel prices relative to income, and less efficient vehicles. Managing fuel spend is therefore the single highest-impact lever for logistics cost reduction.

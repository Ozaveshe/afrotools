      Frequently Asked Questions

      What happens if I miss the birth registration deadline?

      Late registration is possible in all four countries but typically requires additional documentation such as a statutory declaration, witness statements, or a court order. Fees may also be higher. Contact your country's civil registration office as early as possible.

      Is a birth certificate required for school enrollment?

      Yes — in Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, and Ghana, a birth certificate or birth registration notification is required for primary school enrollment and is essential for accessing government services throughout life.

      Can I register a birth that occurred at home (not in a hospital)?

      Yes, but the process varies. You typically need witness statements from community members or a traditional birth attendant. In some countries, a local government official may need to certify the birth occurred.

      Do I need a death certificate for insurance claims?

      Yes. A certified death certificate is required for life insurance claims, pension survivor benefits, bank account access, estate administration, and tax clearance in all covered countries.

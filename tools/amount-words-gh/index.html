Ghana Cedi Facts

  • Currency code: GHS
  • Symbol: GH₵
  • Subunit: 100 pesewas (Gp)
  • Central bank: Bank of Ghana (BoG)
  • Coins: 1, 5, 10, 20, 50 Gp; GH₵1, GH₵2
  • Notes: GH₵1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200

History

The Ghana Cedi was redenominated in 2007 — the "new cedi" (GHS) replaced the old cedi at a rate of 10,000 old cedis = 1 new cedi. "Cedi" comes from the Akan word for cowrie shell, historically used as currency.

Writing Ghana Cedi Amounts in Words

Converting Ghana Cedi amounts into words is a daily requirement for businesses, accountants, lawyers, and individuals in Ghana. Whether you're writing a cheque at Ecobank, preparing a contract for a property transaction, or drafting an invoice for your business, the amount must be clearly expressed in both figures and words to prevent fraud and disputes.

The correct Ghanaian format starts with "Ghana Cedis" followed by the amount in words, with pesewas (the subunit) included if applicable. For example, GH₵ 12,500.75 would be written as "Ghana Cedis Twelve Thousand Five Hundred and Pesewas Seventy-Five Only." The word "Only" is always placed at the end to prevent any additions to the amount.

The Ghana Cedi (GHS) has been the country's currency since independence, though it was redenominated in 2007. The name "cedi" derives from the Akan word "sedi," meaning cowrie shell — the traditional medium of exchange in West Africa before colonial currencies. The pesewa, the cedi's subunit (100 pesewas = 1 cedi), is named after a small gold dust weight used in Ashanti trade.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a Ghana Cedi cheque correctly?↓
Write the date in DD/MM/YYYY format, fill in the payee name, write the amount in figures in the box, then write the amount in words starting with "Ghana Cedis" and ending with "Only." Draw a line through any remaining space on the amount line.
What are pesewas and how do I include them?↓
Pesewas (Gp) are the subunit of the Ghana Cedi. 100 pesewas equal 1 cedi. When writing amounts in words, include pesewas after "and Pesewas" -- for example, GH₵ 50.30 becomes "Ghana Cedis Fifty and Pesewas Thirty Only."
Is the current Ghana Cedi the same as the old cedi?↓
No. Ghana redenominated its currency on July 3, 2007. The new Ghana Cedi (GHS) replaced the old cedi (GHC) at a rate of 10,000 old cedis to 1 new cedi. All current transactions use the new cedi.
Do Ghanaian banks still accept cheques?↓
Yes, cheques are still widely used in Ghana for business transactions, rent payments, and government dealings. However, mobile money (MTN MoMo, Vodafone Cash, AirtelTigo Money) has become dominant for everyday transactions. For large amounts, cheques and bank transfers remain standard.

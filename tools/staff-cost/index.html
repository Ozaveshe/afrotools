Why Total Cost Matters

Understanding the true cost helps with budgeting, pricing your services, and making informed hiring decisions.

Understanding Employee Costs in Africa

Hiring an employee in Africa involves much more than just the agreed salary. Every country has mandatory statutory contributions that employers must pay, from pension schemes to social insurance funds. These costs can add 20-60% on top of the gross salary, depending on the country and benefits package.

Country-Specific Payroll Requirements

Nigeria (2026 NTA): The Nigeria Tax Act 2025 took effect January 2026. PAYE now starts at 0% for the first NGN 800,000 annually, with bands up to 25%. CRA has been abolished and replaced with Rent Relief. Employer pension remains at 10%, NSITF at 1%, and ITF at 1%.

Kenya: NSSF entered Year 4 in February 2026 — the ceiling rose to KES 108,000 with maximum contributions of KES 6,480 per side. SHIF replaced NHIF at 2.75% of gross. The Affordable Housing Levy is 1.5% each for employer and employee. PAYE has 5 bands from 10% to 35%.

South Africa: For the 2025/2026 year, PAYE brackets remain 18%-45% with a primary rebate of R17,235. UIF is 1% each (capped at R177.12/month), SDL is 1% employer-only. The UIF ceiling is R17,712/month.

Ghana: Employer SSNIT is 13%, employee 5.5%, plus a 5% Tier 2 pension. PAYE ranges from 0% (first GHS 490/month) to 35%. The SSNIT cap is GHS 61,000.

Hidden Costs of Hiring

  • Recruitment costs: Job adverts, recruiter fees (typically 1-3 months' salary), interview time
  • Equipment: Laptop, desk, software licenses, phone
  • Training: Onboarding, courses, conferences — budget 2-5% of annual salary
  • Office overhead: Space, electricity, internet per employee
  • Leave costs: Public holidays (10-15 per year), annual leave (15-21 days), sick leave

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the overhead multiplier?
The overhead multiplier shows how much the total cost exceeds the gross salary. A multiplier of 1.35x means the employee costs 35% more than their salary. Across Africa, typical multipliers range from 1.15x (low-cost countries) to 1.60x (comprehensive packages in high-cost countries).
Are the 2026 Nigeria Tax Act rates included?
Yes. This calculator uses the new NTA 2025 rates effective January 2026: 0% on the first NGN 800,000 annually, progressive bands up to 25%, abolished CRA replaced by Rent Relief, and removed minimum tax.
Does this include Kenya's new NSSF Year 4 rates?
Yes. From February 2026, NSSF uses the Year 4 ceilings: Tier I up to KES 9,000 and Tier II up to KES 108,000, with maximum contributions of KES 6,480 per side.
How accurate are the PAYE estimates?
The PAYE calculations use official tax brackets and standard deductions. However, individual circumstances (additional reliefs, multiple employers, etc.) may differ. Always consult a tax professional for exact figures.
Is 13th month salary mandatory?
It varies by country. In Nigeria it's widespread practice but not strictly mandated. In some countries like Angola it is legally required. This calculator lets you toggle it on or off.

FAQ
What is the difference between gross margin and contribution margin?

Gross margin subtracts COGS from realised revenue. Contribution margin subtracts COGS plus every other variable cost. Contribution margin is therefore the amount each unit contributes toward fixed costs and profit.

What LTV/CAC ratio is healthy?

There is no universal healthy ratio. Compare contribution LTV with CAC, cash payback timing, retention evidence and your own capital constraints. This tool calculates a ratio only when you enter both CAC and expected paid units per customer.

What gross margin should I target in Nigeria?

Use your own historical margins and current supplier, labour, logistics, payment, tax and returns evidence. Sector averages can hide major differences in product mix, geography, channel and business stage.

Unit Economics Guide

Key Formulas

Gross Margin = (Price - COGS) / Price
Contribution Margin = Price - All Variable Costs
Break-Even = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin
Contribution LTV = Contribution per Unit × Expected Paid Units per Customer
LTV/CAC = LTV / Customer Acquisition Cost

Input Evidence

  • Use realised selling price after discounts and refunds
  • Separate COGS from packaging, payment and delivery costs
  • Use actual acquisition spend and acquired-customer count
  • Base lifetime orders on a dated retention cohort
  • Re-run when supplier, FX, logistics or fee inputs change

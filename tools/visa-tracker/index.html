Frequently Asked Questions

Processing times are based on published government data and community reports as of 2026. Individual cases vary based on complexity, peak season, and whether additional documents are requested. Always check the official immigration portal for the most current times.
The UK Home Office counts business days from when your biometrics are submitted at a Visa Application Centre (VAC), not from when you paid online. The 15 business day standard service excludes weekends and UK public holidays.
UK: Priority service (5 business days) and Super Priority (next working day) are available for most visa types at an extra cost. USA: Emergency appointments are available for genuine emergencies. Canada: No paid expedite; Express Entry applications are processed faster than paper applications. Australia: Priority processing is available in some streams.
UK: You can contact the Home Office after the published timeframe. USA: Check CEAC status; file a service request with USCIS after 60 days past the published timeframe. Canada: Use the IRCC online portal to check status and submit a webform if delayed. Australia: Use ImmiAccount to check status and contact the Department if significantly delayed.

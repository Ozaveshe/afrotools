Frequently Asked Questions

Is M-Pesa cheaper than bank transfer in Kenya?
For small amounts (KES 100-1,000), M-Pesa send-to-mobile is typically cheaper. For KES 1,000-10,000, M-Pesa and Pesalink (bank-to-bank instant) are comparable. For amounts above KES 10,000, Pesalink or EFT is usually cheaper per shilling. M-Pesa also charges withdrawal fees on top of send fees, making total cost higher than just the send fee.
Is mobile money or bank faster?
Mobile money (M-Pesa, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money) transfers are instant — typically under 30 seconds. Instant bank transfers (NIP in Nigeria, Pesalink in Kenya, RTC in SA) are also instant (under 1 minute). Traditional EFT/NEFT takes 1-3 business days. RTGS is same-day but has high minimum amounts and fees. For speed, both mobile money and instant bank transfers are equivalent.
What is the cheapest way to send money in Nigeria?
For most Nigerians, bank-to-bank NIP (NIBSS Instant Payment) transfers are cheapest for amounts above NGN 5,000 — most banks charge NGN 10-50 flat fee. For small amounts (under NGN 2,000) to unbanked recipients, OPay, Palmpay, or Moniepoint wallets are often free or very cheap. GTBank's 737 USSD charges NGN 10/transfer. Avoid over-the-counter cash deposits/withdrawals which attract higher fees.

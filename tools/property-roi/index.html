❓ Common Questions

What's a good property ROI in Africa? +
Total returns (capital gain + rental) of 12–20% per year are achievable in high-growth African markets. In more mature markets like Cape Town, 8–12% is solid. Compare against fixed deposits (8–20% in most African countries) and stock market returns (varies widely).
Does leverage improve ROI? +
Yes — significantly. If you put 20% down on a property that appreciates 10%, your actual return on cash invested is ~50% (10% gain on 100% of the asset value, but only 20% was your money). This is why property can outperform cash investments in rising markets.
Is African property a good investment? +
Africa has some of the fastest-growing property markets globally, driven by rapid urbanisation, a young population, and housing shortages. Key risks include currency depreciation, illiquidity, and tenant risk. Nairobi, Lagos, Accra and Cairo have delivered strong returns over the past decade.
What about capital gains tax? +
South Africa: 18% effective CGT for individuals on profit above R2M exclusion. Nigeria: 10% CGT on gains from property sales. Kenya: 15% CGT effective 2023. Morocco: 20% CGT, reduces over time (5%+ years). Ghana: 15% withholding on gains. Always consult a local tax advisor.

Property ROI Calculator: How It Works

The AfroTools Property ROI Calculator measures the total return on investment for property purchases across African markets. Enter your purchase price, holding period, expected appreciation rate, rental income, and selling costs to get a comprehensive ROI analysis. The calculator factors in capital gains from property appreciation, net rental income over the holding period, agent commissions on sale, and capital gains tax specific to your country. It produces key metrics including total ROI percentage, annualised return, and a breakdown of returns from capital appreciation versus rental income. Country-specific data is built in for Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, Egypt, Morocco, and more — with historical appreciation benchmarks, typical agent fees, and CGT rates for each market. This allows you to make realistic investment projections grounded in actual market conditions rather than generic assumptions. An integrated AI advisor provides expert analysis on property investment strategies, market timing, and how to maximise returns in African real estate. Whether you are evaluating a buy-to-let investment, comparing properties across cities, or building a property portfolio, this calculator gives you the analytical foundation for smarter decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which African countries are supported?

The calculator includes country-specific data for Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, Egypt, Morocco, and additional African markets. Each country has pre-loaded historical appreciation rates, agent commission ranges, and capital gains tax rates.

What is included in the ROI calculation?

The calculation includes capital appreciation over the holding period, cumulative net rental income, selling costs (agent commissions), and capital gains tax. The result is a total ROI and annualised return figure.

How is the annualised return calculated?

The annualised return converts your total ROI into an equivalent annual percentage, accounting for the compounding effect over your holding period. This makes it easy to compare property investment returns with other investment options.

Are the appreciation rates realistic for African markets?

The default appreciation rates are based on historical market data for each country. For example, Lagos and Abuja properties have historically appreciated 8-15% annually. You can adjust these rates to match your specific location and market outlook.

Can the AI advisor help with property investment strategy?

Yes. The built-in AI advisor provides insights on capital appreciation trends, rental yield optimisation, leverage strategies, and tax implications specific to African property markets.

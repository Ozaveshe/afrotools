Understanding Mobile Money Fees in Africa

Mobile money is part of everyday life for many people across Africa. It helps traders get paid, families send support, workers cash out, and customers pay bills. The cost is not always obvious. Fees change by country, provider, transaction type, and amount.

The AfroTools Mobile Money Fee Checker compares common fee bands from major providers in more than 20 African countries. Use it to estimate the fee before you send, withdraw, pay a merchant, or make an international transfer. Treat the result as a guide and confirm large transfers in your provider app or USSD menu.

How the Fee Checker Works

Select your country, enter the amount, and choose the transaction type. Supported Mobile Money Providers

The tool covers the six most widely used mobile money platforms on the continent:

  • M-Pesa — Operated by Safaricom and Vodacom, M-Pesa is the pioneer of mobile money and dominates in Kenya, Tanzania, Ethiopia, Egypt, Ghana, and Rwanda. With over 50 million active users, it offers one of the widest agent networks in East Africa.
  • MTN MoMo (Mobile Money) — MTN MoMo is the largest mobile money service in West and Central Africa, serving users in Nigeria, Ghana, Uganda, Rwanda, Cameroon, Senegal, and several other markets. It benefits from MTN's massive subscriber base.
  • Airtel Money — Airtel Money operates across East, West, and Southern Africa, including Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Ghana, Zambia, Malawi, Nigeria, and Cote d'Ivoire. It is often the most competitively priced alternative to M-Pesa and MTN MoMo.
  • Orange Money — Popular in Francophone West and Central Africa, Orange Money has a strong presence in Senegal, Cote d'Ivoire, Cameroon, Burkina Faso, Mali, and Guinea. It benefits from Orange's extensive telecom infrastructure.
  • Wave — A fintech disruptor in West Africa, Wave offers a flat one-percent fee model that has made it hugely popular in Senegal, Cote d'Ivoire, Burkina Faso, Mali, and Guinea. Its simple pricing is easy to understand and often the cheapest option.
  • OPay — Focused on Nigeria, OPay has grown rapidly since 2021 with competitive send and withdrawal fees, a large agent network, and additional services like ride-hailing and food delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are mobile money fees and why do they vary?

Mobile money fees are charges applied by providers each time you perform a transaction such as sending money, withdrawing cash, or paying a bill. Fees vary because each provider sets its own tiered fee schedule based on the transaction amount, and national regulators in each country impose different rules. For example, Kenya's M-Pesa uses flat-fee bands where sending under KSh 100 is free, while Ghana applies a 1.5% government E-levy on electronic transfers above GH₵100. Transaction type also matters: merchant payments are often free, while cash withdrawals tend to carry the highest fees because they involve agent commissions.

How accurate are the fee estimates shown by this tool?

The estimates use publicly available fee schedules and regulatory filings as of early 2026. Providers can revise tariffs, so confirm the exact fee in your provider's USSD menu or official app before making a large transfer. This is a guide, not a binding quote.

Can I use this tool for international mobile money transfers?

Yes. Select "International Transfer" as the transaction type to see cross-border fees. Keep in mind that international transfers often involve additional foreign-exchange spreads and receiving-side charges that are not captured in the basic fee band. For large remittances, consider comparing mobile money costs against dedicated remittance services like Chipper Cash, Sendwave, or WorldRemit for the best overall rate.

Which mobile money provider is the cheapest in Africa?

There is no single cheapest provider across the entire continent because fees depend on the country, the amount, and the transaction type. In West Africa, Wave's flat one-percent model is often the lowest cost for person-to-person transfers. In East Africa, Airtel Money frequently undercuts M-Pesa on smaller amounts. In Nigeria, OPay tends to have slightly lower send fees than MTN MoMo. The best approach is to use this tool to compare providers for your specific country and transfer amount.

How this local estimate works

This MVP does not upload your text or scan external databases. It checks your draft in the browser, then reports local overlap and writing-pattern signals you can revise before using an official school checker.

  • Overlap score: compares meaningful words and repeated 4-word phrases between your draft and the optional source text.
  • Generic phrase score: flags common template phrases such as broad essay openers and filler transitions.
  • Revision guidance: suggests where to paraphrase, cite, quote, or add your own analysis.
  • Privacy: analysis stays in the browser unless you choose to save the report locally.

How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how the percentage is produced, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before you rely on it.

Methodology & assumptions
The percentage is computed entirely in your browser from two local signals. If you add a source text, we measure overlap: the share of your draft’s unique meaningful words (with stop-words removed) that also appear in the source, plus matched 4-word phrases, combined with the formula overlap score = (word-overlap % × 0.45) + (10 per matched phrase, capped at 60). We also compute a generic-phrase score from a built-in list of around 50 filler / template phrases (5 points each, with extra points for openers like “this essay” or “in conclusion”). The headline figure is the generic score alone in draft-only mode, or a weighted 70% overlap + 30% generic when a source text is provided. Assumption: it only compares against text you paste — it does not search the web or any database.
Limitations & what to verify
This is an indicative self-editing estimate only, not a plagiarism-database scan or an academic-integrity ruling. It does not check against the internet, journals, student archives or Turnitin-style databases, so a low score is not proof of originality and a high score is not proof of misconduct. It does not replace your school’s process: for any submission decision, verify with your institution’s official licensed plagiarism checker.
Sources & references
The generic-phrase list and the 4-word phrase-matching (shingling) method are AfroTools’ own local heuristics run in your browser; no external database or third-party source is queried.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a JAMB aggregate score?

The JAMB aggregate score combines your UTME score, Post-UTME score, and sometimes O'Level grades into a single number used by Nigerian universities for admission screening.

Why do different universities use different formulas?

Each university has autonomy in deciding how to weigh different components for admission. Some prioritize UTME scores while others give more weight to Post-UTME or O'Level performance.

What O'Level grades are used in the UI formula?

The University of Ibadan considers your 5 best relevant O'Level subject grades. The grading is: A1=6 points, B2=5, B3=4, C4=3, C5=2, C6=1, and D7/E8/F9=0 points. The maximum O'Level score is 30 points.

What is a good aggregate score?

This depends on the university and course. Competitive courses like Medicine and Law typically require aggregates above 70, while others may admit from 50-60. Always check the specific cutoff for your chosen university and course.

How accurate are the cutoff marks shown?

Cutoff marks are based on recent admission cycles and serve as estimates. Actual cutoffs may vary each year depending on the number of applicants and available spaces. Always verify with the university directly.

Understanding Bank Charges in Africa

Bank charges are a significant expense for individuals and businesses across Africa. Unlike many developed markets where basic banking is often free, African banks typically charge for account maintenance, transfers, ATM withdrawals, SMS notifications, and various other services. These charges can add up to thousands per month, making it important to compare banks before opening an account.

In Nigeria, the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) has issued guidelines capping certain charges, but banks still charge for inter-bank transfers (via NIP/NIBSS), card maintenance, and SMS alerts. Digital banks like Opay, Kuda, and Moniepoint have disrupted the market with zero or low fees on transfers and no maintenance charges, putting pressure on traditional banks to reduce fees.

Kenya's banking sector has been transformed by mobile money (M-Pesa), which offers cheaper transfers than traditional bank transfers. However, bank accounts are still necessary for salaries, loans, and international transactions. The CBK requires banks to publish their tariff guides, making comparison easier.

South Africa has one of the most transparent banking fee structures in Africa, with all major banks publishing detailed tariff guides. Digital banks like TymeBank and Bank Zero offer zero-fee accounts, while traditional banks charge between R100-R300/month for bundled accounts. The Competition Commission has pushed for greater fee transparency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which bank has the lowest charges?

Digital/fintech banks typically offer the lowest charges. In Nigeria: Kuda, Opay, Moniepoint. In Kenya: Equity (Eazzy Banking). In South Africa: TymeBank, Bank Zero. In Ghana: Fidelity Bank tends to be competitive. However, traditional banks offer wider branch networks and more services.

Can I avoid bank charges entirely?

Some digital banks offer zero-maintenance-fee accounts with free transfers up to a limit. However, you may still incur charges for ATM withdrawals, international transactions, and certain services. Reading the full tariff guide before signing up is essential.

Backup power planning questions

How are the three monthly costs calculated?

Generator cost is runtime × measured fuel use × local fuel price, plus monthly maintenance. Battery cost is installed cost ÷ chosen life, plus grid recharge energy adjusted for efficiency and maintenance. Solar cost is installed cost ÷ chosen life, plus maintenance.

Why does solar not use the backup energy in its cost formula?

The solar line is a simple monthly equivalent for a supplier quote that has already been sized for your need. This calculator does not model irradiance, panel yield, battery depth of discharge or component replacement schedules. Reject any quote that has not been sized locally.

Does this include financing, inflation or lost business?

No. Add financing costs to the quoted scenario before comparing, and assess outage losses separately. The comparison uses nominal values you enter and does not forecast prices.

Can I save a PDF privately?

Yes. Calculate, choose “Print / save PDF,” and use your browser's PDF destination. Nothing in the calculator form is sent to AfroTools or AI.

Before relying on the comparison

  • Measure continuous and startup loads; motors and compressors can surge.
  • Confirm usable battery capacity, inverter rating and safe depth of discharge.
  • Ask for itemised written quotes, warranties and replacement assumptions.
  • Use current fuel and electricity evidence from your location.
  • Have wiring, earthing, ventilation and generator exhaust checked professionally.

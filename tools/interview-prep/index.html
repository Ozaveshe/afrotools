Method and checks

This MVP builds a private interview prep kit from country, company type, role level, and interview format. It combines country dress expectations, company-specific research prompts, role-level questions, STAR rehearsal tips, and a follow-up email template.

  • Checklist sections are grouped by timing: three days before, day before, day of interview, and after the interview.
  • Question prompts combine role-level behavioural questions with company-type questions the candidate can ask the employer.
  • This is career preparation support, not hiring advice, legal advice, immigration advice, salary guarantee, or employer endorsement.

How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how your prep kit is assembled, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before your interview.

Methodology & assumptions
This tool assembles rather than scores: from your four inputs — country, company type, role level, and interview format — it maps to curated content blocks and builds a 30-item checklist grouped by timing (three days before, day before, day of, and after the interview), country-specific dress guidance, company-type research prompts, role-level behavioural questions plus smart questions to ask the employer, STAR-method prompts (Situation → Task → Action → Result), and a follow-up email template. No probability or ranking is calculated. Our assumptions: the guidance reflects general professional and business-culture norms for each country and sector and may not match a specific employer's actual process.
Limitations & what to verify
This is informational career-preparation support only — not hiring advice, legal advice, immigration advice, a salary guarantee, or an employer endorsement, and it does not replace the employer's own instructions. Dress codes, salary-discussion norms, and interview formats vary by employer and change over time. Verify the actual interview format, timing, location or video link, interviewer names, and any employer instructions directly with the employer or recruiter before you attend.
Sources & references
The dress-code, salary-timing, and company-type guidance is compiled from general professional-recruitment practice and country business-culture norms; the STAR framework is a widely documented behavioural-interview method (see the UK National Careers Service interview advice ↗). Always confirm the specifics with the employer's HR or recruitment team. Last verified 2026.

🤝 NEGOTIATION TIPS
  • Commission is always negotiable — most agents start high
  • For properties above a threshold, ask for 1–2% reduction
  • Offer a sole mandate (exclusivity) in exchange for a lower rate
  • Negotiate the renewal fee down to half the letting fee
  • Get the commission rate in writing in the mandate agreement
  • Ask if VAT is included in their quoted rate — always clarify

Commission and mandate check

Agent fees should be tied to mandate, service, timing, tax and who pays. Buyers, sellers, landlords and tenants need to know when commission is earned and whether double billing is possible.

Decisions this clarifies

  • Whether the agent represents seller, buyer, landlord, tenant or both sides
  • Which fee is commission, viewing fee, legal fee, agreement fee or renewal fee
  • When commission becomes due and what happens if the deal collapses

Before you rely on it

  • Get the mandate or authority letter before paying
  • Write who pays commission, when it is earned and whether VAT applies
  • Compare fee against local custom, property value and actual service delivered

Red flags

  • Agent collects from both sides without disclosure
  • Viewing or inspection fee demanded with no authority evidence
  • Commission due even if title, finance or landlord approval fails

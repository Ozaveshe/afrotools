Frequently Asked Questions

What UUID versions are supported?
AfroTools supports UUID v1 (timestamp-based), UUID v4 (random), and UUID v7 (time-sortable, RFC 9562). It also generates ULIDs (Crockford Base32, sortable) and Nano IDs (URL-friendly, configurable).
Are the generated IDs truly random?
UUID v4, v7, ULID, and Nano ID all use crypto.getRandomValues() for cryptographically secure randomness, suitable for production use.
Can I generate IDs in bulk?
Yes. Generate up to 100 IDs at once with individual copy buttons, Copy All, and download options for .txt and .csv.
Is it free to use?
Yes, completely free with no limits, no sign-up, and no ads. Includes a UUID validator, anatomy viewer, and collision probability calculator.
What is the difference between UUID v4 and v7?
UUID v4 is fully random (122 bits), while v7 (RFC 9562) embeds a millisecond timestamp in the first 48 bits for time-sortability. V7 is ideal for database primary keys.
What is a ULID?
A ULID is a 26-character Crockford Base32 identifier combining a 48-bit timestamp with 80 random bits. ULIDs are lexicographically sortable and more compact than UUIDs.
What is a Nano ID?
Nano ID is a URL-friendly unique ID with configurable alphabet and length. Default: 21 chars using A-Za-z0-9_- (~126 bits of entropy).
How likely are UUID collisions?
For UUID v4 with 122 random bits, collision probability is astronomically low. Even 1 billion UUIDs/second for 100 years yields ~1 in 10^18 chance. Use the collision calculator above for exact numbers.

❓ Common Questions

What is a Debt Service Ratio? +
DSR (Debt Service Ratio) is the percentage of your monthly income that goes toward debt repayments. Banks use this to decide if you can afford the loan. In South Africa, the limit is ~30% of net income. In Kenya and Ghana, banks use ~40% of gross income.
Can I use rental income in my application? +
Yes, most African banks will accept 70–80% of rental income as qualifying income. You'll need lease agreements and bank statements showing deposits. Some banks require 2 years of rental history.
How does a joint application help? +
A joint application (spouse or co-buyer) combines both incomes, which can significantly increase the loan amount you qualify for. Both applicants' credit histories are assessed, so both need good credit.
What if I'm self-employed? +
Self-employed borrowers typically need 2–3 years of audited financial statements, 6–12 months of bank statements, and a letter from an accountant. Banks often apply a 10–20% "haircut" on declared income for self-employed applicants.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the calculator determine my maximum loan?

It applies the debt-service ratio rules used by banks in your selected country. This means it calculates the maximum monthly repayment your income can support after accounting for existing debts, then works backward to determine the loan amount.

Which African countries are supported?

The calculator supports Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, and additional African markets. Each country has specific interest rates, affordability rules, and debt-service limits based on actual banking guidelines.

Does existing debt affect how much I can borrow?

Yes. Banks deduct your existing monthly debt obligations (car loans, personal loans, credit cards) from your available income before calculating how much mortgage repayment you can afford. Reducing existing debt increases your borrowing power.

What is a debt-service ratio?

The debt-service ratio (DSR) is the percentage of your income that goes toward debt repayments. African banks typically cap this at 30-40% of your income, meaning your total monthly debt payments cannot exceed that threshold.

Can the AI advisor help me improve my borrowing power?

Yes. The built-in AI advisor can suggest strategies to increase your mortgage affordability, such as paying down existing debt, extending the loan term, considering joint applications, or improving your credit profile.

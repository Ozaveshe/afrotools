Allocation method
The household bill is calculated with the selected country's residential tariff, then distributed across appliance rows in proportion to modeled kWh. Appliance-row rounding is reconciled so the copied costs equal the displayed household total.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are the per-appliance monthly costs calculated?

The calculator allocates its rounded household bill across appliances in proportion to each appliance's modeled monthly kWh. This keeps the appliance rows aligned with the displayed total bill.

Does the wattage field show actual appliance consumption?

Not necessarily. Nameplate wattage is a planning input; compressors, thermostats, voltage, age, and duty cycles change real consumption. Use a suitable plug-in meter or submeter where practical.

How does the calculator treat standby power?

Appliance rows apply standby watts during the hours remaining after active use. The separate standby-cost metric assumes 24 standby hours per day as a conservative upper bound; measure standby draw where practical.

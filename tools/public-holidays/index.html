Public Holidays in Africa

African countries observe a mix of national, religious, and cultural holidays. Nigeria has approximately 13 public holidays, while South Africa has 12 national holidays. Kenya observes 12 gazetted public holidays, and Ghana has 14. Islamic holidays (Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Maulid) shift by about 10-11 days each year due to the lunar calendar.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many public holidays does Nigeria have?

Nigeria has approximately 13 official public holidays, including New Year, Workers' Day, Democracy Day (June 12), Independence Day (October 1), Christmas, and Islamic holidays which vary by lunar calendar.

What happens when a holiday falls on a weekend?

Policies vary. In South Africa, if a public holiday falls on a Sunday, the Monday is observed. In Nigeria, the Federal Government may declare the Monday as a holiday. Kenya follows a similar Monday-substitution rule.

Why are Islamic holiday dates estimated?

Islamic holidays follow the lunar calendar, so exact dates depend on moon sighting. Dates shown are estimates based on astronomical calculations and may shift by 1-2 days.

Public Holidays in Africa 2025

African countries observe a mix of national, religious, and cultural holidays. Nigeria has approximately 12 public holidays, while South Africa has 12 national holidays. Kenya observes 10 gazetted public holidays, and Ghana has 13. Islamic holidays (Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Maulid) and Christian holidays (Christmas, Easter) are observed across the continent.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many public holidays does Nigeria have?

Nigeria has approximately 12 official public holidays in 2025, including New Year's Day, Workers' Day, Democracy Day (June 12), Independence Day (October 1), Christmas, and Islamic holidays which vary by lunar calendar.

What happens when a holiday falls on a weekend?

Policies vary by country. In South Africa, if a public holiday falls on a Sunday, the Monday is observed as a holiday. In Nigeria, the Federal Government may declare the Monday as a holiday, but it's not automatic. Kenya follows a similar Monday-substitution rule.

Non-Disclosure Agreements in Africa — What You Need to Know

An NDA (also called a Confidentiality Agreement) is one of the most fundamental legal documents in business. It is typically the first agreement signed when exploring a business relationship where sensitive information will be shared.

  • Mutual NDA: Both parties share confidential information and both are bound to keep it secret. Best for joint development, partnerships, or M&A discussions.
  • One-Way NDA: Only one party (the disclosing party) shares confidential information. The other party (the receiving party) is bound not to disclose. Best for employer-employee, client-contractor, or investor pitch scenarios.
  • Duration: NDAs typically run 2–3 years. Indefinite NDAs are enforceable in most African jurisdictions but may face challenges in some employment contexts.
  • Trade secrets: Some categories of information (trade secrets, client lists) can be protected indefinitely even after NDA expiry under common law or statutory trade secret provisions.
  • Governing law matters: Choose the governing law of the country where enforcement is most likely to occur. Mauritius and South Africa have well-developed contract law systems.

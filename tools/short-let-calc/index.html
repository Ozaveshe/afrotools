Short-let return and licensing check

Short-let income needs occupancy, platform fees, cleaning, furnishing, utilities, management, tax, damage, seasonality and local permission checks before it beats long-let rent.

Decisions this clarifies

  • Whether short-let use is allowed by lease, estate rules, municipality or building body
  • Which nightly rate and occupancy are realistic by season
  • How cleaning, utilities, furnishing, platform fees and management affect net return

Before you rely on it

  • Compare short-let, long-let and vacant-month scenarios
  • Check building, estate and local rules before listing
  • Budget furnishing replacement, cleaning supervision, utilities and platform commissions

Red flags

  • Return model assumes high occupancy all year
  • No permission from landlord, body corporate or estate manager
  • Tax and platform fees ignored because income is paid digitally

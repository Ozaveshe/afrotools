Frequently asked questions

How much is electricity per kWh in Sudan?

As of the AfroTools 2026-03 dataset, residential customers pay roughly 50 SDG/kWh, commercial rates are around 80 SDG/kWh, industrial rates are around 65 SDG/kWh. Highly subsidized. Exchange rate volatility affects real cost. Always verify against your latest bill or the utility's published tariff.

Who sets electricity tariffs in Sudan?

Electricity prices in Sudan are regulated by SERC (Sudan Electricity Regulatory Commission). Tariff reviews can change rates during the year, so treat calculator results as planning estimates.

How many hours of grid electricity does Sudan average per day?

Grid supply in Sudan averages about 14 hours per day in the AfroTools dataset. Supply varies widely by area and season.

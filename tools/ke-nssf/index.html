Kenya NSSF · Year 4 effective February 2026

Check Tier I and Tier II contributions against one payslip.

The official Year 4 notice uses a 6% employee contribution matched by the employer, a KSh 9,000 lower earnings limit and a KSh 108,000 upper earnings limit. This tool checks that arithmetic; it does not predict credited returns or convert savings into a pension.

Year 4 rules used

  • Effective for contributions from February 2026.
  • Tier I: 6% of pensionable earnings up to KSh 9,000.
  • Tier II: 6% of the band above KSh 9,000 up to KSh 108,000.
  • Employer matches the employee contribution.
  • NSSF reminds employers to remit by the ninth day of the following month.

Earnings above KSh 108,000 do not increase the statutory Year 4 contribution in this calculation.

Calculation boundary

The calculator applies 6% separately to the Tier I and Tier II pensionable bands, then mirrors the employee amount for the employer. It does not include SHIF, the Affordable Housing Levy, PAYE, voluntary top-ups, investment returns, interest, balances or benefits.

Official sources reviewed 23 July 2026

  • NSSF Year 4 (2026) employer notice supplies the limits, 6% rates, maximum contributions, February 2026 effective period and ninth-day reminder.
  • NSSF Benefits & Grants describes current eligibility and application documents. It does not provide a universal pension formula, so this calculator does not estimate a benefit.
  • NSSF downloads links the NSSF Act 2013 and official forms.

Benefit boundary

NSSF currently lists age/retirement, withdrawal, survivors, invalidity and emigration benefits with different eligibility and documents. Use the official benefits page and branch process. The result here is not evidence that a benefit is payable.

Privacy

No input is stored, put in a URL, or sent to analytics, AI or an API. Do not enter a member number, national ID or other personal identifier. CSV, JSON and PDF are created locally.

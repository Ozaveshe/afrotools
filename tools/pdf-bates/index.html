What is Bates Stamping?
Bates stamping (or Bates numbering) assigns a unique sequential identifier to every page in a document set. It is widely used in legal proceedings, regulatory filings, and corporate record-keeping for easy reference and retrieval.
Features
  • Custom prefix and suffix
  • Configurable zero-padding
  • 6 position options
  • Adjustable font size (8-24pt)
  • Custom stamp colour
  • Courier font for legal readability
  • Client-side processing only
Privacy
Your PDF is processed entirely in your browser. No files are uploaded to any server. The stamped document is generated locally and remains completely private.

The PDF Bates Numbering tool adds sequential identification numbers to every page of your PDF documents, a standard practice in legal discovery, litigation support, and regulatory compliance. Upload your PDF, configure the numbering format including prefix, suffix, starting number, and position, and the tool stamps each page accordingly. It handles multi-page documents efficiently and lets you download the stamped PDF immediately. Lawyers, paralegals, compliance officers, and anyone involved in document-heavy legal proceedings will find this tool essential for organising evidence, referencing specific pages during depositions, and maintaining proper document control.

Common formats include a case identifier as the prefix (e.g. "CASE-001-") and a year or document type as the suffix (e.g. "-2024"). The prefix appears before the number and the suffix after. Both are optional.
Zero-padding adds leading zeros to maintain consistent number length. With 6-digit padding, the number 1 becomes "000001", 42 becomes "000042", and so on. This ensures all stamps are the same width, which is standard in legal documents.
Currently, this tool stamps one PDF at a time. To maintain continuous numbering across multiple documents, note the last number used and set the starting number for the next document accordingly. For example, if your first 10-page document ends at 000010, set the starting number to 11 for the next document.
Courier is a monospaced font where every character has the same width. This is the standard for Bates stamps in legal practice because it ensures consistent alignment and easy readability when scanning through pages. All digits occupy equal space, making numbers easy to compare.

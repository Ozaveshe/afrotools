About NYSC Allowance

How much is the NYSC allowance in 2024?
The federal NYSC allowance (allawee) was increased to ₦33,000 per month in 2024, up from ₦19,800. Some states provide additional top-ups ranging from ₦2,000 to ₦20,000 per month. Lagos offers the highest top-up at ₦20,000, bringing total monthly income to ₦53,000.
Which states pay the highest NYSC top-up?
Lagos State offers one of the highest top-ups at around ₦20,000/month. Rivers and Delta states also offer good additional allowances at ₦15,000 and ₦12,000 respectively. Northern states like Borno and Zamfara tend to offer lower or no additional state allowances.
Can corps members work part-time during NYSC?
Corps members can engage in income-generating activities during free time, especially during community development service. Many corpers tutor students, freelance online or run small businesses. Your primary place of assignment takes priority and you must meet your daily obligations first.
What other benefits do NYSC corps members receive?
Beyond the monthly allowance, corps members receive NHIS health insurance coverage, accommodation at CDS venues (corpers lodge or equivalent), NYSC certificate essential for most Nigerian jobs and government positions, networking across states, and opportunity to apply for NYSC Skill Acquisition and Entrepreneurship Development (SAED) grants.
How much can an NYSC corps member save?
With discipline, corps members in low-cost states can save ₦10,000–₦20,000 per month. In Lagos with the high top-up (₦53,000/month total), savings of ₦25,000–₦35,000/month are possible. Avoiding excessive spending at camp and managing accommodation costs are key. Over 12 months, savings of ₦120,000–₦400,000 are achievable.

How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how the result is produced, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before you rely on it.

Methodology & assumptions
We calculate total monthly income with the formula federal allowance + state top-up + side income, using a fixed federal NYSC allowance of ₦33,000 (the 2024 “allawee” rate), the state top-up you select (from ₦0 up to ₦20,000/month for Lagos), and any side income you enter. Net monthly savings is that total minus your monthly expenses, and the 12-month figures simply multiply the monthly totals by 12. Key assumptions: the ₦33,000 federal allowance is paid in full every month, the selected state top-up is fixed and reliable, and your inputs stay constant across the whole service year.
Limitations & what to verify
This is a budget-planning estimate only, not an official NYSC pay determination or a guarantee of payment. The federal allowance figure and especially state top-ups change over time and are sometimes paid late or irregularly. Before you rely on these numbers, verify the current federal allowance, your posting state’s top-up policy, and whether your Place of Primary Assignment (PPA) provides transport, meals or accommodation. Confirm the latest figures with NYSC and your state.

Cybersecurity Readiness Assessment: quick guide

Mark only controls you can verify. The tool scores 20 controls across six domains, applies a limited recent-incident penalty, and highlights priority gaps.

  • What you get: a transparent self-assessment score, domain breakdown, and priority action list.
  • How it works: selected controls earn their displayed points; recent incidents reduce the overall planning score.
  • What to check: confirm technical evidence, current legal duties, insurance terms, and sector requirements with qualified specialists.

Planning aid. The result is not a penetration test, audit, legal opinion, or compliance certificate.

Cybersecurity Readiness Assessment FAQ

What does the cybersecurity score measure?

It measures the 20 controls you mark as present across six domains, with a limited penalty for recent incidents.

Is this a cybersecurity audit or compliance certificate?

No. It is a local self-assessment for planning. Verify technical findings and current legal or sector duties with qualified specialists.

Does the assessment upload security details?

The assessment runs in the browser. Do not enter credentials, customer records, incident evidence, private keys, or other sensitive material.

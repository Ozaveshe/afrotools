Direct answer

A Zimbabwe roora or lobola estimate can include cattle-equivalent value, added cash, gifts, food, travel, and meeting support. This page gives families a clear worksheet in USD. It is not an official price list and should not replace respectful discussion with both families.

Zimbabwe planning checklist

  • Clarify which items are symbolic, which are cash, and which are gifts.
  • Ask whether values are being discussed in USD, local currency, cattle, or a mix.
  • Separate mother, father, family, and ceremony lines only when the family actually uses those categories.
  • Keep a notes field for what was agreed, what is pending, and what should not be assumed.
  • Confirm affordability and timeline before promising a payment schedule.

Questions

Is Zimbabwe roora the same as a fixed price list?

No. Roora or lobola discussions can include symbolic cattle, cash, gifts, and meeting costs, but expectations vary by family and community. Treat any estimate as a discussion aid.

Why does the Zimbabwe page use USD defaults?

Many Zimbabwe planning conversations are expressed in USD or USD-linked values. The calculator still lets you edit every value and record family notes.

What should I separate in a Zimbabwe lobola budget?

Separate livestock-equivalent value, additional cash, gifts for family members, meeting food, travel, and ceremony support so each line can be discussed clearly.

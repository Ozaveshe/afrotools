How to calculate landed cost

Landed cost starts with CIF, then adds duty, taxes, levies, shipping, insurance, port charges, clearing charges and FX impact. This tool keeps official, estimated and user-entered values visibly separate.

What is CIF?

CIF means cost, insurance and freight. It is often used for planning import costs, but the final customs assessment can differ.

What is FOB?

FOB is the value of goods before freight and insurance to the destination port. FOB plus freight plus insurance equals CIF.

What importers should verify

  • HS classification and restrictions.
  • Customs or assessed value.
  • Duty, VAT, levies and exemptions.
  • Shipping, insurance, port and clearing quotes.
  • FX rate used for planning and clearance.

Methodology

  • Start with CIF, then add duty, VAT, levies, port or terminal estimates, clearing costs, and FX impact.
  • When you know the official assessed value or rate, enter it as an override instead of relying on the stored planning preset.

Before shipment

  • Confirm HS code, restricted goods, age limits, valuation method, exemptions, and destination-port charges.
  • Export or copy the planning estimate for supplier, freight, finance, insurance, or clearing-agent review.

What costs are usually missed on import budgets?
Freight, insurance, customs levies, VAT on a duty-inclusive base, port charges, and FX swings are the most common blind spots.
When should I use the calculator?
Use it before placing the order, again when freight is confirmed, and once more before final customs clearance.

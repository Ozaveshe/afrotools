Frequently Asked Questions

How is import duty calculated in Nigeria?

Nigeria's National Customs Service (NCS) calculates duty on the Cost, Insurance, and Freight (CIF) value. The duty rate varies by product category (5-50% depending on classification). After duty is assessed, VAT of 7.5% is applied to the CIF value plus duty. Additional levies include CISS (1% on FOB) and ETLS (0.5% on CIF). The total landed cost includes all these components converted to NGN at the official exchange rate.

What documents do I need to import goods to Kenya?

The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) requires: (1) Bill of Lading or Airway Bill, (2) Commercial Invoice, (3) Packing List, (4) Certificate of Origin, (5) Import Declaration Form (IDF), (6) Company registration documents. Goods may be subject to inspection at the port. You can pay duty using various methods including bank transfer. Filing an Electronic Single Window (ESW) declaration is now mandatory for all imports.

Which African countries have the lowest import duties?

South Africa generally has low tariffs for many categories (particularly electronics at 0%), while countries like Kenya and Tanzania are part of the East African Community (EAC) with harmonized rates. WAEMU countries (including Senegal and Ghana) have fixed tariff bands. Botswana and Namibia also maintain competitive duty rates. The actual cost depends on your product category, so use the calculator to compare destinations for your specific goods.

How do I use an HS code?

HS codes (Harmonized System codes) are 6-10 digit numbers that classify products for customs purposes. Each digit level narrows the classification. For example: 8517 = Telephone equipment, 8517.12 = Smartphones, 8517.12.00 = Smartphones (most specific). Use the HS Code Lookup table above to find your product, or search the WCO HS database. Include the full code on your commercial invoice and customs declaration when importing.

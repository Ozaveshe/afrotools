About HELB Repayment

How is HELB repayment calculated?
HELB repayment is 10% of your gross monthly salary or a minimum of KSh 1,500 per month, whichever is higher. Repayment starts 12 months after completing your studies or when you get your first employment, whichever comes first.
What is the interest rate on HELB loans?
HELB charges a discounted interest rate of 4% per annum (simple interest) for loanees who repay on time. Defaulters are charged 12% per annum. The 4% rate is significantly lower than commercial bank rates in Kenya (currently 13-20%).
What happens if I default on HELB?
HELB defaulters face a 5% annual penalty on the outstanding balance in addition to the 12% interest rate. HELB can also blacklist you with credit reference bureaus (CRBs), making it harder to get bank loans and even some jobs. Employers can be directed to deduct directly from your salary.
How do I repay HELB?
HELB repayments can be made via M-Pesa (Paybill 200999, account number: your ID number), bank transfer, or your employer can deduct directly from your salary via checkoff system. Always keep your HELB ID number safe and update your contact details regularly.
Can I clear HELB early?
Yes, you can make lump sum payments to clear your HELB balance early with no penalties. Some loanees negotiate reduced settlement amounts — contact HELB directly on 0711052000 for settlement discussions. Early clearance saves you significant interest payments.

Country-Specific Formatting

African job markets have distinct expectations. Nigerian employers typically expect to see your date of birth, marital status, state of origin, NYSC details, and a passport photograph. Kenyan CVs include national ID numbers and KCSE/KCPE grades. South African CVs omit all demographic information to comply with employment equity legislation. Egyptian CVs include military service status and photos. The AfroTools CV Builder automatically adjusts which fields appear based on your selected country.

Tips for African Job Applications

  • Keep your CV to 2 pages unless you have 10+ years of experience.
  • Include a professional summary at the top with your key achievements and career goals.
  • Quantify your accomplishments where possible: revenue generated, team sizes managed, projects delivered.
  • List your most recent experience first and work backwards chronologically.
  • Include references with full contact details — most African employers expect this.
  • For Nigerian CVs, always include your NYSC status and state of origin.
  • For South African CVs, exclude personal details like age, gender, and photo.

CVs are downloaded as PDF files formatted to A4 paper size (210mm × 297mm). The PDF is print-ready and compatible with all major job portals and applicant tracking systems (ATS).

Should I include a photo on my CV?

It depends on the country. In Nigeria, Kenya, Egypt, and most West and East African countries, a professional photo is expected. In South Africa, photos are discouraged due to employment equity regulations. The builder will guide you based on your selected country.

What is the difference between CV and resume?

In Africa, "CV" (Curriculum Vitae) is the preferred term. It is a comprehensive document covering your full professional history. In the US, "resume" refers to a shorter 1-page document.

Risk flags

    Decision checklist

      Sources & freshness

      Last reviewed: 16 May 2026. The calculator uses user-entered provider prices, appliance labels, and household or business spend, not a live PayGo tariff feed.

      Source note: Treat the provider quote, appliance nameplates, warranty booklet, utility bill, generator fuel receipts, and mobile-money payment history as the source documents for the final decision.

      Before signing, verify the quote with the solar provider contract, appliance watt labels, battery usable capacity, warranty terms, repossession rules, payment fees, and your latest generator, grid, or charging receipts.

      Methodology

      1. Daily load equals each appliance wattage multiplied by quantity and daily usage hours.
      2. PayGo repayments are normalized to monthly cost using daily, weekly, or monthly frequency.
      3. Total paid equals deposit plus all repayments over the term plus the maintenance buffer.
      4. Coverage compares usable daily kit energy with the daily load and your critical-load target.
      5. Savings compare the PayGo total paid with current generator, grid, candle, charging, or fuel spend over the same term.

      Disclaimer

      This is an estimate only. It does not replace a licensed solar installer, electrician, lender disclosure, consumer-protection advice, or the final PayGo contract. Confirm taxes, fees, warranty, penalties, and ownership transfer terms locally.

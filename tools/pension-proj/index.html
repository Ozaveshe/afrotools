Exact method

The gross annual return minus fee drag becomes one effective net annual rate. The engine converts it to an equivalent monthly rate, grows the opening balance, then adds that month's contributions. Contributions change once per projection year using your growth assumption.

monthly rate = (1 + gross return - fee drag)^(1/12) - 1

Today's-money value divides the ending balance by the compounded inflation factor. The two sensitivity cases change gross return by two percentage points; they are not Monte Carlo probabilities.

Sources and freshness

Investor.gov's compound-interest calculator documents initial investment, monthly contribution, time, estimated rate and compounding frequency as user inputs.

IOPS pension-projection good practices call for visible assumptions covering returns, contributions, inflation and costs. The page was reviewed on 22 July 2026; your scheme evidence must be no more than 366 days old.

Deliberate exclusions

  • No country contribution rates, ceilings, tax relief, retirement age or access rules.
  • No provider rankings, fund forecast, probability, annuity, drawdown or "safe withdrawal" promise.
  • No defined-benefit entitlement, state pension, currency conversion or legal classification.
  • No silent AI request, network calculation, local storage or account gate.

How to use the result

Compare the assumptions with your latest statement and fee disclosure. Run lower-return, higher-fee and higher-inflation cases. Ask the scheme administrator or a licensed adviser about benefits, guarantees, taxes, access, beneficiary treatment and retirement-income products before acting.

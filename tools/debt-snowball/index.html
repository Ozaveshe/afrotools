Frequently Asked Questions

What is held constant in the comparison?

Both methods use the same starting debts and the same total monthly budget: all entered minimums plus the extra payment.

Why can a plan fail to repay within the model?

If the budget does not cover accruing interest or repayment would take more than 600 months, the result is shown as not repaid instead of a false payoff date.

Can actual lender results differ?

Yes. Fees, daily interest, changing rates, payment timing and lender allocation rules can change the result.

Does avalanche always cost less?
Under this model, directing spare budget to the highest annual rate should not cost more interest than a different order. Ties and short repayment periods can produce the same result.
What if I cannot cover the minimum payments?
Do not rely on this payoff plan. Contact lenders or a qualified local debt adviser promptly to discuss hardship, restructuring or other available options.
Should I consolidate?
Compare the new loan's total fees, rate, term and total repayment against keeping the current debts. A lower monthly payment can still cost more over a longer term.

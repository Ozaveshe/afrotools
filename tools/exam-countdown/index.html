How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how the countdown is produced, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before you rely on it.

Methodology & assumptions
Each countdown is a live in-browser calculation: we take your exam date at local midnight (interpreted as date + T00:00:00 in your device's time zone) and subtract the current time from your device clock. The remaining milliseconds are then split into units with the formula days = floor(difference ÷ 86,400,000 ms), with the leftover converted to hours, minutes and seconds; the display refreshes every second. Each card is also tagged with a prep phase based on days remaining — "Final week" at 7 days or fewer, "Past papers" at 30 or fewer, "Revision build" at 75 or fewer, otherwise "Foundation phase". The assumption is that your device clock and time zone are correct. Preset exam windows carry a source note and a link to the relevant examination authority; countdowns you add are saved in your browser's localStorage so they persist between visits.
Limitations & what to verify
This is a planning aid, not an official timetable, and it does not replace the schedule published by any examination board. Preset dates are treated as planning anchors that can change — several presets deliberately ask you to enter the exact first-paper date from the official timetable. Because the timer uses your own device clock and time zone, a wrong system clock will give a wrong countdown. Before making study, travel, or registration commitments, verify the latest first-paper date, registration deadline, venue and subject entries with the relevant authority (ECZ, UNEB, NECTA, WAEC, KNEC, ZIMSEC, or the South African DBE) or your school.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which exams are pre-loaded?
The tool includes current Africa exam presets for ECZ, UNEB, NECTA, South Africa NSC, WAEC Ghana, KNEC KCSE, and ZIMSEC. Some presets have a confirmed planning date; others ask you to enter the exact first paper date from the official timetable.
Does the countdown save when I close my browser?
Yes. Your active countdowns are saved to localStorage in your browser. They will still be there when you come back. No account or sign-up is needed.
Can I track multiple exams at the same time?
Yes. You can add as many countdowns as you want. Each one displays as a separate card with its own live ticking timer showing days, hours, minutes, and seconds.
How do I share a countdown on WhatsApp?
Each countdown card has a green WhatsApp share button. Click it to send a message with the exam name, date, and remaining time to any WhatsApp contact or group.
Are the exam dates accurate?
Treat each preset as a planning aid. Always verify the latest paper timetable with the exam body or your school before making study, travel, or registration plans.

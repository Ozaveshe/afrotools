The 50/30/20 split is a rule of thumb, not a requirement or country-specific standard. The US Consumer Financial Protection Bureau describes 50% of take-home pay for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings goals; its teaching guide also says not everyone can follow the rule and people should choose a rule that fits their circumstances.

Wants: dining out, streaming services, gym memberships, clothing beyond basics, entertainment, holidays. The line can blur — an internet subscription may be a need if you work from home.

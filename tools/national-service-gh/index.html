About Ghana National Service

How much is Ghana National Service allowance in 2024?
Ghana NSS allowance ranges from GH₵1,800 to GH₵2,500 per month depending on the posting sector and year. Government-sector postings typically pay GH₵1,800–2,000, while NGO and health sector placements may be higher. Hardship areas attract additional allowances.
Who qualifies for Ghana National Service?
Ghanaian citizens who hold a first degree, HND or postgraduate qualification from an accredited institution are required to complete 12 months of national service. The requirement applies to both public and private university graduates. Foreign nationals attending Ghanaian institutions may be exempted.
How do I apply for Ghana National Service posting?
Applications are made online through the NSS Secretariat website (nss.gov.gh). You need your institution certificate, ID documents and can indicate posting preferences. Postings are assigned by the NSS Secretariat based on national needs, your qualifications and preferences. Early application improves preferred posting chances.
What are the benefits of Ghana National Service?
National Service provides: monthly allowance, hands-on work experience in your field, professional networking, NSS certificate required for further employment and postgraduate applications. Many service personnel are absorbed permanently by host organisations. It is also a requirement for most government jobs in Ghana.
What happens if I don't do national service in Ghana?
Failure to complete national service bars you from obtaining a National Service Certificate, which is required for many government and private sector jobs, postgraduate admissions and professional registration. NSS has enforcement mechanisms and you can be called up later if you defaulted.

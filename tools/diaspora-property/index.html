Diaspora Investment Considerations
  • FX risk: African currencies tend to depreciate against USD/GBP/EUR. This increases your buying power when purchasing, but reduces rental yield in foreign currency terms over time.
  • Remote management: Budget 10–15% for a property manager if you\'re based abroad. Use the Property Management Fee Calculator.
  • Title verification: Critical for diaspora buyers who can\'t visit frequently. Use the Land Title Verification Checklist.
  • Repatriation: Verify you can send rental income abroad. Nigeria has CBN restrictions on FX repatriation; Kenya and South Africa are more straightforward.
  • Tax obligations: Rental income may be taxable in BOTH your country of residence AND the property\'s country. Check for Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs).
  • Legal representation: Always have YOUR OWN lawyer in the property\'s country. Never use the seller\'s lawyer.

Remote-buyer control plan

Diaspora property purchases carry foreign-exchange, agent, title, construction, family, tax and monitoring risks. The app should turn a remote idea into staged verification and release controls.

Decisions this clarifies

  • Whether the buyer is purchasing, building, renovating or funding a relative-managed project
  • Which controls stop title, FX, construction and agent-risk leakage
  • Whether rental yield, mortgage scheme or future exit justifies the cash transfer

Before you rely on it

  • Use independent title, survey and valuation checks before deposit
  • Stage funds against evidence, not promises or photos alone
  • Track FX rate, transfer fees, tax, service charges and maintenance in one model

Red flags

  • Family or agent refuses independent lawyer or valuer involvement
  • Payments go to personal accounts without receipts or milestones
  • No power of attorney or authority document for the local representative

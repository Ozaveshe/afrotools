Proforma invoice FAQ

What should I check before sending the proforma?

Confirm buyer details, seller registration, Incoterm, ports, origin country, validity date, HS codes, quantities, price basis, freight, insurance, packaging, inspection, payment terms, and any permit or bank wording.

Can this support LC, permit, or broker review?

Yes as a draft handoff. Banks, brokers, and agencies may require specific formats or extra documents, so treat the PDF and copied summary as review material until requirements are confirmed.

Is this a final invoice?

No. It is a proforma quote, not a demand for payment or official filing. Prepare the final commercial invoice only after the order, shipment, and compliance details are agreed.

Proforma invoice methodology

The document builder combines seller, buyer, product, HS code, Incoterm, origin, port, freight, insurance, validity, payment, and delivery fields into a draft quote and export summary. It does not submit anything to customs, banks, buyers, or regulators.

Draft document

Trade wording, HS classification, permits, bank requirements, and shipment costs must be confirmed for the specific transaction.

Before acting

  • Verify HS codes, origin, valuation, permits, inspection wording, Incoterms, ports, and payment terms with the buyer, broker, bank, or relevant authority.
  • Replace planning freight and insurance values with current written quotes before relying on CIF or landed-cost planning.
  • Keep the final commercial invoice separate until contract, shipment, and compliance details are agreed.

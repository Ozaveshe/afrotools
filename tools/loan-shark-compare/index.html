Frequently Asked Questions

How do African informal lenders charge interest?
Most informal lenders charge a flat percentage per week or month on the original loan (not reducing balance). For example, "20% per month" on N500,000 means you pay N100,000/month in interest — forever until principal is repaid. This results in an effective APR of 240%+, many times higher than any formal lender.
Why do people still use loan sharks in Africa?
Speed and accessibility: informal lenders offer cash within hours with no paperwork, no credit check, and accept any collateral. For someone without a bank account, salary slip, or credit history, formal credit is inaccessible. Improving your credit profile (maintaining a salary account, using digital wallets) is the first step toward accessing cheaper credit.
What are safer alternatives to informal lending?
In order of cost (cheapest first): (1) SACCO/credit union loan (8-15%/yr), (2) Salary advance from employer, (3) Digital lending apps (Branch, Tala, Carbon — 20-50% APR, still high but formal), (4) Microfinance institution (36-60% APR), (5) Bank personal loan (18-30% APR). Any of these is better than informal lenders at 240-600% APR.

FAQ

Common Questions

Is this a legally binding contract?
No. CreatorSplit generates a record of understanding between collaborators. For legally binding agreements, consult a lawyer. However, having a written record prevents disputes and serves as strong evidence of intent.
How do splits work for streaming?
When streaming revenue comes in, you log the total amount. CreatorSplit automatically calculates each person's share and tracks who has been paid and who is still owed.
Can I change splits after they're agreed?
Yes, but changes require acknowledgment from all collaborators. The amendment process is built in — propose changes, all parties review, and the new split takes effect going forward.
Does it work offline?
Yes. All split data is stored locally on your device first and synced when you're back online. You can create splits, log earnings, and track payouts with no internet.
How does the AI suggest fair splits?
Our AI analyzes industry-standard splits by project type and role. For example, an Afrobeats track typically splits: producer 20-30%, artist 30-50%, songwriter 10-20%, engineer 5-10%.
Is my data private?
Completely. Your data is stored locally first, then encrypted in Supabase if you create an account. Collaborators only see what you share with them via the shareable link.

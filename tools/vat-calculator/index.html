Frequently Asked Questions

What is VAT (Value Added Tax)?
Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax collected at each stage of production or distribution. Most African countries use VAT as a major revenue source, with rates from 0% to 20%+.
What's the difference between adding and extracting VAT?
Add VAT: Calculates total price from a net (before-tax) amount. Extract VAT: Separates the VAT portion from a VAT-inclusive price. For example, with 15% VAT: adding to 100 gives 115; extracting from 115 gives net 100 and VAT 15.
Which African countries have no VAT?
Somalia, Eritrea, and Libya currently do not have operational VAT systems. Some countries like Liberia and Sierra Leone use GST (Goods and Services Tax) instead.
What are VAT exemptions and reduced rates?
VAT exemptions mean certain goods/services (like basic food, medicine, education) are not subject to VAT. Reduced rates apply lower percentages. Many African countries exempt healthcare and basic food staples.
Can I use this for invoicing?
Yes! Use the Multi-Item Invoice tab to calculate VAT for multiple line items with different treatments (standard, zero-rated, exempt). Export results as a branded PDF receipt.
What is the VAT rate in Nigeria?
Nigeria's VAT rate is 7.5%, one of the lowest in Africa. It was increased from 5% to 7.5% via the Finance Act 2019. Basic food items were exempted from VAT starting January 2026.
What is the VAT rate in Kenya?
Kenya's standard VAT rate is 16%. Some goods and services are zero-rated or exempt, including basic foodstuffs and agricultural inputs.
What is the VAT rate in South Africa?
South Africa's VAT rate is 15%, increased from 14% in April 2018. Basic foodstuffs like bread, milk, and eggs are zero-rated.

Understanding VAT Across Africa

Value Added Tax (VAT) is the primary consumption tax used by most African countries. Rates range from 7.5% in Nigeria to 20% in Morocco and Madagascar, with most countries falling in the 15-18% range. Understanding VAT is crucial for businesses operating across African borders, as rates, exemptions, and registration thresholds vary significantly by country. This calculator supports all 50+ African countries with verified 2026 rates.

The multi-item invoice feature allows businesses to calculate VAT for mixed baskets containing standard-rated, zero-rated, and exempt items. This is particularly useful in countries like South Africa and Kenya where basic food staples are zero-rated while other goods carry the full VAT rate. Use the comparison feature to understand how VAT impacts your costs across different African markets.

Common VAT Exemptions in Africa

Most African countries exempt or zero-rate essential items: basic unprocessed food, medical services and supplies, educational services, and agricultural inputs. Some countries like Nigeria have recently expanded exemptions to include more food items under 2026 tax reforms. Always check your specific country's revenue authority for the latest list of exempt and zero-rated supplies, as these change with annual budget announcements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is VAT (Value Added Tax)? ›

Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax collected at each stage of production or distribution based on the value added at that stage. Most African countries use VAT as a major revenue source, with rates varying from 0% (some countries with no VAT system) to 20%+ in countries like Madagascar and Morocco.

What's the difference between VAT-inclusive and VAT-exclusive? ›

VAT-exclusive: The price shown does NOT include VAT. Add VAT is calculated on top.
VAT-inclusive: The price shown INCLUDES VAT already. When extracting, VAT is removed from the total.

Which African countries have no VAT? ›

Somalia, Eritrea, and Libya currently do not have operational VAT systems. Some countries like Liberia and Sierra Leone use GST (Goods and Services Tax) instead, which functions similarly to VAT.

What are VAT exemptions and reduced rates? ›

VAT exemptions mean certain goods/services (like basic food, medicine, education) are not subject to VAT. Reduced rates apply lower percentages (e.g., Nigeria exempted basic food from Jan 2026). Many countries have exemptions for healthcare and education.

Is this calculator accurate for my business? ›

This calculator provides standard rates as of March 2026. For tax planning and compliance, consult your country's revenue authority or a tax professional, as VAT regulations, exemptions, and thresholds vary significantly by jurisdiction and product category.

How often are VAT rates updated? ›

VAT rates are set by each country's government and can change with budget announcements or tax reforms. We verify rates quarterly and mark major changes. Subscribe to AfroTools for rate update notifications.

Can I use this for invoicing? ›

Yes! This calculator helps you quickly calculate VAT for invoices. Export results as PDF for documentation. However, ensure you're registered for VAT in your country and following local invoicing requirements.

What about digital services VAT (B2C)? ›

Several countries apply different VAT rates for digital services. For example, Tanzania applies 16% for B2C digital payments vs. 18% standard. Check your country's specific rules if selling digital products or services.

How do I register for VAT? ›

VAT registration requirements vary by country and annual turnover thresholds. Contact your local revenue authority: TRA (Tanzania), KRA (Kenya), SARS (South Africa), GRA (Ghana), FIRS (Nigeria), etc. Most require registration once turnover exceeds the threshold.

Can I claim VAT credits as a business? ›

Yes, registered VAT businesses can claim input VAT credits on business purchases and expenses, paying only the difference to the tax authority. This is a core VAT principle across African countries. Keep all invoices for audit trails.

What currencies does this support? ›

This calculator uses local currency symbols for each country (KES for Kenya, ZAR for South Africa, etc.). Enter amounts in your local currency and results display in the same currency. For cross-border comparisons, use live exchange rates.

Is my data saved or shared? ›

All calculations are performed locally in your browser. No data is sent to servers or tracked. Your calculations are completely private. PDF exports are generated client-side and contain only the data you enter.

What is the VAT rate in Nigeria? ›

Nigeria's VAT rate is 7.5%, one of the lowest in Africa. It was increased from 5% to 7.5% via the Finance Act 2019. Basic food items were exempted from VAT starting January 2026 under the new tax reform.

What is the VAT rate in Kenya? ›

Kenya's standard VAT rate is 16%. Some goods and services are zero-rated or exempt, including basic foodstuffs and agricultural inputs. The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) administers VAT collection.

What is the VAT rate in South Africa? ›

South Africa's VAT rate is 15%, increased from 14% in April 2018. Basic foodstuffs like bread, milk, and eggs are zero-rated. SARS administers VAT collection and registration.

Which African country has the highest VAT? ›

Morocco has one of the highest standard VAT rates in Africa at 20%. Other high-VAT countries include Cameroon (19.25%), Senegal (18%), and Tanzania (18%). Use our calculator above to compare rates across all countries.

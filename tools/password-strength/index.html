Password Best Practices
Use a password manager (Bitwarden is free and open-source) to generate and store unique passwords for every account. Never reuse passwords — a breach at one site exposes all your accounts if you reuse. Enable 2FA on every account that offers it, especially email and banking.

Password Strength Checker FAQ

How should I use this?

Type a test password or draft passphrase, review the local score and weaknesses, then copy a brief without the password.

What should I verify?

Check length, reuse, dictionary words, predictable substitutions, service password policy and MFA availability.

Does it prove my password is safe?

No. It is a local estimate and does not check breach databases or guarantee acceptance by a service.

Privacy & verification

Password strength is a local estimate, not a breach check

The analyzer runs in your browser and avoids adding the raw password to the copyable brief. It cannot confirm whether a password has appeared in a breach or will be accepted by a specific service.

Methodology

  • Scores length, estimated entropy, character variety, repeated characters, common-password matches, and predictable sequences.
  • Shows a time-to-crack estimate using a fixed offline guess-rate assumption.
  • Generates draft passphrases locally and copies only the safety brief, not the entered password.

Before acting

  • Use a trusted password manager, unique passwords per site, and MFA for important accounts.
  • Use a trusted breach-monitoring service separately if you need compromise checks.
  • Treat the result as a local planning signal, not a security guarantee, audit, or breach-database lookup.

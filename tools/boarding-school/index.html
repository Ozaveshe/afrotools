Boarding School FAQs

How much does Federal Government College (FGC) cost in Nigeria?
Federal Government Colleges in Nigeria are relatively affordable, typically charging ₦30,000–₦80,000 per term for tuition and boarding combined. Additional costs for uniforms, bedding, provisions and school items add ₦50,000–₦150,000 per term, bringing the total to around ₦100,000–₦250,000 per term.
How much does boarding school cost in Kenya?
Kenya national secondary schools (day) charge KSh 10,000–30,000 per year for government schools. Boarding adds significantly — government boarding schools charge KSh 30,000–80,000 per year total. Private boarding schools range from KSh 150,000–500,000+ per year.
What are the hidden costs of boarding school in Africa?
Beyond school fees, boarding school hidden costs include: uniforms and sports kit (₦30,000–100,000 once-off), bedding and personal items (₦20,000–80,000), school provisions/tuck (₦15,000–50,000/term), entertainment and pocket money, medical fees, transportation during holidays, and annual PTA levies.
Is boarding school worth it in Africa?
Boarding schools in Africa generally produce better academic outcomes due to structured study time, peer learning and focus. Federal/National boarding schools in Nigeria and Kenya offer excellent value. However, the separation from family and cost must be weighed. Day schools closer to home can be equally good with strong parental support.
Which are the best boarding schools in Nigeria?
Top boarding schools in Nigeria include: Federal Government Colleges (Unity Schools), Kings College Lagos, Queen's College Lagos, Government College Umuahia, and premium private schools like Meadow Hall, Atlantic Hall and Greensprings. Government unity schools offer the best value-for-money ratio.

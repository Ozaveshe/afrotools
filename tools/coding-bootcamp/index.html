How it works — methodology & sources

Updated for 2026. Here is exactly how the comparison is built, the assumptions behind it, and what to verify before you apply.

Methodology & assumptions
This comparator filters a small curated dataset of African coding bootcamps by region, your coding level, and budget band (USD), then labels each by funding model (free, income-share/ISA, or paid) and an editorial “top pick” flag, alongside quoted programme facts: duration, cost, track, format, hiring rate, and salary outcome. It compiles a shortlist and a summary (visible options, top picks, free/ISA count, and cheapest paid option) rather than calculating a score. Our key assumption: the hiring rates and salary ranges shown are programme-reported figures, not independently audited, and the dataset is a first-pass planning snapshot rather than a live admissions feed.
Limitations & what to verify
This is a comparison aid only — not an admission decision, endorsement, official quote, contract review, job guarantee, or salary guarantee, and it does not replace the provider's official terms or your own due diligence. Hiring-rate and salary figures are provider-stated and real outcomes vary by cohort, background, and market. Verify the current official programme page, eligibility rules, application deadline, tuition or income-share/ISA contract terms, refund policy, weekly time commitment, and whether placement and salary claims are independently supported before you apply or sign anything.

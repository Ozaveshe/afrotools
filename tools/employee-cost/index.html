Why total employee cost needs an evidence pack

Salary is only one line. Record current employer obligations, benefits, allowances, equipment, administration, and hiring costs separately so finance can trace every assumption.

Boundary: The canonical planner uses user-entered amounts. Country rules, ceilings, and rates can change; verify them with the responsible official authority and qualified payroll or legal professionals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What belongs in the employee-cost plan?

Enter base salary, employer statutory obligations, benefits, allowances, other recurring costs, and one-off hiring or equipment costs as separate evidence-backed lines.

Does the canonical planner supply statutory rates?

No. Use the relevant country route and current official authority, then enter the resulting employer-obligation amount and dated source label here.

Is the result a payroll or legal approval?

No. It is a local planning brief. Payroll, finance, HR, and legal reviewers should confirm the current rules and contract before hiring.

Hiring-cost planning model

Reviewed 2026. Payroll deductions, employer contributions, and benefits can change.

Methodology

  • Combines salary, benefits, allowances, employer contributions, equipment, onboarding, and buffer assumptions.
  • Routes users to country-specific employee-cost calculators for more detailed local inputs.
  • Copyable brief keeps the data local unless the user chooses to copy, save, or export it.

Before acting

  • Confirm statutory deductions, employer contributions, pension, insurance, leave, tax, and contract terms.
  • Check worker classification, probation, equipment, remote-work costs, and current local labour guidance.
  • Treat this as a planning estimate, not legal, tax, payroll, immigration, or compliance advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Each tree sequesters approximately 0.022 tonnes of CO₂ per year (industry average). At $15/tonne (Verra VCS standard price), a 100-tree planting earns roughly $33/year in carbon credits. Over 25 years that's $825 before registration costs.
Fruit trees (mango, avocado, citrus) typically offer the best short-term returns (from year 3-5) through annual harvests. Timber trees (teak, eucalyptus) offer a large lump sum at year 15. Agroforestry mixes (moringa, shea) balance income streams. Indigenous trees score highest for carbon credits and biodiversity.
Register with Verra (VCS), Gold Standard, or Plan Vivo. African brokers include South Pole, Xpansiv, and local carbon aggregators. Minimum project sizes typically require 100+ hectares for direct registration, but cooperatives allow smallholders to aggregate.

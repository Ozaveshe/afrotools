Understanding African Exchange Rates

African currency markets are diverse and dynamic. Countries like Nigeria and Egypt have managed float systems where the central bank influences rates, while South Africa's rand is fully floating. The CFA franc zones (West and Central Africa) are pegged to the Euro. Understanding these mechanisms helps explain rate movements and predict trends. For the most accurate rates for your specific transaction, always confirm with your bank or licensed forex dealer before transacting.

What is the difference between CBN rate and parallel market rate?
The Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) publishes an official exchange rate used for formal bank transactions. The parallel market (also called the bureau de change or "black market") rate is set by supply and demand and is often significantly different. Our converter shows the mid-market rate, which typically falls between the official and parallel rates. For actual transactions, confirm rates with your specific provider.

Frequently Asked Questions

E-waste (electronic waste) includes any discarded electrical or electronic equipment. Africa generates over 2.5 million tonnes of e-waste annually, most of which ends up in informal dumpsites. Proper recycling recovers valuable metals, reduces toxic pollution, and creates jobs.
Values are estimated based on recoverable precious metals (gold, silver, copper, palladium), plastic and glass recycling rates, and current African secondary market prices. Working devices also carry a resale premium. Actual prices at collection centres may vary.
On average, proper e-waste recycling diverts approximately 20 kg of CO₂ per kg of e-waste processed, compared to landfill disposal. This accounts for avoided mining emissions and reduced energy use in manufacturing new components from recycled materials.
Look for certified e-waste collection centres, manufacturer take-back programmes (Samsung, HP, Dell), NGO-run collection points, and informal but organised scrap dealers (ensure they use safe practices). Many supermarkets and telecoms now also run drop-off boxes.

The Land Size Converter helps you convert between common African land measurement units including plots, acres, hectares, square metres, and square feet. Land measurements in Africa vary widely by region, with a plot in Lagos differing from one in Accra or Nairobi. This tool uses standardised and region-specific conversion factors so you can compare land sizes accurately. Whether you are buying property, verifying a survey, or comparing listings from different agents, the converter gives you instant, reliable results. Real estate buyers, developers, surveyors, and land agents across Africa rely on it to avoid costly measurement misunderstandings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the size of a standard Nigerian plot?
A standard Nigerian plot measures 60 feet by 120 feet, which equals approximately 648 square meters. A half plot is 60 by 60 feet (324 sqm). Actual sizes can vary slightly depending on the surveyor and location.
What is a South African Morgen?
A Morgen is a traditional South African unit of land measurement equal to approximately 8,567 square meters (about 2.12 acres). It was historically used by Dutch settlers and is still referenced in some rural property dealings.
Which units does the calculator support?
Square meters, square feet, hectares, acres, Nigerian plots (648 sqm), half plots, SA Morgen (8567 sqm), and Kenyan acres. You can input by dimensions (length x width) or enter area directly.
How does the visual preview work?
When you enter dimensions (length x width), a scaled rectangle is drawn on the canvas showing the proportions of the land. This gives you a visual sense of whether the land is a square, wide rectangle, or narrow strip.
How does the price calculator work?
Enter the price per square meter in your local currency. The calculator multiplies that by the total area in square meters to give the total land cost. This helps you quickly estimate land purchase prices.

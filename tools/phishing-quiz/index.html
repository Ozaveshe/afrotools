Phishing Detection Quiz — Africa FAQ

Are these real messages?

No. They are fictional training examples inspired by common scam patterns. Domains, dates, phone numbers, account details, and offers shown in a scenario must not be treated as verified.

Does a high score prove I can identify phishing?

No. It records your answers to ten fixed examples. Real attackers change tactics, and a genuine-looking sender, logo, or domain alone does not prove a message is safe.

What should I do with a suspicious message?

Do not use its links, attachments, QR codes, or contact details. Open the organisation's official app or independently find its official website or number, then report the message through the appropriate local channel.

Kenya CGT method, tax point and scope

Kenya's general capital gains tax rate is 15% of the net gain, effective from 1 January 2023. The transferor pays the tax. A gain can arise on property situated in Kenya, including some direct and indirect share disposals described in the Eighth Schedule.

The current statutory computation separates net transfer value from adjusted cost. This calculator therefore shows incidental transfer costs separately from acquisition, enhancement and preservation costs. It does not substitute a 1 January 2015 market value automatically for older property.

For property under Part I of the Eighth Schedule, the current Act states the due date is the earlier of receipt of the full purchase price or registration of the transfer. KRA's public FAQ describes the tax point as registration. Because transaction facts and portal instructions can differ, verify the live filing and payment step before completion.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Kenya CGT rate in 2026?

The general rate is 15% of the net gain. It has applied since 1 January 2023.

Are all listed shares automatically exempt?

No broad assumption should be made from the word “listed.” KRA describes an exemption for securities traded on a securities exchange licensed by the Capital Markets Authority. Confirm the exchange, transaction and current conditions.

Can this calculator prove a private-residence exemption?

No. Occupation periods, ownership, land size and other statutory provisos require evidence. The tool only applies zero tax after you explicitly confirm eligibility.

Does the calculator create a PDF or file a return?

No. It provides a local TXT planning summary only. It does not create a tax PDF, sign in to iTax, file, pay or produce a KRA assessment.

Leak checklist and next steps

The checklist updates from the reading, mode, usage level, and leak toggle.

  • Take a fresh meter photo and compare it with the current bill reading.
  • Check visible taps, toilet cisterns, storage tanks, and underground lines.
  • Confirm tariff block, fixed charge, tax, and sewerage/service charge with the utility.

Methodology

The calculation method rebuilds a utility-style water bill from meter consumption and tariff blocks.

  1. Consumption = current meter reading - previous meter reading.
  2. Tariff charge = block 1 usage x rate + block 2 usage x rate + remaining usage x block 3 rate.
  3. Subtotal = tariff charge + fixed charge + sewerage or service charge.
  4. Expected bill = subtotal + VAT or tax. Daily usage = consumption x 1,000 / billing days.
  5. Night-flow test = meter change x 1,000 / test hours. The 30-day loss ceiling assumes that flow continued all day; bill impact reruns the tariff blocks with the projected extra m3 and adds tax.

Assumptions and sources

Presets are editable planning assumptions. They are not live official tariffs and may not match every suburb, customer class, subsidy, or meter plan.

  • Source data: current bill, meter readings, municipal tariff notice, city water utility schedule, and official authority updates.
  • Freshness note: reviewed 16 May 2026. Update the tariff blocks, fixed charge, tax, and sewerage charge when your provider changes rates.
  • Official check: confirm final amounts with your water utility, city authority, regulator, or consumer protection office.

Disclaimer

This is an estimate only. It does not replace an official bill, utility account ledger, licensed plumber inspection, municipal decision, or legal advice.

  • Tiered tariffs, minimum charges, communal meters, estimated reads, penalties, reconnection fees, and subsidies can change the result.
  • If a leak or billing dispute is serious, document readings and ask for the detailed account calculation before paying disputed charges.
  • For suspected contamination or unsafe water, use a water quality checker and contact the responsible authority.

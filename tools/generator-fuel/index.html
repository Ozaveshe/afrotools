Fuel Efficiency Tips

  • Run at 50-75% load — this is the sweet spot for fuel efficiency
  • Regular maintenance (oil, filters) improves efficiency by 5-15%
  • Inverter generators use 20-30% less fuel than conventional models
  • Right-size your generator — oversized units waste fuel at low load
  • Consider a hybrid setup: solar for daytime, generator as backup

The Generator Fuel Calculator helps households and businesses in Africa estimate how much fuel their generator consumes based on wattage, load percentage, and running hours. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to run a generator per hour in Nigeria?
The cost depends on your generator size and fuel type. A typical 3.5KVA petrol generator in Nigeria costs approximately ₦500-800 per hour based on current fuel prices. Use our calculator above for exact figures based on your specific setup.
Is solar cheaper than a generator in the long run?
In most cases, yes. A solar panel system typically pays for itself within 2-4 years when compared to ongoing generator fuel costs, especially in Nigeria where fuel prices are high. Use the Solar ROI chart above to see the exact breakeven point for your setup.
What size generator do I need for my home?
A typical 3-bedroom Nigerian home needs a 3.5-5KVA generator. Factor in your essential appliances: AC units (1-1.5KVA each), refrigerator (200W), lights, and fans.
How can I reduce generator fuel consumption?
Use energy-efficient appliances, avoid running the generator at low load, maintain it regularly (oil changes, air filters), and consider an inverter generator which adjusts output to load.

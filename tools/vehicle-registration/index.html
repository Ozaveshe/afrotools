    Frequently Asked Questions

    What happens if my vehicle registration lapses in Africa?
    Penalties for lapsed vehicle registration vary by country. In Kenya, unregistered vehicles face a fine of KSh 5,000-20,000 plus impoundment. In Nigeria, fines range from ₦5,000-50,000 and vehicles can be impounded by FRSC officials. South Africa charges daily penalties for expired registration. In all countries, driving with expired registration also voids your insurance policy.
    Can I renew vehicle registration online in Africa?
    Online renewal is available in some countries. Kenya's eCitizen portal allows online renewal of vehicle registration via NTSA. South Africa has a partial online process. Nigeria's FRSC and VIO have online platforms but physical visits are often still required. Rwanda's RRA portal supports online applications. Most countries are expanding digital services but physical inspection requirements often necessitate in-person visits.
    What documents do I need for vehicle registration renewal?
    For most African countries: current registration certificate, valid roadworthiness certificate, comprehensive or third-party insurance certificate, proof of payment of previous year fees, and ID document. For motorcycles in East Africa, a helmeted rider photo may be required. Commercial vehicles may need additional operating licenses and route permits.

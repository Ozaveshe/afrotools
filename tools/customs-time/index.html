    Frequently Asked Questions

    Why does Nigerian customs take so long?
    Nigeria's Apapa Port is among the most congested in Africa. Delays stem from: massive import volumes relative to infrastructure capacity, high rate of physical inspections (vs scanner-based), documentation requirements across multiple agencies (NCS, NAFDAC, SON, NEMA), congestion on access roads causing 10+ hour truck queues, and endemic corruption. Shippers often report 30-45 days as typical clearance time. Using a reputable customs agent with established relationships significantly reduces delays.
    What documents are needed for customs clearance in Africa?
    Standard documents for commercial clearance: Bill of Lading/Airway Bill, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Certificate of Origin, Import Declaration, and Customs Bond. Some goods need additional certificates: NAFDAC for food/pharma in Nigeria, KEBS for standards in Kenya, SABS for South Africa. Food imports may need phytosanitary or fumigation certificates. Vehicles need original title documents.
    How much does a customs clearing agent charge in Africa?
    Clearing agent fees in Africa typically range from 1-3% of CIF cargo value, plus disbursements. Minimum fees may apply for low-value shipments. In Nigeria, fees of 0.5-1% are common for established importers with pre-qualified status. In Kenya, KEBS inspection fees and IDF fees add to agent costs. Always get a full written quote from your agent before shipping.

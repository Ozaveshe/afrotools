Payslips for All 54 African Countries

Every African employee is legally entitled to a payslip showing their earnings and deductions. This generator covers all 54 African Union member states — from Nigeria's PITA-based PAYE system to Morocco's IR tax, from Kenya's new SHIF health deduction to Rwanda's RSSB pension. The correct tax bands, pension rates, and social security contributions are applied automatically.

For batch payroll, upload a CSV with your employee roster. The tool calculates deductions for each employee, generates individual PDFs, and delivers a ZIP file. Perfect for SMEs running monthly payroll without expensive payroll software.

The housing allowance optimizer finds the basic/housing split that minimises PAYE in countries where housing allowances are tax-advantaged (e.g. Nigeria, Kenya, Uganda). This is one of the most underused legal tax savings tools available to African employees.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a payslip legally required in Africa?

Yes, across all major African economies. Nigeria's Labour Act, Kenya's Employment Act, South Africa's BCEA, Ghana's Labour Act, Tanzania's Employment and Labour Relations Act, and equivalent legislation across the continent require employers to provide itemised pay statements. Failure to provide payslips can result in penalties during labour inspections.

How do I generate payslips in bulk?

Switch to "Batch Generate" mode. Prepare a CSV with columns: Name, BasicSalary (required). Optional: ID, Department, JobTitle, Housing, Transport, Other, Overtime, Bonus, Country, Period. Upload the file, set default country and period, then click Generate. Individual PDFs are bundled into a ZIP download. Row errors are reported in the error log.

Which statutory deductions are included?

Nigeria: PAYE (PITA 7-24%), Pension 8%, NHF 2.5%. Kenya: PAYE (KRA 10-35%), NSSF, SHIF 2.75%, AHL 1.5%. South Africa: PAYE (SARS 18-45%), UIF 1%. Ghana: PAYE, SSNIT 5.5%. Tanzania: PAYE, NSSF 10%. Uganda: PAYE, NSSF 5%. All 54 countries have their primary deductions implemented.

How does the tax optimizer work?

Click "Optimize for Tax Savings" in the Earnings section. The tool tests different basic/housing salary splits across 17 combinations and finds the one that minimises your PAYE. In Nigeria, increasing the housing allowance reduces the taxable base under PITA. Monthly savings of 5-15% are common for mid-senior employees.

Can I add custom deductions like staff loans?

Yes. Use the Custom Deductions section to add any number of extra deduction lines. Staff loans, salary advances, canteen fees, uniform costs — each appears on the payslip with your chosen label and is included in the net pay calculation.

What is multi-currency mode?

Enables payslips for employees paid in a foreign currency (USD, GBP, EUR, AED, CAD, AUD). Enter the exchange rate and the payslip shows gross earnings in both the base currency and the local currency equivalent. All deductions are calculated in local currency.

What is the payslip serial number?

Each generated payslip receives a unique serial number (e.g. PS-A3F9-2026-04). This is printed on the payslip footer. Employees can quote this number when presenting payslips to banks, landlords, or visa offices. A full verification endpoint is coming to afrotools.com/verify/.

Are these calculations exact?

Calculations use current published tax bands and contribution rates for each country. For complex situations — multiple income sources, special allowances, dependant reliefs, or employment benefits — actual deductions may differ. Always verify with a licensed payroll professional or your country's revenue authority before using for compliance purposes.

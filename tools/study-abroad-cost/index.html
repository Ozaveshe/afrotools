Study Abroad FAQs for Africans

How much does it cost to study in UK as an African student?
UK university tuition for international students ranges from £10,000–£38,000/year. Living costs are £9,000–£18,000/year (higher in London). Student visa costs £490 plus £776/year NHS surcharge. Total cost over 3 years can be £60,000–£170,000. Chevening and Commonwealth scholarships cover full costs.
Is Germany free for African students?
Germany's public universities charge minimal tuition (€0–500/semester), making it one of the most affordable study destinations globally. Living costs are approximately €10,000–13,000/year. A student visa costs €75. DAAD scholarships provide €800–1,200/month stipend on top. Total annual cost is around €10,500–14,000.
How much does it cost to study in Canada as an international student?
Canadian university tuition ranges from CAD 20,000–45,000/year for international students. Community colleges are more affordable at CAD 8,000–15,000/year. Living costs are CAD 12,000–18,000/year. Students can work 20hrs/week during term and full-time during breaks to offset costs.
What scholarships are available for African students to study abroad?
Major fully-funded scholarships include: Chevening (UK, full scholarship for leadership potential), Commonwealth Scholarship (UK/Commonwealth countries), DAAD (Germany, €800-1200/month stipend), Fulbright (USA, full for master's), Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program, African Development Bank Scholarships and Australia Awards Africa.
What is the cheapest country to study abroad for African students?
Germany is the most affordable with near-free tuition at public universities. Eastern Europe (Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary) offers tuition of $2,000–6,000/year with lower living costs. Turkey, Malaysia and China also offer competitive fees with government scholarship programs. Always factor in living costs, not just tuition.

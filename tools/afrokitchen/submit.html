Before you submit

What we need to review a recipe

Required

  • Recipe name
  • Country of origin
  • Ingredients with quantities or practical measures
  • Cooking steps in order, with timing where you know it

Helpful context

  • Region, community, or family source
  • Occasion, serving style, and what to eat it with
  • Variations, substitutions, and texture or doneness cues
  • Your name and email if you want follow-up questions

Images and license

  • Only share photos you took or have permission to use.
  • Do not send watermarked, copied, or restaurant menu images unless you own the rights.
  • By sharing photo links, you allow AfroKitchen to review, crop, and publish approved images with the recipe.
  • Leave photo links blank if you do not have image rights.

Moderation state

  • Submissions are stored with a pending review status.
  • Editors may normalize quantities, wording, categories, and image slots before publication.
  • Submitting does not create an account and does not guarantee publication.

Submission checklist

Help us preserve the story, not just the steps.

  • Use the required fields first: name, country, ingredients, and steps.
  • Add exact ingredient amounts when possible so the recipe is easier to test and review.
  • Tell us when the dish is usually served: celebrations, weekends, family gatherings, or everyday meals.
  • Include regional variations or substitutions if your household cooks it differently.
  • Only include image links when you have permission for AfroKitchen to review and publish them.

Review flow
  1. Submission is saved with pending status.
  2. We check the country, region, dish name, and serving context.
  3. Ingredients are normalized into quantities, units, pantry notes, and substitutions.
  4. Steps are edited for timing, texture cues, and realistic kitchen flow.
  5. Image links are checked for permission, quality, and fit before any public use.
  6. Approved recipes can be generated into the static AfroKitchen archive after review.

If you only know the recipe informally, that is still useful. Share what you know and we can help shape it into a publishable entry.

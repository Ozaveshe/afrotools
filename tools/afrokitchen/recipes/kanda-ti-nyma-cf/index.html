Kitchen snapshot

Kanda ti Nyma

Kanda ti nyma means meat balls in Sango. In this researched version, the beef meatballs are seasoned with onion, garlic, chile, salt, and egg, then simmered in a peanut and okra sauce until tender. Serve it with gozo, rice, or plantain for a hearty Central African Republic table.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Central African Republic
5 steps 65 min total medium
1
Make the meatballs
Combine beef mince, beaten eggs, finely diced onion, garlic, chile, and salt in a bowl. Mix well with your hands until the mixture holds together. Shape into golf ball-sized meatballs.
The eggs help the meatballs hold while they simmer in the peanut-okra sauce.
2
Brown the meatballs
Heat oil in a large pan and brown the meatballs on all sides, about 6 minutes total. Remove and set aside.
Do not overcrowd the pan — brown in batches for the best crust.
Brown meatballs 06:00
3
Make the sauce
In the same pan, saute the diced onion until soft. Add okra and cook 5 minutes. Stir in the peanut butter and 1 cup of warm water, mixing until smooth.
Cook tomato sauce 10:00
4
Simmer meatballs in sauce
Return the meatballs to the sauce. Cover and simmer gently for 25 minutes, turning the meatballs once halfway through.
Keep the heat low — vigorous boiling will break the meatballs apart.
Simmer meatballs 25:00
5
Serve
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning. Serve the meatballs and sauce over gozo or rice.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

Kitchen snapshot

Chebakia

Chebakia is Morocco's Ramadan pastry jewel. Toasted sesame, almonds, spices, orange blossom water, and honey become folded flower pastries that are fried, soaked, and sprinkled with sesame.

Best served withHarira, mint tea, dates, or sellou.
Best occasionRamadan iftar, Eid trays, and tea tables.

Chef watch-outs

  • Adding all seasoning early and forgetting to adjust at the end.
  • Cooking on heat that is too high once the dish should be steaming or simmering.
How you know it is ready
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.
  • The final texture should match the dish style before you plate it.

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Make the dough

2
Rest and roll
Rest the dough, then roll thin and cut into rectangles for folding.
rest 30:00
3
Shape flowers
Slit and fold each rectangle into a chebakia flower shape.
shape 35:00
4
Fry and honey
Fry until deep golden, soak in warm honey, and sprinkle with sesame.

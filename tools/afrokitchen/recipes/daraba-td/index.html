Kitchen snapshot

Daraba

Daraba is the everyday stew of southern Chad, where the Sara people have cultivated a rich agricultural tradition. The dish combines whatever leafy greens are available — spinach, sorrel, or sweet potato leaves — with okra for body and peanut butter for richness. It is simple, nourishing, and endlessly adaptable, making it the backbone of daily meals.

What to know before you cook

The dish combines whatever leafy greens are available — spinach, sorrel, or sweet potato leaves — with okra for body and peanut butter for richness. How to cook it

Step-by-step instructions

Back to Chad
1
Cook the aromatics
Heat palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the diced onion until soft and golden, about 5 minutes. Add the scotch bonnet and chopped tomatoes, cooking until the tomatoes break down.
Cook onion and tomato 08:00
2
Add peanut butter
Stir the peanut butter into the pot with 2 cups of water, mixing until smooth and well combined. Bring to a gentle simmer.
Dissolve the peanut butter in a little warm water first to prevent clumping.
3
Add okra
Add the sliced okra to the simmering sauce and cook for 10 minutes until the okra softens and thickens the stew.
Cook okra 10:00
4
Add the greens
Add the chopped greens to the pot and stir to combine. Cover and cook for 15 minutes until the greens are wilted and tender.
Do not overcook — the greens should be tender but still vibrant green.
Cook greens 15:00
5
Serve
Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Serve hot over boule or rice.
Daraba thickens as it cools — add a splash of water when reheating.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

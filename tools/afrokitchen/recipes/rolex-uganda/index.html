Kitchen snapshot

Rolex

The Rolex is Uganda's most beloved street food, found on virtually every street corner in Kampala. The name is a playful contraction of "rolled eggs." Born from the creativity of street vendors, this simple wrap has become a national icon and was even considered for UNESCO cultural heritage recognition.

How to cook it

Step-by-step instructions

Back to Uganda
1
Make Dough
Mix flour, salt, oil, and warm water into a soft dough. Knead for 5 minutes until smooth. Rest 15 minutes covered.
2
Roll and Fry Chapati
Divide dough into 2 balls. Roll each into a thin circle. Fry in an oiled pan until golden on both sides. Set aside.
A good chapati should be flaky — brush with oil between layers as you cook.
Fry chapati 05:00
3
Make Omelette
Beat eggs with salt. Pour into a hot oiled pan, adding onions, tomato, cabbage, and pepper on top. Cook until just set.
Cook omelette 03:00
4
Roll
Place the omelette on top of a chapati. Roll tightly into a cylinder, tucking the edges in.
Roll while the omelette is still warm so it molds to the chapati.
5
Serve
Wrap the bottom half in paper or foil for easy handling. Serve immediately while hot.
A true Kampala Rolex is eaten standing at the street vendor's stall.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

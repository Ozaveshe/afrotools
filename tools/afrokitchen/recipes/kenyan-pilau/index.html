Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Kenya
6 steps 25 min prep 75 min cook 100 min total medium
1
Cook beef stock
Simmer beef with half the garlic, half the ginger, salt, pepper, and enough water to cover until nearly tender. Reserve the meat and stock.
Simmer beef 35:00
2
Brown onions
Heat oil in a heavy pot and cook onions slowly until deep golden brown.
Brown onions 20:00
3
Bloom spices
Add remaining garlic and ginger, pilau masala, cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Stir until fragrant.
Bloom spices 01:30
4
Coat rice
Return beef to the pot. Add drained rice and stir gently until each grain is coated in the spice oil.
Coat rice 02:00
5
Steam pilau
Pour in hot stock, adjust salt, bring to a boil, then cover tightly and cook on the lowest heat until the rice absorbs the liquid.
Steam pilau 25:00
6
Rest and fluff
Rest covered off heat, then fluff from the edges inward. Serve with kachumbari.
Rest 10:00

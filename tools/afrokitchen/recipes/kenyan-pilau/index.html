Kenyan Pilau

What to know before you cook

How to cook it

Step-by-step instructions

Back to Kenya
1
Brown Meat
Heat oil in a heavy pot over high heat. Season beef with salt and sear until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
Sear beef 10:00
2
Caramelize Onions
In the same pot, add sliced onions. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until deeply golden brown.
Do not rush — deeply browned onions give pilau its signature color and sweetness.
Caramelize 20:00
3
Bloom Spices
Add garlic, ginger, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, cumin, and pilau masala. Stir 1 minute until fragrant. Return beef to pot.
Bloom spices 01:00
4
Cook Rice
Add drained rice and stir to coat. Pour in hot stock. Bring to a boil, reduce to lowest heat, cover tightly and cook until rice is fluffy and liquid absorbed.
Place a clean towel under the lid to absorb excess steam for fluffier rice.
Cook rice 20:00
5
Rest and Fluff
Remove from heat, rest covered 10 minutes. Fluff gently with a fork.
Rest 10:00
6
Serve
Mound pilau on a platter, garnish with cilantro. Serve with kachumbari and lemon wedges.
Pilau is traditionally served at celebrations — make extra.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

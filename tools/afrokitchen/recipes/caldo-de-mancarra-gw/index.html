Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Caldo de mancarra should feel like a broth that became generous. Keep the peanut smooth, the chicken tender, and the rice ready to drink up the sauce.

What the dish tastes like

Guinea-Bissau peanut broth with chicken, tomato, sweet potato, ginger, chile, and a creamy mancarra sauce for rice.

When to cook it

Best for Everyday meals, with a medium cooking level and about 70 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

White rice, lime, cilantro, or boiled cassava

Regional lane

Guinea-Bissau national table. A verified Guinea-Bissau dish in the AfroKitchen archive.

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

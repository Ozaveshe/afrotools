Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Gabon
4 steps 45 min total easy
1
Fry plantains
Fry plantain slices in oil until golden on both sides. Drain and keep warm.
Fry plantains 07:00
2
Cook the sauce
Pour off excess oil, leaving a little in the pan. Cook onion until soft, then add garlic, tomato, chile, salt, and pepper.
Cook sauce 10:00
3
Add smoked fish
Add smoked fish and water or stock. Simmer gently until the fish flavors the sauce.
Stir gently so the fish stays in flakes.
Simmer fish 05:00
4
Serve
Arrange plantains on a plate and spoon the smoked fish sauce over the top.

Some homes keep the sauce dry and chunky; others loosen it with fish stock and serve it over rice.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Mafé should taste roasted and creamy, not like raw peanut butter. Fry the tomato paste, loosen the peanut paste with warm stock, then simmer patiently.

What the dish tastes like

Senegalese mafé, a peanut and tomato stew with beef, sweet potatoes, carrots, onion, garlic, ginger, and slow-cooked sauce.

When to cook it

Best for Everyday family meals, with a medium cooking level and about 100 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

White rice, fonio, couscous, or steamed millet

Regional lane

Onion, lemon, and peanut table. The sauces and stews that balance sharp onion, citrus, chile, and groundnut richness.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.
Pantry lane

broken rice, dried fish, netetou, peanut paste, tamarind

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Senegal
5 steps 100 min total medium
1
Brown the meat
Heat oil. Season beef and brown on all sides. Remove and set aside.
Brown meat 10:00
2
Build the base
Fry onions until soft. Add tomato paste and cook 3 minutes. Add garlic and ginger.
Fry base 05:00
3
Add peanut butter
Dissolve peanut butter in 3 cups warm water. Pour into pot with browned meat. Simmer.
Dissolve fully to avoid lumps.
4
Add vegetables
Add sweet potatoes, carrots. Simmer until tender and sauce is thick, 35-40 minutes.
Simmer stew 40:00
5
Serve
Ladle the mafe over fluffy white rice and serve hot, with enough sauce to coat the rice without drowning the vegetables.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

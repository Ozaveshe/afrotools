What to know before you cook

Genfo is a thick Ethiopian breakfast porridge shaped with a crater of spiced butter and berbere. It is simple, filling, and deeply warming.

What the dish tastes like

Ethiopian thick barley or wheat porridge served with a well of spiced butter and berbere in the center.

When to cook it

Best for Best for breakfast, postpartum meals, and cold mornings., with a medium cooking level and about 30 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Niter kibbeh, berbere, yogurt, or plain milk.

Regional lane

Chef watch-outs
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
  • Adding all seasoning early and forgetting to adjust at the end.
  • Cooking on heat that is too high once the dish should be steaming or simmering.
How you know it is ready
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.
  • The final texture should match the dish style before you plate it.

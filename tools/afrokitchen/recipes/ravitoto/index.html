Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Ravitoto rewards patience. Cook the cassava leaves until their rough edge softens and the pork becomes part of the sauce.

What the dish tastes like

Malagasy pounded cassava leaves braised slowly with pork, garlic, ginger, and coconut milk until deep and earthy.

When to cook it

Best for everyday meals. Plan on a medium cook and about 160 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Steamed rice and tomato rougail

Regional lane

Madagascar national table. A structured Madagascar dish in the AfroKitchen archive.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Madagascar
6 steps 25 min prep 135 min cook 160 min total medium
1
Brown pork
Heat oil in a heavy pot and brown pork until the fat begins to render.
Brown pork 10:00
2
Cook aromatics
Add onion, garlic, and ginger. Cook until soft, then add tomatoes if using.
Cook aromatics 08:00
3
Add cassava leaves
Stir in pounded cassava leaves and water. Mix well with the pork and aromatics.
4
Braise slowly
Cover and simmer on low, stirring occasionally, until the leaves are very tender and the pork is soft.
Braise ravitoto 105:00
5
Finish coconut
Stir in coconut milk and simmer uncovered until the sauce thickens.
Finish sauce 15:00
6
Serve
Adjust salt and serve hot over white rice with tomato rougail.

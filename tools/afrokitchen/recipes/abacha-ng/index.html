Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Abacha is a bright Igbo cassava salad, chewy and savory with palm oil dressing, ugba, fish, crayfish, onion, garden egg, and bitter utazi accents.

What the dish tastes like

Igbo cassava salad tossed with palm oil dressing, ugba, fish, crayfish, onion, garden egg, and utazi.

When to cook it

Best for snacks, visitors, market-style meals, and festive side plates.. Plan on a medium cook and about 45 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Garden eggs, fried fish, kpomo, onions, and chilled drinks.

Regional lane

South-East and Igbo table. Cassava salads, oha, white soup, bambara nut cakes, palm oil, smoked fish, and cocoyam-thickened soups.

Chef watch-outs
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
  • Adding all seasoning early and forgetting to adjust at the end.
  • Cooking on heat that is too high once the dish should be steaming or simmering.
How you know it is ready
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.
  • The final texture should match the dish style before you plate it.

palm oil, crayfish, iru or ogiri, scotch bonnet, melon seed

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Soften abacha
Soak abacha in warm water until flexible, then rinse and drain well.

2
Make palm oil dressing
Stir palm oil with strained potash water until it thickens and turns bright orange.
emulsify oil 04:00
3
Season the salad
Add crayfish, pepper, salt, ugba, and fish to the dressing, then toss in abacha.
toss 06:00
4
Finish with toppings
Top with onion, garden egg, and utazi. Serve warm or room temperature.
finish 03:00

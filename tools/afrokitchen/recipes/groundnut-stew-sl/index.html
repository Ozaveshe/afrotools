Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Sierra Leone
5 steps 80 min total medium
1
Cook the chicken
Season chicken and boil until tender, about 25 minutes. Reserve stock.
Cook chicken 25:00
2
Fry the base
Heat oil, fry onions until golden. Add blended tomatoes and cook 10 minutes until reduced.
Fry base 10:00
3
Add peanut butter
Dissolve peanut butter in 2 cups chicken stock. Pour into the tomato base and stir well.
4
Simmer the stew
Add chicken pieces and scotch bonnets. Simmer 20 minutes until thick and creamy.
Simmer 20:00
5
Serve
Serve over white rice or with fufu.

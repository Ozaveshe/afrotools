Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Fakoye is a Songhai-style soup from Mali's Niger River corridor, where okra, leafy greens, dried fish, and dawadawa build a deep savory base. It is everyday food, but not plain food: the fermented locust bean brings the earthy umami that makes the soup taste rooted and specific. The okra gives body, the greens keep it fresh, and the dried fish seasons the pot as it simmers. Fakoye is usually served with toh or rice, turning a modest set of ingredients into a filling Sahelian meal.

What the dish tastes like

A tangy okra and greens soup with dried fish and dawadawa (fermented locust beans). Deeply rooted in Malian tradition.

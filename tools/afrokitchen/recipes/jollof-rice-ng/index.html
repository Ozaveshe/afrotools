Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Jollof Rice is the undisputed king of Nigerian party food. No owambe (Yoruba party) is complete without it. The debate over whether Nigerian or Ghanaian Jollof is superior has become one of West Africa's most passionate cultural rivalries.

What the dish tastes like

Nigeria's iconic one-pot rice dish cooked in a smoky tomato and pepper sauce. The rice absorbs layers of flavour from blended tomatoes, scotch bonnets, and a signature party smokiness.

When to cook it

Best for Parties and celebrations, with a medium cooking level and about 90 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Fried plantain, coleslaw, and grilled chicken

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Nigeria
6 steps 90 min total medium
1
Blend the base
Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and one onion until smooth. Pour into a pot and cook on medium-high heat until reduced by half.
Frying down the tomato base is the most important step — it must lose all excess water.
Reduce tomato base 30:00
2
Fry the base
Heat vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Fry sliced onions until golden. Add tomato paste and fry for 2 minutes. Pour in the reduced tomato blend.
Fry base 10:00
3
Season
Add curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and salt. Let the sauce fry until oil floats on top, about 10 minutes.
When the oil separates and sits on top, your base is ready for rice.
Season sauce 10:00
4
Add rice
Pour in the washed rice and stir to coat every grain. Add stock or water to sit about 1cm above the rice. Cover tightly with foil then the lid.
The foil seal traps steam and helps the rice cook evenly.
5
Cook the rice
Cook on low heat for 30 minutes without opening the lid. Stir gently from the bottom, re-cover and cook for another 10-15 minutes until tender.
Cook rice 30:00
6
Create the party smokiness
Increase heat to high for the last 2-3 minutes to create a slight burn at the bottom — this is the prized party jollof smoky flavour.
The bottom crust is the most coveted serving at any party.
Smoke the rice 02:30

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Mahshi is the Egyptian stuffed-vegetable craft that fills tables and kitchens with herbs. Rice, tomato, dill, parsley, cilantro, onion, and spice are tucked into zucchini, peppers, eggplant, cabbage, or grape leaves.

What the dish tastes like

Egyptian vegetables stuffed with herbed tomato rice and simmered in a light tomato broth.

When to cook it

Best for Friday lunches, Ramadan tables, and family gatherings.. Plan on a hard cook and about 110 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Roast chicken, yogurt, salad, or soup.

Regional lane

Egypt national table. A structured Egypt dish in the AfroKitchen archive.

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

1
Make the stuffing

2
Fill the vegetables
Stuff vegetables loosely, leaving room for the rice to expand.
fill 25:00
3
Arrange and broth
Pack vegetables upright in a pot and pour in seasoned broth to come partway up the sides.
arrange 08:00
4
Simmer gently
Cover and simmer on low heat until the vegetables are tender and the rice is cooked.
simmer 45:00

Vegetable choice changes by household. Some versions include minced meat, while many Egyptian home versions keep the stuffing rice-forward and herb-heavy.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Guinea-Bissau is one of the world's largest cashew producers, and cashews feature prominently in the cuisine. This rice dish is a simple yet flavourful showcase of the country's prized nut.

What the dish tastes like

Fragrant rice cooked with roasted cashew nuts, onions, and aromatic spices. A celebration of Guinea-Bissau's status as a major cashew producer.

When to cook it

Best for Everyday side dish, with a easy cooking level and about 45 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

How to cook it

Step-by-step instructions

Back to Guinea-Bissau
1
Toast the cashews
Dry-toast cashew nuts in a pan over medium heat until golden and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Set aside.
Watch carefully — they can burn quickly.
Toast cashews 05:00
2
Cook the rice base
Melt butter, fry onion until golden. Add rice and stir to coat. Pour in water or stock, season with salt.
3
Cook
Bring to a boil, cover, reduce to low heat. Cook 18-20 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
Cook rice 20:00
4
Finish
Fluff rice with a fork, fold in toasted cashews. Serve as a side dish.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Fisherman soup is coastal Nigerian comfort, built around fresh seafood and a spicy broth. Fish, prawns, periwinkles, crayfish, and aromatic leaves make it taste like the river and the kitchen met in one pot.

What the dish tastes like

South-South Nigerian seafood soup with fresh fish, prawns, periwinkles, crayfish, pepper, yam thickener, and aromatic leaves.

When to cook it

Best for Best for seafood lovers, Sunday lunch, and swallow meals by the tableful., with a medium cooking level and about 75 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Fufu, eba, pounded yam, boiled plantain, or rice.

Regional lane

South-South and Delta table. Riverine soups, palm nut richness, seafood, afang, edikang ikong, and deeply seasoned broths.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Nigeria
4 steps 75 min total medium
1
Make thickener
Boil yam or cocoyam until soft, then pound into a smooth paste.
thickener 20:00
2
Season the broth
Simmer stock with onion, crayfish, pepper, and salt until flavorful.
broth 10:00
3
Cook the fish
Add fish steaks and simmer gently until almost cooked.
fish 10:00
4
Finish seafood
Add prawns, periwinkles if using, thickener, and herbs. Simmer until the soup thickens and seafood is just cooked.
seafood finish 10:00

Some versions use yam or cocoyam as thickener, some add okra, and seafood changes with what is fresh.

What to know before you cook

Dambou works best when the grains stay separate. Steam, break the clumps, then fold in the greens and seasoning while everything is warm.

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Niger
5 steps 60 min total medium
1
Form grains
Sprinkle water into the millet flour or couscous while rubbing with your fingers to form tiny grains.
2
Steam first
Steam the grains in a lined steamer until firm and fluffy.
Steam grains 20:00
3
Cook greens
Blanch or steam moringa leaves until tender, then squeeze lightly if very wet.
Cook greens 10:00
4
Mix and finish
Break up the steamed grains and fold in moringa, onion, pepper, oil, crushed peanuts, and salt.
5
Warm through
Return to the steamer briefly so the flavors settle and the texture stays fluffy.
Finish steam 10:00

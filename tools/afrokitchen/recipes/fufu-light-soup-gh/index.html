Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Fufu and light soup is a Ghanaian classic built on contrast: elastic swallow, thin peppery soup, and deeply simmered meat or fish.

What the dish tastes like

Ghanaian cassava-plantain fufu served with peppery tomato-based light soup and goat, chicken, or fish.

When to cook it

Best for Best for Sunday lunch, celebratory family meals, and cold-weather soup days., with a hard cooking level and about 115 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Goat light soup, chicken light soup, or fish light soup.

Regional lane

Soups, stews, and swallow table. Groundnut, greens, fish, palm oil, and fufu-friendly bowls.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

shito, palm oil, sorghum leaves, fermented corn dough, groundnut

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

4 steps 115 min total hard
1
Simmer meat
Cook meat with onion, ginger, garlic, salt, and water until it begins to soften.
meat simmer 35:00
2
Blend soup base
Simmer tomatoes and peppers in the pot, lift them out, blend with onion, then return to the soup.
blend base 15:00
3
Finish light soup
Continue simmering until meat is tender and the broth is peppery but still light.
soup finish 25:00
4
Make fufu
Boil cassava and plantain until tender, then pound or process into a smooth elastic mound.
fufu 30:00

Fufu may use cassava with plantain, yam, or cocoyam, and light soup can be goat, chicken, fish, or mixed meat.

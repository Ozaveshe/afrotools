Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Tigadegena is all about a smooth peanut sauce. Loosen the paste first, simmer low, and stir so the groundnuts do not catch.

What the dish tastes like

Malian peanut stew with lamb, beef, or chicken simmered in tomato, onion, garlic, groundnut paste, and vegetables.

When to cook it

Best for Daily meals and family gatherings, with a medium cooking level and about 85 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

White rice, toh, fonio, couscous, or steamed millet

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

5 steps 85 min total medium
1
Brown the lamb
Season lamb with salt. Heat oil and brown on all sides. Remove and reserve.
Brown meat 08:00
2
Fry the aromatics
Add onions and fry until softened. Stir in tomato paste and cook 3 minutes.
Fry onions 05:00
3
Create peanut sauce
Dissolve peanut paste in 3 cups warm water. Pour into pot with browned lamb. Simmer.
4
Cook the stew
Add potatoes and eggplant. Cover and simmer 40 minutes until meat is fork-tender and sauce thickened.
Simmer stew 40:00
5
Serve
Serve over white rice or alongside toh.

Beef, chicken, goat, or vegetables can be used. Some cooks add cabbage, sweet potato, or chilli for a fuller family-style stew.

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Mauritania
6 steps 110 min total hard
1
Season fish
Pound parsley, garlic, chile, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Rub it into the scored fish and rest briefly.
Season fish 15:00
2
Brown fish
Heat oil in a wide pot and brown the fish on both sides. Remove it before it flakes apart.
Brown fish 10:00
3
Build broth
Cook onions, tomato paste, and chopped tomatoes until the paste darkens. Add water or fish stock.
Cook tomato base 10:00
4
Cook vegetables
Add carrots, cabbage, eggplant, okra, and sweet potato or cassava if using. Simmer until just tender, then lift out with the fish.
Simmer vegetables 25:00
5
Cook rice
Stir rinsed rice into the seasoned broth. Cover and cook low until the rice absorbs the sauce.
Cook rice 30:00
6
Assemble platter
Mound rice on a platter and arrange fish and vegetables over the top. Serve with chile paste or lemon.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

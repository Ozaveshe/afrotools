Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The bread or batter should smell pleasantly fermented, toasted, or nutty rather than floury.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

5 steps 125 min total medium
1
Cook meat
Season meat with onion, salt, and pepper. Simmer until tender and reserve the stock.
Cook meat 40:00
2
Start leaves
Simmer cassava leaves in a little water until they soften and lose their raw edge.
Soften leaves 30:00
3
Build stew
Heat palm oil and cook onion, garlic, chile, and ogiri if using. Add leaves, meat, smoked fish, and stock.
Build stew 10:00
4
Add groundnut
Stir in ground peanuts or peanut butter and simmer until the stew thickens and oil rises.
Simmer stew 30:00
5
Serve
Adjust salt and serve over white rice.

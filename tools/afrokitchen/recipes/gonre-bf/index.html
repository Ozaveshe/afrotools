Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Gonre is a Mossi bean dish with two moods: smooth in the dry season and leaf-rich when bean leaves are available.

What the dish tastes like

Burkinabe steamed bean cakes made from cowpea paste, peanut, leafy greens, and maize couscous.

When to cook it

Best for Best for breakfast markets, snack plates, and ceremonial family cooking., with a medium cooking level and about 90 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Tomato sauce, chili oil, or a light onion relish.

Regional lane

Burkina Faso national table. A verified Burkina Faso dish in the AfroKitchen archive.

Chef watch-outs
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
  • Adding all seasoning early and forgetting to adjust at the end.
How you know it is ready
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

Step-by-step method

Blend the beans

Season and rest
Fold in peanut powder, greens, maize couscous, oil, and salt. Let the mixture hydrate.
rest 20:00
3
Steam cakes
Spoon into oiled cups or leaves and steam until firm through the center.
steam 35:00
4
Serve warm
Unmold and serve with tomato sauce or chili oil.
finish 03:00

Kitchen snapshot

Gonre

Gonre is a Mossi bean dish with two moods: smooth in the dry season and leaf-rich when bean leaves are available.

Best served withTomato sauce, chili oil, or a light onion relish.
Best occasionbreakfast markets, snack plates, and ceremonial family cooking.

When to cook it

Best for breakfast markets, snack plates, and ceremonial family cooking.. Plan on a medium cook and about 90 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Tomato sauce, chili oil, or a light onion relish.

Regional lane

Chef watch-outs
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
  • Adding all seasoning early and forgetting to adjust at the end.
How you know it is ready
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Burkina Faso
4 steps 45 min prep 45 min cook 90 min total medium
1
Blend the beans
Blend peeled beans with onion, garlic, chili, and just enough water to make a thick paste.
blend 08:00
2
Season and rest
Fold in peanut powder, greens, maize couscous, oil, and salt. Let the mixture hydrate.
rest 20:00
3
Steam cakes
Spoon into oiled cups or leaves and steam until firm through the center.
steam 35:00
4
Serve warm
Unmold and serve with tomato sauce or chili oil.
finish 03:00

Leafy versions include bean leaves or greens; simpler versions are mostly cowpea paste and seasoning.

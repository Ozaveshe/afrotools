Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Palm butter soup should be deep orange, glossy, and rich. Give the palm base time to simmer until the oil rises and the smoke from the fish seasons the whole pot.

What the dish tastes like

Liberian palm butter soup with palm fruit concentrate, beef or goat, smoked fish, dried shrimp, chile, okra, and rice.

When to cook it

Best for Sunday rice meals, with a medium cooking level and about 95 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

White rice, fufu, dumboy, or boiled cassava

Regional lane

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Liberia
5 steps 95 min total medium
1
Cook meat
Season meat with salt, pepper, onion, and garlic. Simmer with water until nearly tender, then reserve the stock.
Cook meat 30:00
2
Start palm base
Whisk palm fruit concentrate with stock in a large pot and bring to a simmer.
3
Add proteins
Add cooked meat, smoked fish, dried shrimp if using, onions, garlic, and peppers.
4
Simmer glossy
Simmer until the soup thickens and red oil rises to the surface. Stir occasionally so it does not catch.
Simmer soup 40:00
5
Finish vegetables
Add okra or bitterball if using and cook until tender. Adjust salt and serve over rice.
Finish 10:00

Fresh palm nuts make the deepest flavor, but palm fruit concentrate is the practical home shortcut.

Kitchen snapshot

Dobara Biskra

Dobara is the fiery bean bowl associated with Biskra, where tender chickpeas and fava beans are seasoned at the end with raw garlic, cumin, lemon, chili, oil, and herbs. It is cheap, filling, and built for bread.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

4 steps 110 min total easy
1
Cook the beans
Simmer soaked chickpeas and fava beans in water until very tender.
bean simmer 75:00
2
Thicken the broth
Mash a cup of beans against the pot and stir them back in to make the stew creamy.
thicken 05:00
3
Season boldly
Stir in garlic, harissa, cumin, olive oil, lemon juice, and salt while the beans are hot.
season 07:00
4
Rest and serve
Let the stew stand briefly, then finish with cilantro and more chili if desired.
rest 05:00

Some versions use only chickpeas, while Biskra-style bowls often mix chickpeas and fava beans with a generous cumin and chili finish.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Rfissa is one of Morocco's great communal dishes: shredded msemen or trid under saffron-scented chicken, lentils, fenugreek, onions, and broth.

What the dish tastes like

Moroccan chicken, lentil, and fenugreek stew served over shredded msemen or trid pastry.

When to cook it

Best for Best for family gatherings, postpartum meals, and special lunches., with a hard cooking level and about 145 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Msemen, trid pastry, or other thin flatbread.

Regional lane

Tagines and special mains. Slow-cooked, spiced, sweet-savory, and celebratory dishes that anchor the table.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the rest or fermentation period.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The bread or batter should smell pleasantly fermented, toasted, or nutty rather than floury.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Season chicken
Season chicken with ginger, turmeric, saffron if using, salt, and pepper.

Start stew
Cook onions in oil, add chicken, and brown lightly.
onion chicken 15:00
3
Simmer lentils
Add lentils, soaked fenugreek, and enough water to cover. Simmer until chicken and lentils are tender.
long simmer 60:00
4
Serve over bread
Pile shredded msemen on a platter and spoon chicken, lentils, and broth over it.
serve 05:00

Some versions use ras el hanout, smen, or extra fenugreek, and the bread base may be homemade or store-bought msemen.

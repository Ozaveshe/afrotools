Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Nyekoe is patient mountain food. Soak the beans, simmer the sorghum, and add pumpkin late so it softens without disappearing completely.

What the dish tastes like

Basotho bean, sorghum, and pumpkin stew simmered slowly until thick, earthy, and gently sweet.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Lesotho
5 steps 630 min total medium
1
Soak beans
Cover beans with plenty of water and soak overnight. Drain before cooking.
Soak beans 480:00
2
Start grains
Combine beans, sorghum, onion, oil, and water in a heavy pot. Bring to a boil.
3
Simmer slowly
Reduce heat and simmer until the beans and sorghum are nearly tender, adding water as needed.
Simmer grains 90:00
4
Add pumpkin
Stir in pumpkin and continue cooking until it is soft and the stew is thick.
Cook pumpkin 30:00
5
Season and mash
Season with salt and pepper. Mash a small portion against the pot to make the stew creamy.

Some homes use samp or cracked maize in place of sorghum; pumpkin or potatoes are common additions for sweetness and body.

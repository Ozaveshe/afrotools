Mukimo

Mukimo is a comforting Kenyan mash associated especially with central Kenya. Potatoes give it body, maize brings chew, peas or beans add sweetness, and pumpkin leaves, spinach, or amaranth give the mash its green lift.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Mukimo is a comforting Kenyan mash associated especially with central Kenya. Potatoes give it body, maize brings chew, peas or beans add sweetness, and pumpkin leaves, spinach, or amaranth give the mash its green lift.

What the dish tastes like

A Kenyan mashed potato dish with maize, peas or beans, and green vegetables, often served with stew or nyama choma.

When to cook it

Best for Best for family meals, ceremonies, roast meat plates, and stew dinners., with a easy cooking level and about 60 minutes total.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Overcooking the greens until the color and texture collapse.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • Greens should be cooked through but still look alive and glossy.
  • The surface should be deeply colored while the center stays juicy.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

4 steps 60 min total easy
1
Boil potatoes and peas
Boil potatoes and peas in salted water until the potatoes are tender.
Boil 25:00
2
Cook the greens
Add chopped pumpkin leaves or spinach and cook until tender and bright green.
Cook greens 05:00
3
Mash
Drain excess water, add boiled maize, then mash until combined. Leave some maize kernels whole for texture.
Mash 10:00
4
Finish
If using onion, fry it in butter or oil and fold into the mash. Taste for salt and serve hot.
Finish 05:00

Some versions use peas, others beans. Pumpkin leaves are traditional, while spinach is a practical substitute.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Moambe Chicken belongs to the Congo Basin table. The sauce should be deep orange, rich from palm nut cream, and thick enough to cling to chicken before it meets fufu, rice, or plantain.

What the dish tastes like

Tender chicken simmered in palm nut sauce with tomato, onion, garlic, chile, and optional okra.

When to cook it

Best for Sunday meals, celebrations, family gatherings. Plan on a medium cook and about 85 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Fufu, boiled plantains, or white rice

Regional lane

Democratic Republic of Congo national table. A structured Democratic Republic of Congo dish in the AfroKitchen archive.

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

5 steps 25 min prep 60 min cook 85 min total medium
1
Brown the chicken
Season chicken with salt and black pepper. Heat oil and brown the pieces on all sides, then remove them from the pot.
Brown 10:00
2
Cook the aromatics
Cook onions in the same pot until soft. Add garlic and tomato paste and stir until fragrant.
Aromatics 07:00
3
Build the sauce
Add tomatoes, palm nut cream, water, and okra if using. Stir until smooth.
Sauce 05:00
4
Simmer
Return chicken to the pot, add whole scotch bonnets, cover, and simmer until the chicken is tender and the sauce is thick.
Simmer 40:00
5
Serve
Remove the whole peppers, adjust seasoning, and serve with fufu, rice, or boiled plantains.

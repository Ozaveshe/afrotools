Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Benachin means one pot, and the pot should taste complete before the rice goes in. Fry the fish, build a tomato broth, cook the vegetables, then let the rice absorb everything.

What the dish tastes like

Gambian one-pot tomato rice cooked with fish, cassava, sweet potato, cabbage, carrot, chile, and a rich seasoned broth.

When to cook it

Best for Family meals, with a medium cooking level and about 90 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Lime wedges, fresh chile sauce, cucumber salad, or tapalapa bread

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Gambia
5 steps 90 min total medium
1
Season and fry fish
Season fish with salt and pepper. Fry in hot oil until browned on both sides, then remove.
Fry fish 10:00
2
Build tomato broth
In the same pot, cook onions until soft. Add garlic and tomato paste, then tomatoes, hot pepper, and water or stock.
Build broth 12:00
3
Cook vegetables
Add cassava, sweet potato, carrot, and cabbage. Simmer until just tender, then lift the vegetables out and keep warm.
Cook vegetables 15:00
4
Cook rice
Stir rice into the broth. Place fish and vegetables on top, cover tightly, and cook on low until the rice absorbs the broth.
Steam rice 30:00
5
Serve family style
Rest 10 minutes, then serve the rice on a platter with fish and vegetables arranged over it. Add lime if using.
Rest 10:00

Some cooks use fish, others use chicken or meat. Vegetables change with the market, but cassava, cabbage, carrot, and sweet potato are common companions.

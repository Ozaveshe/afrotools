Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Groundnut Soup, often called nkate nkwan, is a Ghanaian comfort dish built around roasted groundnuts, tomatoes, aromatics, and slow simmering. It is especially associated with Akan home cooking, where the soup is prepared for family tables, Sunday meals, and guests who expect something warm and generous. The flavor should be nutty and rounded rather than sugary: the groundnut paste enriches the broth while tomato and chilli keep it lively. In many homes the soup is served with fufu and eaten communally, which is why texture matters as much as seasoning.

What the dish tastes like

A rich, creamy soup from roasted groundnuts simmered with tomatoes and chilli. The natural peanut oils create a velvety broth.

Recipe overview

What to know before you cook

Odika, made from roasted wild mango kernels, gives Gabonese sauces a deep brown color and nutty, slightly smoky body. Chicken cooked in odika is a quiet showstopper.

What the dish tastes like

Gabonese chicken simmered in odika, the roasted wild mango seed sauce sometimes called African chocolate.

When to cook it

Best for Best for Sunday lunch, guest meals, and Gabonese comfort food., with a medium cooking level and about 85 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Rice, boiled plantain, cassava, or baton de manioc.

Chef watch-outs
  • Stopping the base before the pepper, onion, or spice edge has mellowed.
  • Thinning the pot before the body of the soup or stew has developed.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The sauce should coat the spoon and taste rounded, not watery or raw.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

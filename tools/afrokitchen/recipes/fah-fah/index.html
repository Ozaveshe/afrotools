Kitchen snapshot

Fah-Fah

Fah-Fah is a hearty goat bone soup that Djiboutians turn to for warmth, nourishment, and celebration. It is traditionally served to welcome guests, nourish new mothers, and break the Ramadan fast. The slow-simmered broth extracts every bit of flavor from the bones, creating a deeply satisfying soup.

How to cook it

Step-by-step instructions

Back to Djibouti
1
Start Broth
Place goat pieces in a large pot with water. Bring to a boil, skimming any foam that rises to the surface.
Skimming the foam ensures a clear, clean-tasting broth.
Boil 10:00
2
Add Aromatics
Add onions, garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and whole chili. Reduce heat and simmer until meat is very tender.
The longer you simmer, the richer the broth — at least an hour for best results.
Simmer 60:00
3
Add Vegetables
Add carrots and potatoes. Continue simmering until vegetables are tender.
Cook vegetables 20:00
4
Season
Remove the whole chili. Season with salt. The broth should be richly flavored.
5
Serve
Ladle into deep bowls with meat and vegetables. Garnish with cilantro and a lime wedge. Serve with bread for dipping.
Fah-Fah is traditionally served in large communal bowls for sharing.

Every household has small variations. Start here, then adjust seasoning, heat, and serving sides to your kitchen.

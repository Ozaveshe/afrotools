What to know before you cook

Skoudehkaris should smell like the port cities of the Horn: lamb, rice, tomato, cardamom, cumin, cinnamon, and a little chile all settling into one pot.

What the dish tastes like

Djibouti's national dish — spiced rice with lamb, caramelized onions, cardamom, and cumin, a one-pot feast.

When to cook it

Best for everyday meals. Plan on a medium cook and about 80 minutes total.

What to serve alongside it

Banana, salad, yogurt, or chile sauce

Chef watch-outs
  • Adding too much liquid after the rice goes in.
  • Stirring too often once the grains should be steaming.
  • Rushing the base before the raw edge has cooked out.
How you know it is ready
  • The grains should be tender but still distinct, with steam carrying the seasoning upward.
  • The aroma should smell rounded rather than raw or sharp.
  • Oil, sauce, broth, or steam should look settled and deliberate.

How to cook it

Step-by-step method

Keep the rhythm calm, watch the texture, and adjust seasoning at the end.

Back to Djibouti
5 steps 20 min prep 60 min cook 80 min total medium
1
Brown the lamb
Season lamb with salt and black pepper. Brown it in hot oil until the edges take color.
Brown 10:00
2
Cook the tomato base
Add onions and cook until soft. Stir in garlic, tomatoes, tomato paste, cardamom, cumin, cinnamon, turmeric, and chile.
Tomato base 12:00
3
Simmer the meat
Add water or stock, cover, and simmer until the lamb is almost tender.
Simmer 30:00
4
Steam the rice
Add drained rice to the pot. Cover tightly and cook on low until the rice absorbs the broth.
Rice 20:00
5
Rest and finish
Rest covered, fluff gently, scatter cilantro over the top, and serve with banana or salad.
Rest 10:00

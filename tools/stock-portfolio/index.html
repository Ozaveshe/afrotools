Frequently Asked Questions

How do I read African stock prices?
NSE Nigeria quotes stocks in Naira (kobo for fractions). JSE quotes in South African cents (1 Rand = 100 cents). GSE Ghana and NSE Kenya quote in local currency units. Most African exchanges publish prices on their websites daily and many mobile apps (like Robinhood-equivalents) provide real-time NSE and JSE quotes.
How can I buy stocks on NSE, JSE, or GSE?
You need a stockbroker or licensed investment platform. In Nigeria: Stanbic IBTC, Meristem, Chaka, Trove. In South Africa: Easy Equities, Investec, Standard Bank Online Share Trading. In Kenya: Dyer Blair, Standard Investment Bank. In Ghana: Databank Brokerage, IC Securities. Online platforms are making access much easier.
Are African stocks a good investment?
African stock markets offer opportunities for growth but come with higher volatility and currency risk. The JSE (South Africa) is the most liquid and internationally accessible. The NSE Nigeria offers some of the highest potential returns but also more volatility. Diversification across sectors and countries is advisable.

PDF Merge & Split: How It Works

The AfroTools PDF Merge and Split tool lets you combine multiple PDF files into a single document or split a multi-page PDF into separate files. To merge, simply drag and drop your PDF files into the upload area and arrange them in the desired order — drag to reorder, then click merge to produce a single combined PDF. This is ideal for assembling application packs, combining scanned pages, or creating compiled reports from separate documents. To split, upload a multi-page PDF and the tool separates it into individual single-page files that you can download separately. The drag-and-drop reordering makes it intuitive to arrange documents in exactly the order you need before merging. All processing happens entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript — your documents are never uploaded to any server. This makes the tool safe for sensitive business documents, legal contracts, financial statements, and personal records. No account or software installation is required. Just open the page, upload your PDFs, and get your merged or split files in seconds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many PDFs can I merge at once?

You can merge as many PDFs as your browser can handle. For typical document sizes, combining 10-20 files works smoothly. Very large files may take a moment to process depending on your device.

Can I reorder PDFs before merging?

Yes. After uploading your files, drag and drop them into any order you like. The merged PDF will follow the exact sequence you arrange on screen.

How does splitting work?

Upload a multi-page PDF and the tool extracts each page as a separate PDF file. You can then download individual pages or select specific pages you need.

Are my PDFs uploaded to a server?

No. All merging and splitting operations happen entirely in your browser. Your documents stay on your device and are never transmitted to any external server.

Does merging reduce the quality of my PDFs?

No. The merge process combines the original PDF pages without recompressing or altering their content. The output quality is identical to the source files.

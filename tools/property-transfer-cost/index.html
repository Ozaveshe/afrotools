❓ Common Questions

Who pays transfer costs — buyer or seller? +
Transfer duty and legal fees are typically paid by the buyer. Agent commission is usually paid by the seller, but this can be negotiated in private sales. In Nigeria, both parties often pay their own agent fees separately.
When are transfer costs paid? +
Transfer costs are typically paid at the time of transfer registration, not at signing. In SA, the conveyancer (attorney) collects all costs upfront before lodging at the Deeds Office. In Kenya and Nigeria, costs are paid during the conveyancing process.
Can transfer costs be financed with the mortgage? +
In South Africa, some banks will add transfer costs to the bond for qualifying buyers. In most other African countries, transfer costs must be paid from your own funds. Budget for 8–15% of the property price in cash beyond your deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What costs are included in the calculation?

The calculator includes stamp duty, transfer duty, legal/conveyancing fees, estate agent commission, registration fees, and any applicable VAT. The specific fees vary by country and are based on each country's property transfer regulations.

Which African countries are supported?

The tool supports Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Ghana, and additional African markets. Each country has its own fee structures, duty rates, and tax brackets accurately modelled in the calculator.

How much do transfer costs typically add to the property price?

Transfer costs in African markets typically range from 5-15% of the purchase price, depending on the country and property value. This includes all taxes, legal fees, and commissions involved in the transaction.

Who pays the transfer costs — buyer or seller?

This varies by country and is often negotiable. In South Africa, the buyer typically pays transfer duty and conveyancing fees. In Nigeria, costs are often split. The AI advisor can provide country-specific guidance on the buyer/seller cost split.

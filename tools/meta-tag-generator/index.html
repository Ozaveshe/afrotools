SEO Tips

Title: 50-60 characters. Include primary keyword near the start. Add brand name with a separator (| or -).

Description: 150-160 characters. Include a call to action. Use the primary keyword naturally.

OG Image: 1200x630px recommended. This is what appears when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp.

Canonical URL: Always set to prevent duplicate content issues. Use the preferred URL format.

Why Meta Tags Matter for SEO

Meta tags are HTML elements that provide information about your web page to search engines and social media platforms. While many metadata fields do not directly determine rankings, titles and descriptions shape how people understand a result before clicking. Good snippets make the page purpose, location, offer, and brand clear.

Essential Meta Tags Every Page Needs

  • Title tag: The most important on-page SEO element. Appears in search results, browser tabs, and social shares.
  • Meta description: The snippet shown below your title in search results. Not a ranking factor but crucial for CTR.
  • Canonical URL: Tells search engines the preferred URL for a page, preventing duplicate content issues.
  • Open Graph tags: Control how your page appears when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and other platforms.
  • Twitter Card tags: Control the appearance of shared links on Twitter/X.
  • Robots meta: Instructs search engines whether to index and follow links on the page.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal title tag length?
Google displays approximately 50-60 characters. Titles longer than 60 characters may be truncated. Place your most important keywords at the beginning and your brand name at the end, separated by | or -.
Does meta description affect rankings?
Not directly. Google has said meta descriptions are not a ranking factor. A clear description can still improve how useful the search snippet is for people deciding whether to click.
What size should OG images be?
The recommended size is 1200x630 pixels (1.91:1 ratio). This works well on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and Twitter. Use JPEG or PNG format. Keep file size under 1MB for fast loading.

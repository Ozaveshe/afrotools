PDF Page Numbers: How It Works

The AfroTools PDF Page Numbers tool adds professional page numbering to any PDF document. Upload your PDF and customise the numbering format, position, font size, and style. Choose from multiple numbering formats including standard numbers (1, 2, 3), Roman numerals (i, ii, iii), and custom prefixes like "Page 1 of 10." Position the page numbers at the top or bottom of each page, aligned to the left, centre, or right. Adjust the font size and starting number to match your document requirements — for example, start numbering from page 3 if your document has a cover page and table of contents. The tool processes each page of your PDF and overlays the page numbers without altering the original content, then produces a new PDF ready for download. This is essential for academic papers, legal documents, business reports, book manuscripts, and any multi-page document that needs organised pagination. All processing is client-side, so your documents remain private and never leave your browser. No software installation or account is needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I choose where the page numbers appear?

Yes. You can position page numbers at the top or bottom of each page, with left, centre, or right alignment. This lets you match the formatting conventions of your document type.

Can I start numbering from a specific page?

Yes. Set a custom starting number so you can skip cover pages, title pages, or table of contents sections. For example, start numbering from "1" on the third page of your document.

What numbering formats are supported?

Options include standard Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3), Roman numerals (i, ii, iii), and custom formats with prefixes or "Page X of Y" style numbering for professional documents.

Does adding page numbers change the PDF content?

No. The page numbers are overlaid onto the existing pages without modifying the original content. Your text, images, and formatting remain exactly as they were in the source PDF.

Is my document kept private?

Absolutely. All processing occurs in your browser. Your PDF is never uploaded to any server, making it safe for confidential legal documents, financial reports, and sensitive business files.

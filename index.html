How Nigerian PAYE Tax Works (2026)

1

Calculate Gross Income

Start with your total monthly salary including all allowances (basic + housing + transport + others).

2

Subtract Statutory Deductions

Pension (8% minimum) and NHF (2.5% of basic) are deducted BEFORE calculating tax. These reduce your taxable income.

3

Apply Rent Relief (NEW!)

If you pay rent, you get relief equal to 20% of your annual rent (max ₦500,000/year). This further reduces taxable income.

4

Calculate PAYE Using Progressive Rates

The first ₦800,000 of your annual chargeable income is tax-free. Income above this is taxed at progressively higher rates (15%, 18%, 21%, 25%).

5

Get Your Net Salary

Net Salary = Gross Income - Pension - NHF - PAYE Tax. This is what hits your bank account monthly.

Example: If you earn ₦500,000/month (₦6M/year):

• Gross: ₦6,000,000
• Pension (8%): -₦480,000
• NHF (2.5% of basic): -₦90,000
• Chargeable Income: ₦5,430,000
• First ₦800k: ₦0 tax (0%)
• Next ₦4.63M: ₦746,400 tax
• Monthly PAYE: ₦62,200
• Net Salary: ₦390,300/month

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the new 2026 tax system

Is the first ₦800,000 really tax-free?

+

Yes! Under the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 (effective January 1, 2026), the first ₦800,000 of your chargeable income is taxed at 0%. Note that "chargeable income" is calculated AFTER subtracting pension, NHF, and rent relief from your gross salary.

Example: If you earn ₦1M gross but have ₦150k deductions, your chargeable income is ₦850k. The first ₦800k is tax-free, and you only pay tax on the remaining ₦50k.

What is Rent Relief and how do I claim it?

+

Rent relief is a NEW tax benefit in 2026. You can deduct 20% of your actual annual rent paid (maximum ₦500,000/year) from your taxable income.

How to claim: Provide your employer with: (1) Valid lease agreement, (2) Rent payment receipts, (3) Bank statements showing payments. Your HR will process this and reduce your monthly PAYE deductions.

Example: If you pay ₦1.2M/year in rent, your relief is 20% × ₦1.2M = ₦240,000/year (₦20,000/month reduction in taxable income).

Will I pay more or less tax in 2026?

+

Most workers will pay LESS tax in 2026. Specifically:

✅ Earning ₦800k/year or less: You'll pay ZERO tax (vs ~₦40k in 2025)
✅ Earning ₦1.5M - ₦25M/year: You'll pay 10-30% less tax
⚠️ Earning above ₦50M/year: You may pay slightly more due to the 25% top rate

Use the "2025 vs 2026" tab in this calculator to see your specific difference!

When does the new tax system start?

+

The Nigeria Tax Act 2025 became effective on January 1, 2026. Your January 2026 salary (paid in February) will be the first to use the new tax rates.

If you were paid in January 2026 for December 2025 work, the old 2025 rates still apply to that payment.

Is this calculator accurate?

+

Yes! This calculator uses the official tax rates from the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 as published by the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS).

However, your actual take-home may differ slightly due to: (1) Additional employer-specific deductions (unions, loans, etc.), (2) Rounding differences in payroll software, (3) State-specific taxes in some cases.

For the most accurate figure, always verify with your HR department.

What's the difference between gross salary and net salary?

+

Gross Salary is your total salary before any deductions. This is the number on your offer letter.

Net Salary (Take-Home Pay) is what actually hits your bank account after all deductions: PAYE tax, pension, NHF, and any other deductions.

Formula: Net Salary = Gross Salary - PAYE - Pension - NHF - Other Deductions

Can I increase my pension contribution to reduce tax?

+

Yes! This is a smart tax strategy. The minimum pension contribution is 8%, but you can voluntarily contribute up to 20% of your salary. Higher pension contributions reduce your taxable income, which lowers your PAYE tax.

Example: If you earn ₦1M/month and increase pension from 8% (₦80k) to 15% (₦150k), you reduce your taxable income by ₦70k/month = ₦840k/year. This could save you ₦126k-210k in annual tax!

Bonus: The extra pension is still YOUR money, growing for retirement.

Do I need to file a tax return?

+

If you're a salaried employee only (no side business), your employer handles everything through PAYE. You don't need to file a separate return.

You MUST file if you have: (1) Side business or freelance income, (2) Rental income, (3) Multiple employments, (4) Capital gains, (5) Foreign income.

Filing deadline is March 31 of the following year (e.g., March 31, 2027 for 2026 income).

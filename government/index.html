Operating Standard

How this hub should stay current

Government data is brittle: portals move, fees change, agencies publish PDF circulars, and some services require login. The update workflow tracks source availability and content fingerprints, then flags the tool owner to verify facts before changing public guidance.

  1. Source first: watch the official portal, regulator, treasury, civil registry, electoral body, or ministry page.
  2. Change detected: compare HTTP status, title, content hash, ETag, and last-modified metadata against the previous run.
  3. Human review: update calculator data only after confirming the changed official text, fee table, or downloadable document.
  4. Publish proof: rerun the source checker and link checks before calling the hub current.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these official government services?
No. AfroTools is an independent information platform. Use it to prepare, estimate, and understand your route. Always confirm final fees, forms, deadlines, and eligibility on the official government website before paying or submitting personal documents.
What changed on this hub?
The page now shows 15 linked civic workflows instead of claiming 20. It also adds the missing Work Permit and Kenya DPA tools, groups tools by user job, and exposes a source-monitoring workflow.
How often should the sources be checked?
High-risk lanes such as passports, IDs, work permits, welfare, budgets, and public holidays should be checked weekly or after a known government announcement. Lower-risk explainer pages can be reviewed monthly.
Why not automatically change fees whenever a page changes?
Many official pages use login gates, PDFs, circulars, or temporary notices. The automation detects changes and broken sources, but a reviewer still confirms the exact public-facing rule before editing a calculator or guide.

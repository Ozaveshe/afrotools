Calculation methodology

The shared local engine adds or extracts the Article 18 standard 19% rate and rounds to whole XOF. The 10% treatment requires an exact Annex I match and the 0% treatment requires export evidence. Exemptions remain separate and no custom, withholding, registration or filing decision is inferred.

Known limitations

  • Informational estimate only. It is not professional tax, legal, payroll, or filing advice.
  • Annex I, export and exemption classification require transaction-level legal and invoice evidence.
  • Sector-specific, regional, treaty, relief, and special-regime rules may not be fully modeled.
  • Confirm filing, registration, and remittance duties with the official authority or a qualified adviser before submission.

Test-case examples

Input: Net amount: XOF 100,000 at the standard 19% rate.
Expected: VAT is XOF 19,000 and total is XOF 119,000.
Why: Confirms the core VAT-exclusive calculation path and makes the rate dependency auditable.

Input: VAT-inclusive amount: XOF 119,000 at the standard 19% rate.
Expected: Net amount is XOF 100,000 and VAT is XOF 19,000.
Why: Confirms reverse VAT handling for invoices that already include tax.

Input: A supply labelled only as reduced, export or exempt.
Expected: The 10% and 0% paths remain locked without exact CIVA evidence; exemptions are not treated as zero-rated.
Why: Prevents unsupported special treatment.

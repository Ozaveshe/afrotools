Ghana Tax FAQ

Common Questions

What are the Ghana GRA PAYE tax bands for 2025?

Seven bands: 0% on first GHS 5,880; 5% on next GHS 1,320; 10% on next GHS 1,560; 17.5% on next GHS 38,000; 25% on next GHS 192,000; 30% on next GHS 360,000; 35% above GHS 600,000. Applied on chargeable income after SSNIT and reliefs.

How is SSNIT calculated in Ghana?

Employees contribute 5.5% of basic salary (Tier I: 0.5% + Tier II: 5%). Employers add 13%. The 2025 insurable earnings cap is GHS 61,000/year — if your basic exceeds this, employee SSNIT is capped at GHS 3,355/year. SSNIT applies to basic salary only, not allowances.

What is SSNIT Tier III and how does it reduce my tax?

Tier III is a voluntary occupational pension scheme managed by a licensed trustee. Contributions are tax-deductible up to 16.5% of your basic salary per year — directly reducing your chargeable income before PAYE is applied. It's one of Ghana's most powerful and underused legal tax tools.

What personal reliefs can I claim in Ghana?

Marriage relief (GHS 1,200/yr), child relief (GHS 1,200 per child, up to 2 children), disability relief (25% of income), old age relief GHS 1,500 (60+ years), and dependent relative relief. All must be registered with GRA via Relief Application Form.

How are bonuses taxed in Ghana?

The first 15% of annual basic salary paid as bonus is taxed at a reduced flat rate of 5%. Any bonus above 15% of basic is added to normal employment income and taxed at the progressive GRA PAYE rates.

What is the employer's total cost in Ghana?

Total employer cost = employee gross salary + 13% employer SSNIT on basic salary (capped at GHS 61,000). For example, an employee on GHS 60,000 gross with GHS 40,000 basic costs the employer approximately GHS 65,200/year total.

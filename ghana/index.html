About Ghana's Tax System

Ghana applies progressive PAYE through seven bands with a top rate of 35%, administered by the GRA. SSNIT Tier I contributions (5.5%) are deductible from gross income before applying tax bands, reducing effective income tax. The fiscal year follows the calendar year.

SSNIT Tier I contributions are 5.5% employee and 13% employer, capped at GHS 61,000 insurable earnings per year. Employers also contribute to Tier II occupational pension schemes. SSNIT provides retirement pension, invalidity and survivors' benefits.

Ghana is West Africa's second-largest economy. Gold, cocoa, oil and gas are primary exports. The GRA is one of West Africa's most capable revenue authorities with strong digital filing infrastructure and the GHS 61,000 SSNIT earnings cap updated annually.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Ghana's PAYE tax bands?
Ghana has 7 progressive bands to 35%. SSNIT Tier I (5.5%) is deductible from gross income before tax bands apply, reducing taxable income.
What are SSNIT rates in Ghana?
Tier I: 5.5% employee and 13% employer on earnings capped at GHS 61,000/year. Employers also contribute to Tier II occupational pension.
Is Ghana part of ECOWAS?
Yes. Ghana is a founding ECOWAS member and a major West African economic hub. The GRA operates under the Revenue Administration Act 2016.

The Problem

Africa deserves better than copy-paste calculators

Try searching for a Nigerian PAYE calculator. You'll find dozens — and most of them are wrong. Outdated tax bands, missing Consolidated Relief Allowances, wrong NHIF contribution schedules.

Kenyans can't find tools that reflect the 2024 Tax Laws Amendment Act. Ghanaians struggle with SSNIT tier calculations. South Africans need tools that handle medical tax credits and age rebates correctly.

AfroTools fixes this. Every calculator references the exact, current legislation — updated within days when tax laws change, not months.

Founder

Built by Ozaveshe

AfroTools was founded by Ozaveshe, a software engineer who grew frustrated with the lack of accurate financial tools for African countries. After building a Nigerian PAYE calculator for personal use and discovering that every publicly available alternative used outdated tax bands, he set out to build what the continent actually needed: a comprehensive, free, and accurate toolkit for every African nation.

The project launched in 2025 with a single country calculator and has grown to over 2,594 tools covering all 54 African countries. Every tool is hand-built with direct references to each country's official tax legislation, revenue authority guidelines, and government gazettes. When laws change, AfroTools updates within days — not months.

AfroTools is built entirely with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no frameworks, no bloat. This ensures tools load fast on any device, including low-end smartphones on slow mobile networks across the continent. Privacy is foundational: all calculations run in the browser and no personal data is ever transmitted to external servers.

Free, accurate financial tools for every African nation — because the continent deserves world-class calculators that understand its tax codes, currencies, and systems.

Why AfroTools Exists

Try searching for a Nigerian PAYE calculator. You'll find dozens — and most of them are wrong. They use outdated tax bands, ignore the Consolidated Relief Allowance, or mix up the Personal Income Tax Act with the old assessment rules.

The same problem exists across the continent. Kenyans can't find calculators that reflect the 2024 Tax Laws Amendment Act. Ghanaians struggle with SSNIT tier calculations. South Africans need tools that handle medical tax credits and age rebates correctly.

AfroTools fixes this. Every calculator we build uses the exact, current legislation — not approximations, not last year's rates.

Our Approach

  • Accuracy — Every calculation references specific sections of tax law. When legislation changes, we update within days, not months.
  • Coverage — We're building for all 54 African nations. Not just the big five. From Nigeria to Namibia, Ghana to Gabon.
  • Accessibility — Every tool is free, works on any device, and requires no account to use. Your data stays in your browser.

What We're Building

We started with PAYE salary calculators — the tool every working African needs. Next comes VAT calculators, then mortgage tools, investment calculators, and business utilities. Each tool type gets built across all 54 countries before we move to the next.

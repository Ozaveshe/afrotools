Mozambique taxes employment income through the IRPS — Imposto sobre o Rendimento das Pessoas Singulares — with seven bands and a top rate of 32%. A monthly tax-free threshold exempts low earners entirely. Employers withhold IRPS monthly and remit to the Autoridade Tributária. Mozambique has significant natural gas reserves increasingly important to fiscal revenues.

Social security is administered by INSS — Instituto Nacional de Segurança Social. Employees contribute 3% of gross salary; employers contribute 4%. INSS provides retirement pension and occupational risk coverage. The INSS system is expanding coverage as Mozambique's LNG sector increases formal employment.

Mozambique is a Southeast African nation with a long Indian Ocean coastline. Significant natural gas reserves in the Rovuma Basin, with major LNG projects from Eni and TotalEnergies, are reshaping Mozambique's fiscal base. Portuguese is the official language. Maputo is the capital.

How Malawi PAYE Tax Is Calculated 2025/26

Malawi's Pay As You Earn (PAYE) income tax is administered by the Malawi Revenue Authority (MRA) and calculated on a monthly basis. The four progressive MRA bands effective 30 December 2025 cover incomes from 0% on the first MWK 170,000/month to a maximum 40% above MWK 10,000,000/month. This calculator applies PAYE after the standard employee pension deduction used for covered employment.

Malawi's Pension Act provides a statutory pension framework for covered employment. The standard minimum rates are 10% by the employer and 5% by the employee on pensionable emoluments, and the employee portion is deducted before PAYE in this calculator. Employer pension is shown separately in total employer cost.

Malawi's four monthly MRA PAYE bands (effective January 2026): 0% on the first MWK 170,000/month; 30% on MWK 170,001–1,570,000; 35% on MWK 1,570,001–10,000,000; and 40% above MWK 10,000,000/month. In this calculator, an employee's standard 5% pension contribution is deducted before those bands are applied. Employers remit PAYE to MRA by the 14th of the following month.

Malawi uses the MWK (Malawi Kwacha). The economy is largely agricultural, and the MRA has been expanding digital filing capability. Employers must file monthly PAYE returns even for nil-tax months. Witholding tax on dividends, rental income, and interest is also collected by MRA. Verify current bands and any recent budget adjustments at mra.mw.

Malawi Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Malawi PAYE tax bands for 2025/26?

Four monthly bands (effective January 2026): 0% on first MWK 170,000; 30% on MWK 170,001–1,570,000; 35% on MWK 1,570,001–10,000,000; 40% above MWK 10,000,000. The 25% band was removed in the December 2025 MRA reform. In this calculator, PAYE is applied after the standard employee pension deduction.

Is there a mandatory pension scheme in Malawi?

Yes. For standard covered employment under Malawi's Pension Act, minimum pension contributions are 10% by the employer and 5% by the employee on pensionable emoluments. This calculator deducts the employee portion before PAYE and shows the employer portion separately.

How is PAYE calculated in Malawi?

PAYE is calculated monthly on gross salary using the four progressive bands. An employee earning MWK 500,000 would pay: MWK 170,000 at 0% (MWK 0), then MWK 330,000 at 30% (MWK 99,000), total MWK 99,000 PAYE.

Are there any tax-free amounts in Malawi?

The first MWK 170,000 of monthly gross salary is taxed at 0% — this is the tax-free band. Salaries at or below MWK 170,000 per month pay no PAYE. Above this, progressive rates apply starting at 30%.

When must employers remit PAYE to MRA?

Employers must file and remit PAYE to MRA by the 14th day of the month following the payroll month. Monthly PAYE returns are required even for nil-tax months.

Can I reduce my Malawi PAYE tax?

Mandatory and approved pension contributions can reduce taxable income within the statutory rules. This calculator already models the standard 5% employee pension deduction, and employer-specific approved pension arrangements may affect payroll in practice.

Calculation methodology

The calculator annualizes salary where needed, applies modeled employee statutory deductions, runs taxable income through the country PAYE bands, and derives net pay from gross pay minus modeled PAYE and statutory deductions. Employer-cost lines are informational where the page exposes them.

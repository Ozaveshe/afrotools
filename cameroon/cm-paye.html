Cameroon Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Cameroon income tax bands for 2025/26?

10% on first XAF 2,000,000; 15% on XAF 2,000,001–3,000,000; 25% on XAF 3,000,001–5,000,000; 35% above XAF 5,000,000. Applied annually to taxable income after CNPS deduction. Source: Direction Generale des Impots (DGI).

Is CNPS deductible from income tax?

Yes — the employee CNPS contribution (4.2% of gross salary) is fully deducted from taxable income before applying tax bands. Employer CNPS (11.2%) is a separate employer cost and does not affect your take-home pay.

What is the CNPS contribution rate in Cameroon?

Employees pay 4.2% of gross salary to CNPS (Caisse Nationale de Prevoyance Sociale). Employers pay 11.2% on top. CNPS covers old-age pension, workplace injury, and family benefits. There is a contribution ceiling of XAF 750,000/month.

How is PAYE calculated in Cameroon?

Start with gross monthly salary, deduct CNPS (4.2%), multiply by 12 to get annual taxable income, apply the 4-band progressive tax table, then divide by 12 for monthly PAYE. Additional surtaxes (CAC at 10% of tax) may apply.

What is the CAC (Centimes Additionnels Communaux)?

The CAC is a 10% surcharge on your income tax that goes to local municipalities. If your PAYE tax is XAF 50,000, you pay an additional XAF 5,000 in CAC, making your total tax XAF 55,000.

Are there any tax exemptions in Cameroon?

Cameroon offers family allowances and deductions for dependents. Married employees and those with children may qualify for reduced tax rates. Overtime pay may also be partially exempt depending on industry and employment contract.

What is the employer cost of hiring in Cameroon?

Employers pay 11.2% CNPS on top of the employee's gross salary. Total cost to company is gross salary plus the 11.2% employer CNPS contribution. There are no additional employer payroll taxes for most private-sector employees.

How Cameroon PAYE Tax Is Calculated 2025/26

Cameroon's income tax on salaries (Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques — IRPP) is administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts (DGI) and calculated on an annual basis. Employers withhold monthly based on annual projections. Unlike many African systems, Cameroon's IRPP starts at 10% — there is no 0% tax-free band for income tax; every franc of taxable income is subject to at least 10%. Employees earning below approximately XAF 166,740 gross per month (after CNPS) still pay 10% income tax.

Social security contributions are managed by the Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale (CNPS). Employees contribute 4.2% of gross salary to CNPS, covering old-age pensions, disability, and survivors' benefits. This 4.2% is fully deductible from gross salary before the IRPP calculation. Employers pay a further 11.2% of gross salary to CNPS, bringing the total combined social security rate to 15.4% of gross.

Cameroon uses four progressive annual DGI bands, all of which carry positive rates (no 0% band). The 10% rate applies to the first XAF 2,000,000 of annual taxable income. Rates then rise to 15%, 25%, and top out at 35% above XAF 5,000,000/year. Monthly PAYE (called retenue à la source) must be remitted to the DGI by the 15th of the following month.

Cameroon is a bilingual country (French/English) and part of the CEMAC (Central African Economic Community) monetary zone, using the XAF (Central African CFA franc), also fixed to the euro at 655.957. Employers' total cost is gross salary plus 11.2% CNPS. Verify rates with the DGI at impots.cm.

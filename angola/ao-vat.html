Legal boundaries

What the calculator will not guess

  • 14% is the general rate.
  • 7% simplified and eligible hospitality, 5% Annex I/II goods, and 1% eligible Cabinda transactions require confirmation.
  • Below AOA 25m is the exclusion regime; AOA 25m to below 350m is simplified; AOA 350m and above is general, subject to statutory exceptions and options.
  • Exports and listed operations are exemptions under the Code; this page does not relabel every exemption “zero-rated”.
  • Article 21 exclusions can override an apparent captive case.

Calculation methodology

The shared local engine adds or extracts the 14% general IVA rate. It exposes the 7% simplified-regime and eligible hospitality treatments, the 5% Annex I/II food and agricultural-input treatment, and the conditional 1% Cabinda treatment as explicit choices that require legal confirmation. Its regime screen applies the AOA 25 million and AOA 350 million boundaries and keeps Article 21 imposto cativo separate from the VAT rate.

Known limitations

  • The tool does not classify goods, services, hospitality eligibility, Cabinda eligibility or exemption treatment automatically.
  • Manufacturing, voluntary-option and Article 21 captive-tax eligibility depend on facts not established by the calculator.
  • Input-tax recovery, invoice compliance, filing, payment, refund and transition rules are outside the calculation.
  • Confirm the current legal treatment with AGT or a qualified Angolan tax professional before filing or remitting tax.

Test-case examples

Input: Net amount AOA 1,000 at the general 14% rate.
Expected: VAT AOA 140 and VAT-inclusive total AOA 1,140.
Why: Confirms the current general-rate add path.

Input: VAT-inclusive amount AOA 1,140 at the general 14% rate.
Expected: Net AOA 1,000 and VAT AOA 140.
Why: Confirms extraction and two-decimal rounding parity.

Input: Annual turnover AOA 24,999,999, AOA 25,000,000 and AOA 350,000,000.
Expected: Excluded, simplified and general regime results respectively, unless a stated manufacturing or voluntary-option rule applies.
Why: Protects both statutory turnover boundaries without turning them into automatic filing advice.

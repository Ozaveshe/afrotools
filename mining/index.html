Mining tools — frequently asked questions

What mining tools are live on AfroTools?

Seven tools are live today: the Mining Royalty Calculator covering 18 African jurisdictions, the Mining License Fee Calculator, a commodity price reference, the Diamond Valuation Estimator, the Oil Well Production Estimator, the Oil and Gas Revenue Calculator, and the Artisanal Mining Income Calculator. Three remain on the roadmap — mineral export duty, environmental impact fee and petroleum product pricing — and are marked as planned, not live.

How current are the mining royalty rates?

Royalty rates are planning-grade defaults drawn from each country's Mining Code or Finance Act and shown with a review date. They change at budget, and several countries use price- or profit-based sliding scales, so always confirm the current rate with the official source shown. The rates are backed by a public source ledger and are not legal or tax advice.

Which African countries does the Mining Royalty Calculator cover?

Eighteen mining jurisdictions: Ghana, Tanzania, South Africa, Zambia, DR Congo, Zimbabwe, Botswana, Namibia, Nigeria, Kenya, Mali, Burkina Faso, Egypt, Sierra Leone, Côte d'Ivoire, Liberia, Madagascar and Gabon — across gold, diamonds, copper, cobalt, coltan and other minerals. Jurisdictions we have not yet bound to an official source are recorded as known gaps rather than shown as fact.

Are these mining tools free?

Yes — every live tool here is completely free, works on any phone or computer with no signup, and is built for African mining markets across 54 countries.

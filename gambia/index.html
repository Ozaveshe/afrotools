The Gambia taxes employment income through progressive PAYE administered by the GRA with rates rising to 35%. Employers deduct PAYE monthly and remit to the GRA. The Gambia is the smallest country on mainland Africa and English-speaking, with a tax system based on the British legal tradition.

Pension and social protection is managed by SSHFC — Social Security and Housing Finance Corporation. Employees contribute 5% of gross salary; employers contribute 10%. The SSHFC administers both pension provision and housing finance schemes for formal sector workers.

The Gambia is a narrow strip of land along the Gambia River, completely surrounded by Senegal except for its Atlantic coastline. Tourism, groundnut exports and diaspora remittances are major economic contributors. The Dalasi (GMD) is the official currency.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

How is PAYE calculated in The Gambia?
Progressive PAYE bands with a top rate of 35%. Employers withhold monthly and remit to the GRA. English-speaking jurisdiction with British-tradition tax law.
What is the SSHFC?
SSHFC (Social Security and Housing Finance Corporation) manages pension contributions (5% employee, 10% employer) and housing finance facilities for formal sector workers.
What currency does The Gambia use?
The Gambia uses the Dalasi (GMD), introduced in 1971 to replace the West African Pound at independence.

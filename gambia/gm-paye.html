Gambia Tax FAQ

Common Gambia PAYE Questions

What are the Gambia GRA PAYE tax bands for 2025/26?

Five monthly bands: 0% on first GMD 270,000; 8% on GMD 270,001–520,000; GMD 20,000 plus 20% on GMD 520,001–760,000; GMD 68,000 plus 25% on GMD 760,001–1,000,000; GMD 128,000 plus 30% above GMD 1,000,000. These bands are applied monthly against taxable income (after SSHFC deduction).

Is SSHFC contribution deductible from PAYE taxable income?

Yes — the employee's mandatory 5% SSHFC contribution is fully deductible from gross pay before calculating PAYE. This reduces your taxable income and therefore your GRA income tax. The employer's 10% SSHFC contribution is a separate employer cost, not deducted from employee pay.

What is the total employer cost in Gambia?

Employers in Gambia pay gross salary plus 10% SSHFC. There is no SDL or WCF in Gambia. On a GMD 20,000/month salary, the employer adds GMD 2,000 SSHFC, making the total employer cost GMD 22,000/month. Employers must also remit the 5% employee SSHFC and monthly PAYE to the relevant authorities.

How is secondary employment taxed in Gambia?

Income from a second job in Gambia is taxed at a flat rate of 30% at source, with no deductions or tax-free band allowed. This is a final withholding tax — the secondary employer withholds 30% of the full gross amount and remits it to GRA. This prevents the progressive bands from being applied twice across two employers.

What is SSHFC in Gambia?

The Social Security and Housing Finance Corporation (SSHFC) is Gambia's mandatory social security body. Employees contribute 5% of gross salary and employers contribute 10% of gross salary. The SSHFC provides retirement pensions, disability and survivors' benefits. The employee contribution is fully deductible from taxable income before GRA income tax is calculated.

When must employers remit PAYE to GRA?

PAYE must be filed and paid to GRA monthly. SSHFC contributions are due by the end of the following month. Employers must file a monthly PAYE return even for nil-tax months. Late payment attracts penalties. Verify current filing deadlines at gra.gm.

How Gambia Income Tax Is Calculated 2025/26

The Gambia's income tax is administered by the Gambia Revenue Authority (GRA) and calculated on an annual basis, with monthly employer withholding. The employee's mandatory SSHFC contribution (5%) is deductible from gross salary before income tax bands are applied. Gambia uses progressive annual bands from 0% at the lowest incomes up to 30% at the top bracket. The Gambian Dalasi (GMD) is the national currency.

Social security in Gambia is administered by the Social Security and Housing Finance Corporation (SSHFC). Employees contribute 5% of gross salary, covering retirement pensions, disability, and survivors' benefits. This 5% is fully deductible from gross pay before GRA income tax is calculated. Employers contribute a matching 10% of gross salary to SSHFC. The combined SSHFC contribution of 15% is in line with the regional average for West Africa.

The Gambia is a small West African nation heavily dependent on tourism, remittances, and agriculture. The GRA has been strengthening its digital filing platform. Employers must file annual income tax returns in addition to monthly PAYE remittances. The Dalasi (GMD) has experienced fluctuations against major currencies.

For employers, total monthly cost per employee is gross salary plus 10% SSHFC. On a GMD 20,000/month salary, the employer adds GMD 2,000 SSHFC = total GMD 22,000/month. Verify current GRA income tax bands and SSHFC rates at gra.gm.

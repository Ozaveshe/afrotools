VAT & Business Tax Tools for African Entrepreneurs

Running a business in Africa means navigating tax systems that vary by country, filing period, invoice treatment, withholding rule, and source date. Nigeria charges 7.5% VAT, Kenya's standard VAT rate is 16%, South Africa remains at 15%, and Ghana's standard charge combines 15% VAT with 2.5% NHIL and 2.5% GETFund. Import duties, withholding taxes, zero-rated supplies, and exemptions add further complexity.

The VAT & Business Tax hub helps you move from a country question to a practical route. Start with pricing, invoices, withholding, filing, expansion, or rate checks, then open the calculators and document tools that match the job.

For SMEs and finance teams: The calculators still handle VAT-inclusive and VAT-exclusive calculations, profit margin, markup, break-even, invoices, and receipts. The hub helps a shop, startup, farm, agency, or importer find the right tool without exposing internal filing queues, customer data, invoice lines, uploaded files, or raw tax documents.

Zimbabwe operates a dual-currency PAYE system. The ZiG — Zimbabwe Gold — was introduced in April 2024 as the official currency. USD continues to be widely used. ZIMRA administers PAYE with progressive rates reaching 40%. An additional AIDS levy of 3% is charged on the computed income tax, bringing the effective top rate to approximately 41.2%.

Social contributions in Zimbabwe are managed through NSSA — National Social Security Authority. Both employees and employers contribute approximately 4.5% each of gross salary. The AIDS levy — 3% of computed income tax — is collected by ZIMRA and directed to the National AIDS Council.

Zimbabwe is a Southern African nation with significant agricultural, mining and tourism assets. Harare is the capital. The ZiG (Zimbabwe Gold) was introduced in 2024 as the latest currency, backed by gold reserves. ZIMRA publishes separate PAYE tables for ZiG and USD salary bases.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the AIDS levy in Zimbabwe?
Zimbabwe charges a 3% AIDS levy on the income tax computed under PAYE. This levy is collected by ZIMRA and directed to the National AIDS Council, effectively increasing the top rate from 40% to approximately 41.2%.
What currency should Zimbabwean salaries be paid in?
Zimbabwe officially uses the ZiG (Zimbabwe Gold), introduced April 2024. USD remains widely used. ZIMRA has PAYE tables for both currencies. Employers use the table matching the currency in which salary is paid.
How many currencies has Zimbabwe had?
Zimbabwe has had multiple currency iterations since 2009: USD (de facto), RTGS Dollar, Zimbabwe Dollar (ZWL), Zimbabwe Gold (ZWG), and now ZiG (2024). Each required ZIMRA to update PAYE tables.

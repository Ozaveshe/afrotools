South Africa taxes employment income through seven progressive bands with a 45% top rate for 2025/26, administered by SARS. Primary rebate of R17,235 applies to all taxpayers under 65. Medical Tax Credits (R364/month taxpayer, R246/month per dependant) reduce tax liability directly. The tax year runs March to February.

UIF contributions are 1% employee and 1% employer, capped at monthly remuneration of R17,712. UIF provides unemployment, maternity, illness and adoption benefits. Private retirement funds dominate pension provision with annual employer and employee deduction limits set by SARS.

South Africa is Africa's most industrialised economy. SARS is consistently ranked among Africa's top revenue authorities for digital capability. The March–February tax year means PAYE reconciliations and IRP5 certificates align with this distinctive fiscal cycle.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What are SARS PAYE bands for 2025/26?
7 bands: 18% on income up to R237,100 rising to 45% above R1,817,000. Primary rebate R17,235 (under 65). Secondary R9,444 (65–74). Tertiary R3,145 (75+).
What are Medical Tax Credits in South Africa?
MTC reduces income tax by R364/month for the taxpayer, R246/month for the first dependant, and R246/month for each additional dependant. These reduce tax owed, not taxable income.
What is UIF in South Africa?
UIF is 1% employee + 1% employer on monthly remuneration capped at R17,712/month. Pays unemployment, maternity, illness and adoption benefits.

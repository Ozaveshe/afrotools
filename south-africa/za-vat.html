South Africa VAT FAQ

Common VAT Questions

What is the standard VAT rate in South Africa?

The standard VAT rate in South Africa is 15%. This applies to most goods and services. Certain food items are zero-rated, and some services are exempt from VAT.

Which food items are zero-rated for VAT?

19 basic food items are zero-rated: maize, wheat, rice, flour, bread, milk, cheese, eggs, cooking oil, sugar, salt, tea, coffee, lentils, beans, spinach, cabbage, onions, and potatoes (including frozen versions).

What services are exempt from VAT in South Africa?

Exempt services include: financial services, life insurance, residential rental accommodation, education, health services, and municipal services. These do not charge VAT.

How do I calculate reverse VAT in South Africa?

To extract VAT from a total amount: Divide the total by 1.15 to get the net, then subtract net from total to get VAT amount. This calculator does this automatically when you select "Remove VAT" mode.

What is the VAT registration threshold in South Africa?

Businesses with annual turnover exceeding R1 million must register for VAT with SARS. Smaller businesses can voluntarily register. Registration allows reclaiming input VAT.

How often must VAT returns be filed?

VAT-registered businesses must file monthly VAT returns with SARS and remit VAT by the 25th of the following month. Returns are filed electronically through the SARS e-Filing system.

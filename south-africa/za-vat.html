Calculation methodology

For standard-rated supplies, the calculator adds 15% to a VAT-exclusive amount or extracts VAT from an inclusive amount by dividing by 1.15. Zero-rated and exempt outcomes require the user to confirm the applicable section 11 or section 12 treatment; a custom rate is a non-statutory planning scenario. The registration screen compares rolling 12-month taxable supplies using strict greater-than boundaries: above R2.3 million for compulsory review and above R120,000 through R2.3 million for the general voluntary-registration range, effective 1 April 2026.

Known limitations

  • Informational estimate only. It is not professional tax, legal, payroll, or filing advice.
  • Product classification, exemption, and zero-rating rules can require invoice-level review.
  • Sector-specific, regional, treaty, relief, and special-regime rules may not be fully modeled.
  • Confirm filing, registration, and remittance duties with the official authority or a qualified adviser before submission.

Test-case examples

Input: Standard-rated VAT-exclusive amount: ZAR 1,000.
Expected: VAT is ZAR 150 and the VAT-inclusive total is ZAR 1,150.
Why: Confirms the current 15% standard-rate addition path.

Input: Standard-rated VAT-inclusive amount: ZAR 1,150.
Expected: The VAT-exclusive amount is ZAR 1,000 and VAT is ZAR 150.
Why: Confirms the 15/115 inclusive extraction path.

Input: Confirmed section 11 zero-rated supply: ZAR 1,000.
Expected: VAT is ZAR 0 and the total remains ZAR 1,000.
Why: Confirms that a zero outcome is available only through explicit confirmed classification.

Input: Rolling 12-month taxable supplies: ZAR 2,300,001.
Expected: Registration status is compulsory-review.
Why: Confirms the strict greater-than compulsory threshold effective 1 April 2026.

Input: Rolling 12-month taxable supplies: ZAR 2,300,000.
Expected: Registration status is voluntary-range.
Why: Confirms that the compulsory test does not trigger at the exact threshold.

Input: Rolling 12-month taxable supplies: ZAR 120,000, then ZAR 120,001.
Expected: ZAR 120,000 is below-voluntary-threshold; ZAR 120,001 is voluntary-range.
Why: Confirms the strict greater-than voluntary threshold effective 1 April 2026.

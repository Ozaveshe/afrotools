Key Differences

Ghana's Favourable Bonus Taxation

One area where Ghana clearly outperforms Nigeria is bonus taxation. Bonuses from formal employment (up to 15% of annual basic salary) are taxed at a flat 5% in Ghana. Similarly, overtime earnings attract only 5%. In Nigeria under both PITA and NTA 2026, all earnings including bonuses are aggregated into the total taxable income and taxed at the applicable PAYE rate — no flat bonus rate exists.

SSNIT vs Nigeria's Pension

Ghana's SSNIT Tier I employee contribution of 5.5% is lower than Nigeria's mandatory 8% pension. However, SSNIT Tier I (13.5% total: 5.5% employee + 8% employer to SSNIT, 5% employer to provident fund) is structured differently from Nigeria's PRA 2014 which sends all 18% (8%+10%) to a Retirement Savings Account. Ghanaian employees on the mandatory Tier II (occupational pension) contribute an additional 5%, raising their total to 10.5%.

VAT: A Major Difference

While PAYE is the focus here, it's worth noting that Ghana's effective VAT rate is 18.5% (15% VAT + 2.5% NHIL + 1% GETFund) — much higher than Nigeria's 7.5%. This significantly affects purchasing power after tax, even if PAYE rates were identical.

Nigeria's Top Rate Advantage

At high salary levels, Nigeria's 24% cap is a clear advantage over Ghana's 35%. A senior professional at GHS 50,000/month in Ghana would pay ~35% on the marginal slice; their Nigerian counterpart at ₦5M/month pays 24% on the top band. This makes Nigeria more attractive to very high earners in West Africa's formal sector.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Ghana or Nigeria have a higher top tax rate?

Ghana's top rate is 35% vs Nigeria's 24% under NTA 2026. At equivalent high salary levels, Nigerian workers keep significantly more take-home pay.

How does SSNIT compare to Nigeria's pension?

SSNIT employee contribution (5.5%) is lower than Nigeria's mandatory pension (8%), giving Ghanaian workers an advantage on this specific deduction. But Ghana's employer SSNIT (13%) is higher than Nigeria's employer pension (10%).

Are bonuses taxed differently in Ghana?

Yes — Ghana taxes bonuses at a flat 5% rate (up to 15% of annual basic salary). Nigeria has no special bonus rate; all income including bonuses is part of the regular PAYE calculation.

How do I calculate Ghana PAYE vs Nigeria PAYE?

Use the Ghana PAYE Calculator for GRA-compliant calculations including SSNIT Tier I/II/III, overtime, and bonus. Use the Nigeria PAYE Calculator for NTA 2026 and PITA side-by-side comparison with pension and NHF.

Which West African country has the best tax environment for businesses?

Nigeria's lower individual tax rates benefit employees, while Ghana's more transparent bonus and overtime rules benefit structured payroll planning. Nigeria's corporate tax rate (30%) is the same as Ghana's (25%), so Ghana has an edge for corporates. Overall personal tax burden is lower in Nigeria for most salary levels.

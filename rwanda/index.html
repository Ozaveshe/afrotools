About Rwanda's Tax System

Rwanda taxes employment income through four progressive bands under Law 027/2022, administered by the RRA. Effective January 2025, RSSB (Rwanda Social Security Board) contributions doubled from 3%+3% to 6% employee and 6% employer — a significant increase in total employment cost.

RSSB contributions from January 2025 are 6% employee and 6% employer of gross salary (total 12%), doubled from the previous 3%+3%. RSSB provides retirement pension, medical insurance and occupational risk coverage for all formally employed workers in Rwanda.

Rwanda is one of Africa's fastest-growing economies. Kigali is a modern capital consistently ranked as one of Africa's cleanest and safest cities. Rwanda's RRA is considered one of Sub-Saharan Africa's most efficient revenue authorities with strong digital filing compliance.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What changed with RSSB in January 2025?
RSSB contributions doubled from 3%+3% to 6% employee + 6% employer (total 12%) from January 2025. This affects all formal sector employers and employees.
What are Rwanda's PAYE bands?
4 bands under Law 027/2022: 0% up to RWF 30,000/month, 20% on RWF 30,001–100,000, 30% on the highest band. Administered by the RRA.
How efficient is Rwanda's tax system?
Rwanda's RRA is consistently ranked among Sub-Saharan Africa's most efficient revenue authorities. Digital filing, mobile payments and strong enforcement have significantly raised the tax-to-GDP ratio.

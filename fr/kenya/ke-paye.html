Kenya Calculateur PAYE — How It Works

This Kenya PAYE calculator uses the current KRA 5-band progressive tax system with NSSF Niveaux I+II contributions (updated Feb 2025), SHIF at 2.75% (which replaced NHIF in October 2024), and the Prélèvement Logement Abordable at 1.5%. Critically, SHIF and AHL are deducted BEFORE calculating taxable income (they are pre-tax déductions as of 2025), while AHL tax relief was repealed in December 2024. Kenya Tax FAQ

Common Questions

How is PAYE calculated in Kenya?

PAYE uses 5 progressive bands: 10% on first KES 24,000/mo, 25% to KES 32,333, 30% to KES 500,000, 32.5% to KES 800,000, and 35% above. Personal relief of KES 2,400/month is deducted from gross tax.

What is SHIF and how does it replace NHIF?

SHIF (Social Assurance Maladie Fund) replaced NHIF from October 2024. Rate: 2.75% of gross, min KES 300, no cap. Unlike NHIF’s fixed bands, SHIF is percentage-based and is a pre-tax deduction.

Is AHL tax-deductible?

No. AHL tax relief was repealed by the Tax Laws (Amendment) Act 2024 effective December 2024. AHL (1.5%) is deducted from net pay only.

What is the disability exemption?

Persons with disability registered with NCPWD get the first KES 150,000/month (KES 1.8M/year) exempt from PAYE. This significantly reduces tax for qualifying individuals.

What are the NSSF Tier I and Tier II rates?

From Feb 2025: Tier I is 6% on first KES 8,000 (max KES 480). Tier II is 6% on KES 8,001–72,000 (max KES 3,840). Total max employee NSSF: KES 4,320/month. Employer matches the same amounts. NSSF is a pre-tax deduction that reduces your taxable income.

What is the pension deduction cap?

Registered pension scheme contributions are deductible up to KES 30,000/month (KES 360,000/year). This reduces taxable income before applying PAYE bands.

How does mortgage interest relief work?

Relief of 15% of interest paid on qualifying mortgage for owner-occupied property, up to KES 25,000/month (KES 300,000/year). This is deducted from gross tax alongside personal relief.

When is PAYE due?

PAYE must be remitted by the 9th of the following month via KRA iTax (Form P10). NSSF by 9th via NSSF i-portal. Late penalties apply.

What is the personal relief of KES 2,400/month?

Every resident individual taxpayer in Kenya receives a personal tax relief of KES 2,400 per month (KES 28,800 per year). This is deducted from your calculated PAYE tax, not from your income. It effectively means the first KES 24,000 of monthly income is taxed at 0% net. The relief is automatic and applied by your employer.

How do I get my P9 form in Kenya?

Your employer is required to issue a P9 Tax Deduction Card at the end of each tax year (by February). It summarizes your gross pay, PAYE deducted, and reliefs claimed. You need the P9 to file your annual tax return on KRA iTax. If your employer hasn’t provided it, request it from your HR or payroll department.

How Kenya PAYE Tax Works in 2026

Kenya’s Pay As You Earn (PAYE) system is administered by the Kenya Autorité fiscale (KRA) and applies to all employment income earned in Kenya. Employers must deduct PAYE monthly and remit it to KRA by the 9th of the following month using the iTax platform (Form P10). The tax is computed on taxable income after deducting NSSF contributions, SHIF, registered pension contributions, and other allowable déductions.

Kenya uses a five-band progressive tax system updated by the Finance Act 2023. Monthly taxable income up to KES 24,000 is taxed at 10%, then 25% up to KES 32,333, 30% up to KES 500,000, 32.5% up to KES 800,000, and 35% on amounts exceeding KES 800,000. After computing gross tax, a personal relief of KES 2,400 per month is deducted, along with any insurance relief or mortgage interest relief that applies.

Several statutory déductions affect your take-home pay beyond PAYE. NSSF contributions follow new rates effective February 2025: Tier I at 6% on the first KES 8,000 and Tier II at 6% on earnings between KES 8,001 and KES 72,000, for a maximum employee contribution of KES 4,320 per month. SHIF (Social Assurance Maladie Fund) replaced NHIF in October 2024 at 2.75% of gross salary with a minimum of KES 300 and no upper cap. The Prélèvement Logement Abordable (AHL) is 1.5% of gross salary, deducted after tax following the repeal of AHL tax relief in December 2024.

Persons with disabilities registered with NCPWD benefit from a substantial exemption: the first KES 150,000 of monthly income is exempt from PAYE. Mortgage interest relief allows qualifying homeowners to claim 15% of interest paid, up to KES 25,000 per month. These provisions make Kenya’s payroll system one of the most feature-rich in East Africa.

Kenya PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Kenya's PAYE system is administered by the Kenya Autorité fiscale (KRA) under Impôt sur le Revenu Act Cap 470. Five progressive monthly bands range from 10% to 35%, with a personal relief of KES 2,400 per month. PAYE, SHIF, and AHL must be remitted by the 9th of the following month via KRA iTax (Form P10).

Key 2025/26 déductions include: NSSF Tier I+II (February 2025 increase, UEL KES 72,000), SHIF at 2.75% of gross (replaced NHIF from October 2024, min KES 300), and the Prélèvement Logement Abordable (AHL) at 1.5% of gross. Both SHIF and AHL are pre-tax déductions that reduce the PAYE taxable base.

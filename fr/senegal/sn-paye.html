Senegal Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

What are the Senegal DGID tax bands for 2025?

Six annual bands: 0% on first XOF 630,000; 20% on XOF 630,001–1,500,000; 30% on XOF 1,500,001–4,000,000; 35% on XOF 4,000,001–8,000,000; 37% on XOF 8,000,001–13,500,000; 40% above XOF 13,500,000. These bands are applied annually against taxable income (after CSS deduction).

Is CSS contribution deductible from DGID taxable income?

Yes — the employee CSS contribution (5.6%) is fully deductible from gross pay before calculating DGID tax. This reduces your taxable income and therefore your DGID tax. The employer's CSS contribution is a separate employer cost.

What is the CSS in Senegal?

The Caisse de Sécurité Sociale (CSS) is the mandatory social security scheme in Senegal covering pensions, health insurance, and disability benefits. The employee contribution is 5.6% of gross salary, and this is fully tax-deductible.

When must employers remit DGID to tax authorities?

DGID taxes must be filed and paid quarterly or monthly depending on your tax bracket, with final settlement by March 15 of the following year. CSS contributions are typically remitted monthly to CSS. Check with DGID (dgid.sn) for your specific filing schedule.

How is PAYE calculated in Senegal?

Senegal uses an annual computation. First, the employee's 5.6% CSS contribution is deducted from gross salary. The resulting taxable income is then run through the six progressive DGID bands (0%–40%) to determine income tax. Take-home pay equals gross minus CSS minus DGID tax.

What is the tax-free threshold in Senegal?

The first XOF 630,000 of annual taxable income (after CSS deduction) is taxed at 0%. This means employees earning below approximately XOF 667,373 gross per year (XOF 630,000 / 0.944) pay no income tax — only the CSS contribution.

What is the total employer cost in Senegal?

Employers pay the gross salary plus employer CSS contributions, which cover family allowances, workplace injury insurance, and retirement. The total employer social contribution rate varies but typically ranges from 15%–20% depending on the schemes applicable. Employers must also withhold and remit employee CSS and DGID tax.

How Senegal Impôt sur le Revenu (DGID) Is Calculated 2025

Senegal's personal income tax system is administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts et Domaines (DGID) under the General Tax Code (Code Général des Impôts). The Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques (IRPP) applies to all employment income earned by residents and non-residents with Senegalese-source income. Employers deduct the equivalent of PAYE — called retenue à la source — monthly and remit it to DGID. Senegal's tax system follows the francophone model inherited from the French fiscal tradition, shared by Côte d'Ivoire, Cameroon, and other OHADA member states.

Senegal uses an annual six-band progressive tax structure. The first XOF 630,000 of annual taxable income is exempt from tax. Income from XOF 630,001 to XOF 1,500,000 is taxed at 20%; XOF 1,500,001 to XOF 4,000,000 at 30%; XOF 4,000,001 to XOF 8,000,000 at 35%; XOF 8,000,001 to XOF 13,500,000 at 37%; and above XOF 13,500,000 at 40%. The maximum marginal rate of 40% applies to high earners — among the higher personal income tax rates in West Africa.

The Caisse de Sécurité Sociale (CSS) manages social security contributions. The employee contribution rate is 5.6% of gross salary and is fully deductible from taxable income before income tax is computed — reducing the IRPP base. Employer social contributions are substantially higher, covering family allowances (7% up to a ceiling), workplace accident insurance (1-5%), and retirement contributions — total employer social charges typically range from 15% to 20% of gross payroll depending on the applicable scheme and sector.

Senegal uses the West African CFA Franc (XOF, FCFA), which is fixed at 655.957 XOF per Euro — providing currency stability for businesses. Senegal is one of Francophone Africa's most stable and diversified economies, with a population of approximately 17 million and a GDP of around $27 billion. The country is a member of ECOWAS and UEMOA, and cross-border trade with Côte d'Ivoire, Mali, Mauritania, and Guinea is significant. Senegal PAYE Tax Guide 2025/26

Senegal taxes employment income through the IRPP (Impôt sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques), administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts et des Domaines (DGID). The schedule spans six progressive annual bands in West African Francs (XOF), from a 0% band on the first XOF 630,000 annually to a top rate of 40% on income above XOF 13,500,000. A professional expense deduction of 30% of gross salary applies.

Social contributions are split: CSS (Caisse de Sécurité Sociale) charges 5.6% on employee wages (capped) and 9.4% on employer payroll for family benefits and occupational risk. A retirement fund (IPRES) contribution also applies to employees and employers in the private sector.

Comment le PAYE de Cameroon est calcule 2025/26

Cameroon's payroll income tax (IRPP) is administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts (DGI) and calculated on an annuel basis. The calculateur first deducts employée CNPS, then applies the annuel XAF 500,000 professional allowance, computes IRPP on the four positive bands, and finally adds CAC at 10% of IRPP.

Social security contributions are managed by the Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale (CNPS). Employée pension CNPS is 4.2% of salary and deductible before IRPP, while the baseline employer CNPS shown on this page is 11.2%. Both are applied on a capped salary base of XAF 750,000/month. Sector-specific accident-at-work premiums are separate and vary by risk group.

Cameroon uses four progressive annuel IRPP bands, all of which carry positive rates. The first XAF 2,000,000 of assessable income is taxed at 10%, followed by 15%, 25%, and 35% above XAF 5,000,000. The assessable base on this page is salary after employée CNPS and the fixed XAF 500,000 professional allowance.

Cameroon is part of the CEMAC monetary zone and uses the XAF (Central African CFA franc), fixed to the euro at 655.957. The employer-cost strip on this page is a baseline that includes capped employer CNPS only. Verify specialized payroll classifications directly with impôts.cm and CNPS Cameroon.

Cameroon Tax FAQ

Common PAYE Questions

Quelles sont les Cameroon income tax bands for 2025/26?

10% on the first XAF 2,000,000 of annuel assessable income, 15% on XAF 2,000,001–3,000,000, 25% on XAF 3,000,001–5,000,000 and 35% above XAF 5,000,000. This page deducts employée CNPS and the annuel XAF 500,000 professional allowance before applying the bands, then adds CAC at 10% of IRPP.

Is CNPS deductible from income tax?

Yes. Employée CNPS at 4.2% is deducted before IRPP is computed, subject to the statutory cap of XAF 750,000/month. The baseline employer CNPS at 11.2% is shown separately and does not affect take-home pay.

Qu'est-ce que the CNPS contribution rate in Cameroon?

Employées pay 4.2% and the baseline employer CNPS shown here is 11.2%, both on a capped salary base of XAF 750,000/month. Additional employer accident-at-work premiums vary by risk group and are not included in the baseline employer estimate.

How is PAYE calculated in Cameroon?

Start with annuel gross salary, deduct employée CNPS, subtract the XAF 500,000 professional allowance, apply the 4-band IRPP scale, then add CAC at 10% of IRPP. Net annuel pay equals gross salary minus CNPS minus IRPP minus CAC.

Qu'est-ce que the CAC (Centimes Additionnels Communaux)?

The CAC is a 10% surcharge on your income tax that goes to local municipalities. If your PAYE tax is XAF 50,000, you pay an additional XAF 5,000 in CAC, making your total tax XAF 55,000.

Are there any tax exemptions in Cameroon?

Cameroon offers family allowances and déductions for dependents. Married employées and those with children may qualify for reduced tax rates. Overtime pay may also be partially exempt depending on industry and employment contract.

Qu'est-ce que the employer cost of hiring in Cameroon?

Employers pay 11.2% CNPS on top of the employée's gross salary. Total cost to company is gross salary plus the 11.2% employer CNPS contribution. There are no additional employer payroll taxes for most private-sector employées.

Mauritius Tenancy Agreement — Legal Requirements

Tenancy agreements in Mauritius are governed by Landlord and Tenant Act (Act No. 22 of 1999, as amended). The maximum security deposit is 1–2 months rent. Landlord notice period: 3 months written notice. Tenant notice period: 1 month written notice (monthly tenancy); 3 months (annual). Disputes are resolved through the Rent Tribunal / District Court.

Tenant protection: Eviction requires 3 months notice and court order if disputed. Rent increases: No statutory cap for private sector; market-driven. Stamp duty: Yes — registration duty on leases; stamp duty applies.

Disclaimer: This is a template. Tenancy laws vary and change. Consult a qualified property lawyer in Mauritius before signing any lease agreement.

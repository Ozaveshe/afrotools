About Senegal

Senegal Tax & Finance Overview

Senegal's tax system is administered by the Direction Generale des Impots et des Domaines (DGID) and applies a progressive personal income tax known as the Impot sur le Revenu (IR) with rates from 0% to 40%, denominated in the West African CFA Franc (XOF). As a WAEMU member state, Senegal shares its currency with seven other West African nations and benefits from the monetary stability provided by the CFA Franc's peg to the Euro. The progressive tax structure features multiple bands with rates increasing from 0% through 20%, 30%, 35%, 37%, to the top marginal rate of 40% on the highest income levels. Employees in Senegal contribute to the Caisse de Securite Sociale and the Institution de Prevoyance Retraite du Senegal (IPRES) for social security, with the employee share covering retirement and health insurance while employers bear the larger portion including family allowances, occupational risk, and pension contributions. The combined social charges can exceed 20% of gross salary. Senegal applies a standard VAT rate of 18% on most goods and services, with exemptions for essential foodstuffs, medical products, and educational materials. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers must withhold IR and remit it to the DGID monthly. Senegal's economy is one of the most dynamic in West Africa, driven by agriculture, fishing, mining (phosphates, gold), tourism, and emerging oil and gas production. Dakar serves as the economic hub of francophone West Africa.

Nigeria Tax FAQ

Common Questions

What are the new Nigeria PAYE tax bands for 2026?

Under the Nigeria Tax Act 2026: 0% on the first ₦800,000; 15% on ₦800,001–₦3,000,000; 18% on ₦3,000,001–₦12,000,000; 21% on ₦12,000,001–₦25,000,000; 23% on ₦25,000,001–₦50,000,000; and 25% on income above ₦50,000,000.

What happened to CRA under the new tax law?

The Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA) is abolished under NTA 2026. It is replaced by Rent Relief — the lower of 20% of annual rent paid or ₦500,000. If you own your home, you cannot claim Rent Relief.

When does the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 take effect?

The NTA was signed into law on June 26, 2025 but takes effect from January 1, 2026. Your 2025 payslip and tax return still use the old PITA system with CRA. Select "Old System — PITA" above for 2025 calculations.

How is pension calculated under NTA 2026?

Under the new law, pension (8% employee contribution) is calculated only on pensionable emoluments — Basic Salary + Housing Allowance + Transport Allowance. Not total gross salary. This differs from the old PITA approach.

Who is exempt from PAYE in Nigeria?

Under NTA 2026, employees earning ₦800,000 or less annually pay 0% income tax. Under the old PITA, employees earning at or below the national minimum wage (₦70,000/month from May 2024) are exempt from PAYE.

Does NHF still apply under the new tax law?

Yes. NHF (National Housing Fund) at 2.5% of basic salary remains a statutory deduction and is still allowable against taxable income under NTA 2026. NHIS contributions are also still deductible with proper documentation.

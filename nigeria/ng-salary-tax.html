Nigeria Tax FAQ

Common Questions

What are the new Nigeria PAYE tax bands for 2026?

Under NTA 2026: 0% on first ₦800,000; 15% on ₦800,001–₦3M; 18% on ₦3M–₦12M; 21% on ₦12M–₦25M; 23% on ₦25M–₦50M; 25% above ₦50M.

What happened to CRA under the new tax law?

CRA is abolished under NTA 2026. Replaced by Rent Relief — the lower of 20% of annual rent paid or ₦500,000. If you own your home, you cannot claim Rent Relief.

When does the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 take effect?

Signed June 26 2025. Takes effect January 1 2026. Your 2025 payslip still uses PITA with CRA. Select "Old System — PITA" above for 2025 calculations.

How is pension calculated under NTA 2026?

Under NTA 2026, pension (8% employee) applies only to Basic Salary + Housing + Transport — not total gross. This is a significant change from the old PITA approach.

Who is exempt from PAYE in Nigeria?

Under NTA 2026, employees earning ₦800,000 or less annually pay 0% tax. Under PITA, employees earning ≤₦70,000/month (from May 2024) are exempt.

Does NHF still apply under NTA 2026?

Yes. NHF at 2.5% of basic salary remains statutory and deductible against taxable income under NTA 2026. NHIS contributions also remain deductible with documentation.

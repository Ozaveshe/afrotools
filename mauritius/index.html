About Mauritius

Mauritius Tax & Finance Overview

Mauritius has one of the most streamlined and business-friendly tax systems in Africa, administered by the Mauritius Revenue Authority (MRA). Personal income tax follows a simple two-bracket progressive structure with rates of 10% and 15%, denominated in the Mauritian Rupee (MUR). This flat-rate approach, combined with generous personal deductions and allowances, makes Mauritius one of the lowest-taxed jurisdictions on the continent for individuals. Employees contribute to the National Pension Fund (NPF) at approximately 3% of basic salary for the Contributory Social Security scheme, and the National Savings Fund (NSF) at 2.5%, while employers contribute matching amounts plus additional contributions to the Training Levy. The total social contribution burden is relatively moderate compared to other African nations. Mauritius applies a standard VAT rate of 15% on most goods and services, with zero-rating on exports and exemptions for essential items including basic foodstuffs, medical services, and educational services. The fiscal year runs from July to June, and employers must withhold PAYE and remit it to the MRA by the end of the month following the pay period. Mauritius consistently ranks as one of the easiest places to do business in Africa, with strong institutions, low corruption, and an efficient tax administration. The country's economy is diversified across tourism, financial services, textiles, ICT, and real estate.

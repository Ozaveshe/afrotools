How a build works

The process keeps your calculator practical: scope the outcome, verify the rules, prototype the workflow, then publish with a clear review path.

Define the customer question

Examples: calculate net pay, compare fees, qualify a lead, estimate import cost, plan tuition, or prepare a document checklist.

Verify sources and limits

Bring formulas, official links, rate tables, legal disclaimers, data freshness expectations, and claims that must be avoided.

Prototype the calculator and lead path

Ship the smallest usable input/output flow, CTA, source notes, and opt-in form before expanding the feature set.

Publish, embed, and review

Launch on AfroTools or partner infrastructure, test mobile behavior, review leads, and decide whether to expand.

Custom calculator questions

The strongest builds start with one clear user decision, a source model, and a business action that respects consent.

What should a custom calculator brief include?

Include the target country or region, the customer question, known formulas or sources, required outputs, lead action, compliance limits, and whether the calculator should be hosted, embedded, or both.

Can AfroTools build from business assumptions?

Yes, but assumptions must be labeled clearly. Official, legal, tax, lending, or compliance claims need source review before launch.

Can the calculator collect leads?

Yes. Lead capture can be added when the user takes an explicit action and consents to submit the enquiry. The calculator itself should stay useful before the form appears.

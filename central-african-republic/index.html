About Central African Republic

Central African Republic Tax & Finance Overview

The Central African Republic's tax system is administered by the Direction Generale du Tresor et de la Comptabilite Publique (DGTCP) and applies a progressive personal income tax known as the Impot sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques (IRPP) with rates from 0% to 50%, denominated in the Central African CFA Franc (XAF). This makes the Central African Republic home to one of the highest top marginal income tax rates on the entire African continent. The progressive IRPP structure features multiple bands with rates increasing from a tax-free threshold through intermediate rates before reaching the exceptionally high top marginal rate of 50% on the highest income levels. Employees contribute to the Caisse Nationale de Securite Sociale (CNSS) at 2% of gross salary, while employers contribute 7%, for a combined social security contribution of 9% covering basic pension benefits and occupational risk insurance for formal sector workers. As a CEMAC member state, the Central African Republic shares the XAF currency with five other Central African nations (Cameroon, Chad, Congo, Equatorial Guinea, and Gabon), benefiting from the monetary stability provided by the CFA Franc's fixed peg to the Euro. The country applies a standard VAT rate of 19% on most goods and services, with exemptions for essential items. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers in the formal sector must withhold IRPP from employee salaries and remit it to the DGTCP within established monthly deadlines. The Central African Republic's economy is primarily driven by subsistence agriculture, forestry, diamond and gold mining, and livestock, with Bangui serving as the commercial and administrative capital. Despite significant natural resource wealth including diamonds, gold, uranium, and timber, the country faces considerable governance and infrastructure challenges that limit formal tax administration reach and economic development. The DGTCP continues to work with international development partners to strengthen revenue collection capacity and broaden the tax base. The fiscal year follows the calendar year. Bangui is the capital. Significant economic activity is informal, limiting formal PAYE coverage.

Social security contributions to CNSS are modest: employees contribute 2% and employers contribute 7% of gross salary. The Central African Republic is a landlocked Central African nation with significant diamond, gold and timber resources. Despite natural wealth, it ranks among the world's least developed countries. The XAF is shared across six CEMAC nations. Peace-building efforts continue with international support.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the top income tax rate in CAR?
The Central African Republic has a top IRPP rate of 50% — one of the highest in Africa. The progressive system applies lower rates to lower incomes before reaching this high marginal band.
What are CNSS rates in CAR?
Employees contribute 2%; employers contribute 7% to CNSS. Coverage includes basic pension and occupational risk for formal sector workers.
What currency does CAR use?
The Central African CFA Franc (XAF), shared across six CEMAC nations and pegged to the Euro.

About Tunisia

Tunisia Tax & Finance Overview

Tunisia's tax system is administered by the Direction Generale des Impots (DGI) and applies a progressive personal income tax known as the Impot sur le Revenu des Personnes Physiques (IRPP) with rates from 0% to 35%, denominated in the Tunisian Dinar (TND). The progressive structure features multiple bands with rates increasing from a tax-free threshold through intermediate rates of 15%, 25%, 30%, and 32% before reaching the top marginal rate of 35% on the highest income levels. Employees in Tunisia contribute to the Caisse Nationale de Securite Sociale (CNSS) for social security at approximately 9.18% of gross salary, while employers contribute approximately 16.57%, for a combined social security contribution of roughly 25.75% covering pensions, health insurance, and related social benefits. This makes Tunisia one of the higher social contribution jurisdictions in North Africa. Tunisia applies a standard VAT rate of 19% on most goods and services, with reduced rates of 7% and 13% applying to essential items including basic foodstuffs, medical supplies, and certain professional services. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers must withhold IRPP and remit it to the DGI within prescribed monthly deadlines. Tunisia's economy is diversified across manufacturing (textiles, automotive components, electronics), tourism, agriculture (olive oil, dates, citrus), phosphate mining, and a growing technology and offshoring services sector. The country benefits from strong trade links with the European Union and has a relatively well-developed financial services sector. Tunisia has one of the most educated workforces in Africa, with high university enrollment rates supporting its knowledge-based economic ambitions. The DGI has implemented electronic tax filing and payment systems to improve compliance and modernize administration.

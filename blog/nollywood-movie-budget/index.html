The Three Budget Tiers of Nollywood

Nollywood productions broadly fall into three budget categories, each with distinct production values, distribution strategies, and revenue expectations.

Low Budget: 5 - 30 Million Naira ($3,000 - $20,000)

This is where the majority of Nollywood output sits. Low-budget productions are characterised by short shooting schedules (5-10 days), small casts with emerging actors, minimal locations (often donated or borrowed), basic equipment, and streamlined post-production. Distribution is typically through YouTube, social media, or direct digital sales.

These films are the backbone of the industry's volume. While production quality varies enormously, some low-budget films have achieved viral success on YouTube, generating millions of views and substantial ad revenue. The key constraint at this level is not creativity but resources for equipment, locations, and talent.

Mid Budget: 30 - 100 Million Naira ($20,000 - $65,000)

Mid-budget productions represent the sweet spot for many Nollywood producers. At this level, you can afford recognisable actors (not necessarily A-list), professional equipment and crew, multiple locations, proper lighting and sound, and competent post-production with colour grading and sound design.

These films target streaming platforms (Netflix, Prime Video, Showmax, iROKOtv), limited cinema releases, and television broadcast licensing. A mid-budget film with a good story and recognisable faces can generate strong returns through a combination of platform licensing deals and cinema revenue.

High Budget: 100 Million - 1 Billion+ Naira ($65,000 - $650,000+)

High-budget Nollywood productions are the films that dominate the box office and make international headlines. These productions feature A-list actors, top-tier directors and cinematographers, premium locations (including international shoots), advanced visual effects, orchestral soundtracks, and extensive marketing campaigns.

Films in this category include cinema-first releases that target the growing Nigerian box office market. The top-grossing Nollywood films have earned over 1 billion naira at the Nigerian box office, proving that significant investment can yield significant returns.

Detailed Budget Breakdown

Pre-Production (10-15% of Budget)

Pre-production costs include script development, location scouting, casting, production design, and planning. For a mid-budget production, expect to spend:

  • Script: 500,000 - 3 million naira for an original screenplay. Adaptations or scripts from established writers cost more
  • Location scouting and securing: 200,000 - 1 million naira depending on the number and type of locations
  • Casting: 100,000 - 500,000 naira for casting director fees and audition logistics
  • Production design: 500,000 - 2 million naira for art direction, props, and set construction
  • Wardrobe and makeup: 300,000 - 2 million naira depending on the period and genre

Cast (20-35% of Budget)

Actor fees are often the single largest line item in a Nollywood budget, and they vary enormously based on star power:

  • Emerging actors: 100,000 - 500,000 naira per movie
  • Mid-level recognisable actors: 1 - 5 million naira per movie
  • A-list stars: 10 - 30 million naira or more per movie
  • International guest stars: Highly variable, often $10,000 - $50,000+ USD

Many producers balance their cast budget by pairing one or two recognisable names (who drive ticket sales and streaming interest) with emerging actors who deliver strong performances at lower cost.

Crew (15-20% of Budget)

A professional film crew in Lagos in 2026 commands the following approximate daily or project rates:

  • Director: 2 - 15 million naira per project (top directors command significantly more)
  • Director of Photography: 1 - 5 million naira per project
  • Sound Recordist: 300,000 - 1 million naira per project
  • Gaffer (Lighting Chief): 200,000 - 800,000 naira per project
  • Production Manager: 500,000 - 2 million naira per project
  • Assistant Director: 300,000 - 1 million naira per project
  • Grip and electric team: 50,000 - 150,000 naira per person per day

Equipment (8-12% of Budget)

Camera and equipment rental rates in Lagos have become more competitive as the number of rental houses has grown:

  • Cinema camera package (RED, ARRI, Blackmagic): 150,000 - 500,000 naira per day
  • Lens set: 100,000 - 300,000 naira per day
  • Lighting package: 100,000 - 400,000 naira per day
  • Sound equipment: 50,000 - 150,000 naira per day
  • Grip equipment (dolly, jib, stabiliser): 50,000 - 200,000 naira per day
  • Generator: 30,000 - 80,000 naira per day (essential due to power supply challenges)

Locations (5-10% of Budget)

Location fees in Lagos and other Nigerian cities vary widely:

  • Private homes (Lagos Island): 200,000 - 1 million naira per day
  • Private homes (mainland): 100,000 - 500,000 naira per day
  • Office spaces: 150,000 - 500,000 naira per day
  • Hotels and event centres: 300,000 - 2 million naira per day
  • Outdoor public locations: Often require permits costing 50,000 - 200,000 naira
  • Studio space: 200,000 - 1 million naira per day for a professional soundstage

Post-Production (10-15% of Budget)

Post-production transforms raw footage into a finished film:

  • Editing: 1 - 5 million naira for a feature film
  • Colour grading: 500,000 - 3 million naira
  • Sound design and mixing: 500,000 - 3 million naira
  • Original score/music: 500,000 - 5 million naira
  • Visual effects (basic): 500,000 - 3 million naira
  • Visual effects (advanced): 3 - 20 million naira or more for action or fantasy genres
  • Subtitling and localisation: 200,000 - 500,000 naira

Marketing and Distribution (10-20% of Budget)

Marketing is increasingly critical for Nollywood success, particularly for cinema releases:

  • Poster design and key art: 200,000 - 1 million naira
  • Trailer production: 500,000 - 2 million naira
  • Social media campaign: 500,000 - 5 million naira
  • Billboard and outdoor advertising: 2 - 10 million naira
  • Premiere event: 1 - 10 million naira
  • Cinema distribution fees: Cinemas typically take 50% of box office revenue
  • NFVCB certification: Required for cinema release, fees vary

Revenue and Return on Investment

Understanding potential revenue is essential for budgeting decisions. Nollywood films generate income through several channels:

  • Cinema box office: Top films earn 200 million to over 1 billion naira. Average performing films might earn 20 - 100 million naira
  • Streaming platform deals: Netflix licensing fees for Nigerian content reportedly range from $50,000 to $500,000+ depending on the project
  • YouTube: Ad revenue from millions of views, typically generating $1-3 per 1,000 views
  • Television broadcast: Licensing to TV stations can generate 2 - 20 million naira
  • International distribution: Diaspora screenings, international streaming platforms, and film festival circuits

Cost-Saving Strategies for Filmmakers

Shoot Efficiently

Every additional shooting day adds equipment rental, crew fees, catering, and transport costs. Tight scheduling, thorough pre-production, and an experienced assistant director can save millions of naira by keeping the shoot on schedule.

Negotiate Deferred Payments

Some actors and crew members will accept deferred payment or backend deals (a percentage of revenue) in exchange for reduced upfront fees. This is more common with emerging talent who want the exposure and are willing to bet on the project's success.

Leverage Your Network for Locations

Location fees are negotiable, and many filmmakers secure locations through personal connections. A friend's apartment, a relative's office, or a community centre can serve as a film location at a fraction of the commercial rate.

Invest in Sound

Poor audio quality is the most common technical flaw in low-budget Nollywood productions. Investing in a good sound recordist and proper audio equipment is relatively cheap compared to other line items but makes an enormous difference in the final product's quality and marketability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum budget to make a Nollywood movie?

The minimum budget for a basic Nollywood movie in 2026 is approximately 5 to 15 million naira (roughly $3,000 to $10,000 USD). This covers a small cast, minimal crew, basic equipment, limited locations, and simple post-production. Quality at this level is basic and distribution is limited primarily to YouTube and social media.

How much do Nollywood actors get paid?

Fees vary enormously. Emerging actors earn 100,000 to 500,000 naira per movie. Mid-level actors earn 1 to 5 million naira. A-list stars can command 10 to 30 million naira or more. Some top actors also negotiate backend deals with a percentage of revenue.

How do Nollywood movies make money?

Through cinema box office, streaming platform licensing (Netflix, Prime Video, Showmax), YouTube ad revenue, television broadcast rights, international distribution, and digital sales. The top-grossing Nollywood films have earned over 1 billion naira at the Nigerian box office.

Can I get funding for a Nollywood movie?

Yes. Sources include private investors, bank loans (some Nigerian banks have entertainment desks), the Bank of Industry Creative Industry Financing Initiative, corporate sponsorships, crowdfunding, and streaming platform commissioning. Netflix and Amazon have directly funded Nigerian original content.

How long does it take to shoot a Nollywood movie?

Low-budget productions shoot in 5 to 10 days. Mid-budget films take 2 to 4 weeks. High-budget productions can take 4 to 8 weeks or longer. Post-production adds another 2 to 6 months depending on complexity.

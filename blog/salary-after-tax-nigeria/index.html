How Nigerian Salary Tax Works in 2026

Nigeria uses a Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) system where employers deduct income tax from employees' salaries before payment. The tax is progressive, meaning higher income earners pay a higher percentage of income in tax. For 2026 payroll, the important change is that the old Personal Income Tax Act bands and Consolidated Relief Allowance are no longer the main planning assumption. The Nigeria Tax Act 2025 introduced a wider 0% band, new higher-income bands, and rent relief in place of the old CRA structure.

Step 1: Determine Gross Annual Income

Your gross income includes all compensation from employment: basic salary, housing allowance, transport allowance, utility allowance, meal allowance, and any other benefits. Employers typically structure salaries with a basic component (usually 40-60% of gross) and various allowances making up the remainder. This structure matters because some deductions are calculated specifically on the basic salary.

Step 2: Apply Current NTA 2026 Reliefs

Under the old PITA system, many payroll estimates started with the Consolidated Relief Allowance of 20% of gross income plus the higher of ₦200,000 or 1% of gross income. For 2026 NTA calculations, do not use that old CRA formula as the default.

Current summaries of the Nigeria Tax Act show a 0% band on the first ₦800,000 of annual taxable income and rent relief equal to 20% of annual rent paid, capped at ₦500,000, where the worker can support the claim. If you do not have rent evidence or your employer does not apply it in payroll, your no-rent-relief PAYE estimate will be higher.

Step 3: Deduct Pension and NHF Contributions

Statutory deductions further reduce your taxable income:

  • Employee Pension Contribution: 8% of (Basic Salary + Housing Allowance + Transport Allowance). Under the Pension Reform Act, employees contribute a minimum of 8% while employers contribute 10%.
  • National Housing Fund (NHF): 2.5% of Basic Salary. This mandatory contribution applies to employees earning above the national minimum wage.

Step 4: Apply PAYE Tax Bands

After subtracting approved pension, NHF and any supported reliefs from gross income, the remaining annual taxable income is taxed at the NTA 2026 progressive rates:

What Does Each Deduction Mean?

PAYE Income Tax

PAYE is the income tax deducted at source by your employer. It is remitted to the relevant State Internal Revenue Service (SIRS) where you are resident. Lagos State, for instance, collects PAYE through the Lagos Internal Revenue Service (LIRS). Your employer is legally required to deduct and remit PAYE; failure to do so makes the employer liable for the tax plus penalties.

Under the NTA 2026 provisions, the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) and state revenue authorities remain central to payroll compliance, while the law changes the personal income tax bands and relief structure. Treat this guide as a planning estimate, not as a filing instruction.

Pension Contribution

The employee pension contribution of 8% applies under the contributory pension scheme, while the employer contributes at least 10%. PenCom guidance describes the minimum total contribution as 18% of monthly emoluments, with the pensionable base not less than basic salary, housing allowance and transport allowance. Many payroll templates use those three components, but your employment contract may define pensionable emoluments more specifically.

Your pension contribution is tax-deductible and accumulates in your Retirement Savings Account (RSA) managed by a Pension Fund Administrator (PFA) of your choice. Combined with the employer's 10% contribution, this represents an 18% savings rate toward retirement.

National Housing Fund (NHF)

The NHF contribution of 2.5% of basic salary was established by the National Housing Fund Act 1992. It applies to all workers earning above the minimum wage and is designed to create a pool of long-term, low-cost funds for housing development. Contributors can access NHF-backed mortgages through the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria at preferential interest rates, typically around 6% per annum, significantly below commercial mortgage rates.

Other Possible Deductions

  • NHIS/Health Insurance: Some employers deduct employee contributions toward the National Health Insurance Scheme or private health insurance plans.
  • Union Dues: If you belong to a trade union, monthly dues may be deducted at source.
  • Cooperative Contributions: Many workplaces have cooperative societies where members authorize payroll deductions for savings or loan repayments.
  • Salary Advances: Repayments for salary advances or staff loans are deducted from monthly pay.

NTA 2026: What Changed?

The Nigeria Tax Act 2025, effective from January 1, 2026, introduced several reforms that affect salary after tax calculations. For a detailed analysis, see our full guide to NTA 2026 changes. Key provisions affecting employees include:

  • Minimum wage and low-income relief: Employees earning not more than the ₦70,000 monthly national minimum wage are not liable to PAYE deductions, and current reform notes also describe annual gross income up to about ₦1,200,000 as exempt because it translates to about ₦800,000 taxable income.
  • CRA replaced for 2026 planning: The old CRA formula is no longer the default 2026 calculation. Rent relief of 20% of annual rent, capped at ₦500,000, is now the practical relief to model where the worker can support it.
  • New progressive bands: The annual taxable-income bands now run from 0% on the first ₦800,000 to 25% above ₦50,000,000, which changes both low-income and executive payroll estimates.
  • Digital filing mandates: Employers are increasingly required to file PAYE returns electronically, which should improve accuracy and reduce disputes between employers, employees, and tax authorities.
  • Enhanced penalties for non-compliance: The act strengthens enforcement mechanisms against employers who fail to remit PAYE deductions, protecting employees whose tax is deducted but not paid to the government.

How to Maximize Your Take-Home Pay

While you cannot avoid statutory deductions, there are legitimate strategies to optimize your net salary:

1. Negotiate Your Salary Structure

Since pension and NHF are calculated on specific components (basic + housing + transport for pension, basic only for NHF), a salary structure with a lower basic salary and higher non-pensionable allowances can reduce total deductions. However, this also reduces your pension savings and NHF contributions, which have long-term benefits.

2. Claim All Eligible Reliefs

Additional deductions or reliefs may be available, depending on your records and payroll setup:

  • Life insurance relief: Premiums paid on life insurance policies are tax-deductible.
  • NHIS contributions: National Health Insurance premiums can reduce your taxable income.
  • Voluntary pension contributions: Additional pension contributions beyond the mandatory 8% are tax-deductible up to a cap, and the investment income within your RSA grows tax-free.
  • Mortgage interest: Home loan interest payments may qualify for tax relief under certain conditions.

3. Ensure Accurate Tax Computation

Errors in PAYE computation are surprisingly common. Verify your payslip against an independent calculation using the AfroTools PAYE Calculator. If your employer is over-deducting, raise the issue with your HR or finance department with supporting calculations.

Quick Payslip Audit Checklist

Before you challenge a deduction or accept a new offer, collect the details that affect your real take-home pay. A one-line gross salary is not enough for a reliable PAYE estimate.

  • Confirm the salary period: Know whether the figure is monthly, annual, gross, net, or total cost to company.
  • Ask for the salary split: Pension and NHF can depend on basic salary, housing and transport rather than every allowance in the package.
  • Separate employee and employer pension: Your 8% employee contribution reduces take-home pay. The employer's 10% contribution is a benefit, but it should not be subtracted from your net salary.
  • Check rent relief evidence: If payroll applies NTA 2026 rent relief, keep rent receipts, tenancy agreements or other records your employer or adviser requires.
  • Compare old and new regimes where relevant: If you are reconciling a 2025 payslip, use the old PITA mode. For 2026 payroll planning, use the NTA 2026 mode.
  • Keep filing boundaries clear: Use calculators for planning and variance checks, then confirm official remittance, state PAYE filing and dispute steps with your employer, the relevant state tax authority or a licensed adviser.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much tax do I pay on a ₦500,000 salary in Nigeria?

On a gross monthly salary of ₦500,000, a no-rent-relief NTA 2026 estimate with the article's salary split gives about ₦66,200 monthly PAYE, ₦30,000 pension, ₦5,000 NHF and roughly ₦398,800 take-home. Use the PAYE Calculator for your exact salary structure.

What is the minimum taxable income in Nigeria 2026?

Under the NTA 2026 rules, the first ₦800,000 of annual taxable income is taxed at 0%, and employees earning not more than the ₦70,000 monthly national minimum wage are not liable to PAYE deductions.

What deductions come out of my salary in Nigeria?

Standard payroll deductions can include PAYE income tax under the 2026 bands, employee pension contribution of 8% of qualifying monthly emoluments, National Housing Fund contribution of 2.5% of basic salary, and health insurance or other approved deductions where your employer applies them.

How is PAYE calculated under the NTA 2026?

For 2026 NTA estimates, start with annual employment income, deduct approved pension, NHF and other allowable deductions, apply any supported rent relief, then tax the remaining annual taxable income at 0% on the first ₦800,000, 15% on the next ₦2,200,000, 18% on the next ₦9,000,000, 21% on the next ₦13,000,000, 23% on the next ₦25,000,000 and 25% above ₦50,000,000.

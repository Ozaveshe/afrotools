Nigeria's VAT rules changed materially on January 1, 2026. The standard rate remains 7.5%, but the old shorthand that every business above ₦25 million turnover must register no longer describes the current law. The Nigeria Tax Administration Act 2025 sets a new small-business test and gives qualifying businesses a VAT filing exemption, with an option to enter the VAT system. Businesses outside that test must plan for registration, invoices, records and monthly returns through the Nigeria Revenue Service, or NRS.

This guide explains the current decision path. It is a planning guide, not a tax ruling. Confirm your classification and supply type with the NRS or a qualified adviser before changing how you invoice customers.

What changed for Nigeria VAT in 2026?

The new tax laws commenced on January 1, 2026. They also replaced the Federal Inland Revenue Service with the Nigeria Revenue Service for federal tax administration. Older pages, invoices and search results may still use FIRS or TaxPro Max language. For a current registration journey, start with the official NRS Taxpayer Self-Service registration page, which provides routes for corporate, individual, non-resident and free-trade-zone taxpayers.

Does a small business have to register and charge VAT?

Section 147 of the Nigeria Tax Administration Act defines a small business using three conditions. The business must earn gross turnover of ₦100 million or less per year, have total fixed assets below ₦250 million, and not provide professional services. All parts matter. Turnover means gross business receipts, not profit after expenses. Fixed assets also matter, so turnover alone is not a safe classification test.

Section 22 exempts a small business from the monthly VAT-return rule. It also allows a qualifying small business to opt out of that exemption by sending written notice to the NRS. A business that opts in registers, charges VAT on taxable supplies and files returns. Petroleum operations are excluded from that opt-in provision, so sector-specific advice is necessary there.

A business should not assume that being below ₦100 million settles every question. Professional-service businesses do not qualify under the statutory definition. A growing business also ceases to receive the filing exemption when it no longer qualifies. Review the position before the first invoice of a new accounting period, after a major asset purchase, and when revenue approaches the ceiling.

Keep a short classification memo with annual turnover, a fixed-asset schedule, the nature of services supplied and the source provisions checked. That record is more defensible than a screenshot of an old threshold article.

How to register and prepare for VAT administration

Tax registration and VAT charging are related but not identical questions. The NTAA requires persons within the tax system to obtain a tax ID, while the small-business provisions determine whether a qualifying business must operate the monthly VAT process. Start at the official NRS Taxpayer Self-Service portal. Do not rely on an unofficial agent page for credentials or payment instructions.

  1. Confirm the legal person. Match the CAC name, registration number, tax ID, trading name and business address.
  2. Classify the business. Record turnover, fixed assets, sector and whether the activity is a professional service.
  3. Map supplies. Separate standard-rated, zero-rated and exempt sales. A business can make more than one type of supply.
  4. Set up invoice fields. Use sequential invoice numbers, transaction dates, customer and supplier details, descriptions, taxable value and VAT as separate fields.
  5. Create VAT ledgers. Keep output VAT on sales separate from potentially creditable input VAT on purchases.
  6. Retain proof. Save portal acknowledgements, filed returns, payment receipts, invoices, credit notes and calculations for each period.

The law permits the NRS to deploy technology for real-time or other prescribed reporting. The NRS self-service portal already lists e-invoicing among its services. Treat e-invoicing requirements as an implementation item to verify for your taxpayer segment rather than assuming that every business follows the same onboarding schedule.

How to calculate output VAT and input VAT

For a VAT-exclusive taxable price, multiply the taxable value by 7.5%. A taxable supply priced at ₦1,000,000 before VAT produces ₦75,000 VAT and a ₦1,075,000 invoice total. This is a formula walkthrough based on the statutory rate, not a claim about a particular transaction.

For a VAT-inclusive amount, the VAT portion is 7.5 divided by 107.5 of the total. On a VAT-inclusive receipt of ₦1,075,000, that isolates ₦75,000 VAT. The AfroTools VAT calculator handles both directions, but the user must still determine whether the underlying supply is taxable.

Output VAT is VAT charged on taxable sales. Input VAT is VAT incurred on purchases. Under the Nigeria Tax Act, input VAT connected with taxable supplies may be credited, subject to the statutory conditions and documentation. Costs connected solely with exempt supplies do not produce the same recovery outcome. Where a cost supports both taxable and exempt activity, an apportionment may be required.

Do not claim a credit merely because a supplier wrote “VAT” on a receipt. Check that the purchase belongs to the business, supports taxable supplies, has a valid tax invoice and has not already been claimed. Keep rejected or deferred items in a reconciliation schedule.

Monthly VAT returns and the 21st-day deadline

Section 22 of the NTAA requires a taxable person covered by the monthly rule to file on or before the 21st day of the following month, whether or not economic activity took place. The return reports input VAT paid, output VAT collected and the resulting VAT payable for the preceding month. An extension to file does not automatically extend the time to pay.

A practical month-end control should reconcile the sales ledger to issued invoices, the purchase ledger to supplier evidence, VAT control accounts to the return, and the filed return to the payment receipt. Review credit notes and cancelled invoices separately. If the portal rejects a return, retain the error evidence and escalation record instead of treating an attempted upload as a completed filing.

Taxable, exempt and zero-rated supplies

Do not use a generic internet list as your final product tax map. The Nigeria Tax Act contains schedules for exempt and zero-rated supplies, and classification depends on the exact legal description. Zero rating means VAT applies at 0%, which can preserve input-credit treatment. Exemption means the supply sits outside the charge in a different way, with different consequences for related input VAT.

The Act provides relief across specified areas such as basic food items, medical products and services, educational supplies, public transport and exported supplies, but the boundary language matters. For example, a broad label such as “food,” “health” or “education” is not enough to classify every product sold by a retailer or service provider. Check the current schedule and any NRS guidance against the actual item.

Non-resident suppliers also need a separate review. The NTAA brings non-resident persons supplying taxable goods or services to Nigeria into the tax-registration framework. Digital services, marketplace collection and business-to-business transactions can carry special collection mechanics. Do not copy the domestic small-business decision tree onto a cross-border supply.

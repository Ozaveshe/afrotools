A South African VAT invoice is not just a payment request with VAT typed into a total line. For a VAT vendor, the invoice is evidence that output tax was charged correctly. For the buyer, it is the practical support for an input tax deduction. If the invoice is missing required details, the commercial deal may still be real, but the VAT evidence can fail.

This guide was verified on May 19, 2026 against SARS public guidance, including the SARS tax invoice page, the SARS VAT404 Guide for Vendors, the SARS tax invoice checklist, the VAT rate page and the April 2026 VAT threshold FAQs. It is written for small business owners, finance teams, accountants, freelancers and agencies that issue or review VAT invoices in South Africa. It is not legal advice. For complex supplies, imports, exports, connected parties, second-hand goods, fixed property, accommodation, mixed supplies or credit notes, confirm the treatment with a South African VAT practitioner.

The key difference between a normal invoice and a valid VAT tax invoice is evidence. The document must identify the parties, the supply, the VAT vendor details, the invoice sequence and the tax amount clearly enough for SARS, the seller and the buyer to trace what happened.

Full Tax Invoice Checklist For Supplies Above R5,000

SARS guidance says a full tax invoice is required where the consideration for the supply is more than R5,000. The SARS checklist lists seven criteria. All seven need to be present for a full tax invoice to meet the basic document test.

SARS full invoice field What it means in practice Common failure
Words tax invoice, VAT invoice or invoiceThe document must be labelled clearly enough to identify it as the invoice record.Sending only a quote, pro forma or payment request.
Supplier name, address and VAT registration numberThe seller's legal identity and VAT registration must appear on the document.Showing a trading name but omitting the VAT registration number.

Accurate description of goods or servicesThe buyer and SARS should be able to identify what was supplied.Using vague descriptions such as "services rendered" for high-value work.
Quantity or volume suppliedThe invoice should show units, hours, items, volume or another clear measure where applicable.Listing only a total price with no support for the quantity.
Value, VAT amount and total considerationThe invoice should show the VAT-exclusive value, the tax charged and the VAT-inclusive total, or use another SARS-accepted presentation method.Showing one total with no clear VAT split.

For a supplier, these fields should be locked into the invoice template instead of typed manually every time. The supplier name, address, VAT number and invoice numbering logic should come from one source of truth. The line-item description, quantity, VAT treatment and totals should be reviewed for each invoice because those change with the transaction.

For a buyer, the checklist is an input tax control. Before claiming input VAT, confirm that the supplier is a VAT vendor, the buyer details are right, the VAT amount is visible and the document is the final tax invoice rather than a quote or pro forma. If the invoice is wrong, request a corrected invoice before the return is filed.

Abridged Tax Invoice Rules For Smaller Supplies

SARS guidance allows an abridged tax invoice where the consideration is greater than R50 and not more than R5,000. The abridged invoice has a shorter checklist than a full invoice. It still needs the invoice label, supplier name, supplier address, supplier VAT registration number, serial number, issue date, accurate description, and the value, VAT amount and total consideration.

The main practical difference is buyer identity. An abridged invoice does not carry the same recipient detail requirement as a full tax invoice. That makes sense for many lower-value retail or routine supplies, but it should not become a shortcut for bigger B2B transactions. Once the consideration exceeds R5,000, use a full tax invoice.

When the consideration is R50 or less, SARS says a tax invoice is not required, but a document such as a till slip or sales docket showing VAT charged will be needed to verify input tax deducted. In real finance workflows, this means small receipts still matter. A missing receipt can break a small claim even when the amount is below the formal tax invoice threshold.

VAT Calculation Checks Before You Send The Invoice

The South African standard VAT rate remains 15% according to SARS guidance checked on May 19, 2026. The invoice calculation should start with classification. A supply can be standard-rated, zero-rated or exempt. Standard-rated supplies carry VAT at 15%. Zero-rated supplies are taxable supplies at 0%. Exempt supplies sit outside output VAT and usually do not support ordinary input tax recovery in the same way.

For standard-rated invoices, check whether your price is VAT exclusive or VAT inclusive. If the agreed price is VAT exclusive, VAT is normally calculated as price times 15%. If the agreed price is VAT inclusive, the VAT portion is extracted by multiplying the inclusive total by 15 divided by 115. The South Africa VAT Calculator can check both directions quickly.

Zero-rated taxable supplyVAT rate is 0%, subject to source rules and evidence.Do not treat zero-rated and exempt as the same thing.
Exempt supplyNo output VAT is charged on the exempt supply.Check input tax consequences before claiming related VAT.

Do not fix an invoice by changing only the total. If the VAT amount changes, the line item value, tax amount, total consideration, accounting entry and VAT201 support should all agree. A buyer who receives a corrected invoice should archive the corrected document and avoid relying on the earlier broken version.

Buyer And Supplier Controls That Prevent VAT Problems

Suppliers should build invoice controls into the issuing workflow. Use sequential invoice numbers, prevent duplicate numbers, store VAT registration details in the template, keep customer VAT numbers for vendor customers, and label credit notes or cancellations separately from ordinary invoices. The goal is not only to send a professional document. The goal is to produce a document that can survive VAT review months later.

Buyers should run a receiving checklist before claiming input VAT. Confirm that the supplier name matches the contract or purchase order, the VAT registration number is present, the buyer's vendor details are included when a full invoice is required, the goods or services are described clearly, the VAT amount makes mathematical sense, and the invoice date falls in the return period being used.

For recurring subscriptions, retain each tax invoice or the SARS-compliant recurring invoice record. A bank statement alone can prove payment, but it does not necessarily prove the VAT details of the supply. For card expenses, keep the receipt or tax invoice, especially where the business intends to recover input VAT.

For remote services and digital work, do not assume that a foreign-looking service invoice is a South African VAT invoice. Identify the supplier, the supply location, the VAT registration position and whether imported services or foreign supplier VAT rules are relevant. This guide covers ordinary South African VAT invoice evidence, not every cross-border edge case.

Stale VAT Invoice Claims To Remove

Remove any claim that every invoice can be treated the same way regardless of value. SARS distinguishes between full tax invoices above R5,000, abridged tax invoices for supplies above R50 and not more than R5,000, and lower-value evidence for supplies of R50 or less.

Remove any template that still uses the old compulsory VAT registration threshold without a source note. SARS states that from April 1, 2026, the compulsory registration threshold increased to R2.3 million and the voluntary threshold increased to R120,000, subject to the applicable rules and exceptions. Businesses close to the threshold should monitor taxable supplies on a rolling basis.

Remove any claim that a quote, pro forma invoice or payment request is automatically enough for input tax. A buyer needs proper VAT evidence. If the supplier sends a pro forma before payment and a tax invoice after supply, the buyer should retain the tax invoice for the VAT file.

Remove any invoice process that hides whether prices are inclusive or exclusive of VAT. That confusion creates bad quotations, under-collected VAT, buyer disputes and incorrect accounting. Every template should make the pricing basis visible.

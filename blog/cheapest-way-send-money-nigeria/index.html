Cheapest Way to Send Money to Nigeria in 2026

By AfroTools Team Published Mar 13, 2026 11 min read
AfroTools Team Mar 13, 2026 Last updated: March 2026 11 min read

Nigeria is one of the largest remittance markets in the world, receiving over $20 billion annually from its diaspora. Whether you are sending money home to family, paying a Nigerian freelancer, or settling a business invoice, the platform you choose can mean a difference of thousands of naira on every transfer.

This guide compares the six most popular ways to send money to Nigeria in 2026: Wise (formerly TransferWise), Remitly, WorldRemit, Western Union, Chipper Cash, and Grey. We break down fees, exchange rates, speed, and when to use each service.

Detailed Review: Each Service

Wise (formerly TransferWise)

Wise is consistently one of the cheapest options for sending money to Nigeria from the US, UK, Canada, and Europe. The key advantage is that Wise uses the mid-market exchange rate (the same rate you see on Google) and charges a transparent, upfront fee.

Pros:

  • Mid-market exchange rate with zero markup
  • Transparent fee shown before you send
  • Multi-currency account available
  • Regulated in multiple jurisdictions

Cons:

  • Only delivers to bank accounts in Nigeria (no cash pickup)
  • Transfers can take 1-2 business days
  • Not available in every African country as a sender

Best for: Regular senders who prioritize the best exchange rate and do not need instant delivery or cash pickup.

Grey

Grey is a Nigerian fintech that provides foreign currency accounts (USD, GBP, EUR) primarily for Nigerian freelancers and remote workers. It has become popular for converting foreign income to naira at competitive rates.

Pros:

  • Zero or very low transfer fees on many corridors
  • Competitive exchange rates close to the parallel market
  • Fast delivery to Nigerian bank accounts
  • Virtual USD/GBP/EUR accounts for receiving payments

Cons:

  • Primarily designed for receiving and converting, not traditional remittance
  • Requires Nigerian BVN for full verification
  • Limited to sending to Nigeria (not multi-country)

Best for: Nigerian freelancers and remote workers receiving USD/GBP payments who need to convert to naira.

Remitly

Remitly offers two speed tiers: Express (delivered in minutes, higher fee or lower exchange rate) and Economy (1-3 business days, lower fee). It supports bank deposits, mobile money, and cash pickup at partner locations across Nigeria.

Pros:

  • Express option for urgent transfers
  • Multiple delivery options including cash pickup
  • Frequent promotions for first-time users
  • Available from US, UK, Canada, and EU

Cons:

  • Exchange rate markup of 1-3% on most transfers
  • Express transfers are more expensive
  • Rates vary significantly by payment method (debit card vs bank transfer)

Best for: Users who need flexibility in delivery method, especially cash pickup in Nigerian cities.

WorldRemit

WorldRemit is one of the oldest digital remittance platforms and covers more African countries than most competitors. It supports bank transfer, mobile money, cash pickup, and airtime top-up in Nigeria.

Pros:

  • Wide range of delivery options
  • Covers 130+ countries for sending
  • Airtime top-up option for quick gifts
  • Generally faster than traditional banks

Cons:

  • Exchange rate markup of 1-2.5%
  • Fees vary by corridor and payment method
  • Customer service can be slow during peak periods

Best for: Users who need a reliable, multi-option platform and value the ability to send airtime or cash pickup across Africa.

Chipper Cash

Chipper Cash is an African-founded fintech that enables cross-border payments across several African countries. It charges zero fees on P2P transfers but makes money on the exchange rate spread.

Pros:

  • No transfer fees
  • Fast delivery within Africa
  • Also supports stock and crypto investing
  • User-friendly mobile app

Cons:

  • Exchange rate markup of 1-3% absorbs the "free" transfer benefit
  • Primarily designed for Africa-to-Africa transfers
  • Limited support for sending from Europe or North America

Best for: Africa-to-Africa transfers and users who prefer a mobile-first experience.

Western Union

Western Union remains the most accessible option, with a wide agent network across Nigeria. It is the go-to choice when the recipient does not have a bank account or needs cash immediately.

Pros:

  • Largest cash pickup network in Nigeria
  • Available in rural areas where digital services do not reach
  • Cash pickup within minutes
  • Decades of trust and brand recognition

Cons:

  • Highest fees of any option ($5-25+ depending on amount and method)
  • Exchange rate markup of 2-5%
  • The combined cost (fee + markup) makes it the most expensive option for most transfers

Best for: Emergency cash transfers to recipients without bank accounts, especially in rural areas.

How to Choose the Right Service

The best service depends on your specific situation. Here is a decision framework:

  • Sending regularly to a bank account? Use Wise for the best exchange rate and total value.
  • Nigerian freelancer receiving foreign income? Grey gives you a virtual USD/GBP account and competitive conversion rates.
  • Need cash pickup urgently? Remitly Express or Western Union for same-day cash.
  • Sending within Africa? Chipper Cash for fee-free transfers (watch the rate markup).
  • Sending to someone without a bank account? WorldRemit or Western Union for cash pickup or mobile money.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Beyond the stated fee, there are several hidden costs that can eat into your transfer:

  • Exchange rate markup , The biggest hidden cost. A 3% markup on $1,000 costs you $30, even if the "fee" is $0. Always check how much naira the recipient will actually receive.
  • Payment method surcharges , Paying by credit card often costs 1-3% more than bank transfer or debit card. Some services charge more for instant funding.
  • Recipient bank charges , Some Nigerian banks charge a small fee for receiving international transfers. This is typically NGN 50-100 but can vary.
  • Intermediary bank fees , For SWIFT wire transfers (not applicable to Wise, Grey, or Chipper Cash), intermediary banks may deduct $15-30 from the transfer.

Tips for Getting the Best Rate

  1. Compare on the day you send. Exchange rates change daily. Use the AfroTools Currency Converter to check the current mid-market rate, then compare what each service offers.
  2. Send larger amounts less frequently. Fixed fees mean sending $500 once costs less in fees than sending $100 five times.
  3. Pay by bank transfer, not card. Most services charge lower fees for bank-funded transfers.
  4. Check promotions. Remitly and WorldRemit frequently offer zero-fee first transfers or enhanced exchange rates for new customers.
  5. Time your transfer. The naira rate can fluctuate significantly week to week. If the transfer is not urgent, monitor the rate and send when it is favorable.

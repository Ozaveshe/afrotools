Tanzania VAT looks simple until an invoice, platform fee, or export sale needs to be posted correctly. The headline rule is familiar: standard rated supplies are taxed at 18% and exports of goods and services are listed at 0%. The harder work is deciding when registration is required, keeping the right supporting documents, issuing the correct electronic records, and knowing which newer Finance Act changes affect digital and withholding workflows.

What Tanzania VAT Covers

TISEZA describes VAT as a consumption tax charged on taxable goods, services, and immovable property whenever value is added at each stage of production and at the final stage of sale. The same official page says VAT is charged on both locally produced goods and services and imports, and that it is charged only by persons registered for VAT.

That definition has two practical consequences. First, VAT is not just a retail tax at the point of sale. It follows value added through the supply chain. Second, a business should not charge VAT merely because its competitors do. It needs to be registered, or required to register, and it needs records that can support the VAT position on the invoice.

For most Tanzanian businesses, the first review question is not "what rate should I charge?" It is "is this supply taxable, zero-rated, exempt, imported, exported, or outside the VAT registration requirement?" Once that classification is wrong, the rate and calculator result can also be wrong.

Current Tanzania VAT Rates and Supply Types

The current official rate set is narrow at the headline level. TISEZA lists 18% for standard rated supplies and 0% for exports of goods and services. The zero rate does not mean the transaction is irrelevant. It means the supply remains a taxable supply charged at 0%, so the records still matter.

Supply type Rate What to check before invoicing
Standard rated local supply 18% Confirm the seller is VAT registered, then apply output VAT to the taxable value.
Export of goods or services 0% Keep export evidence and contract records. Zero-rated is not the same as undocumented.
Import Usually assessed through customs and tax rules Check the customs value, VAT treatment, and any exemption or special schedule before pricing.
Digital or platform-linked supply Depends on the VAT Act rule and payment path Review the Finance Act 2025 changes for online intermediation, marketplace, and payment platform services.

Do not treat zero-rated and exempt as interchangeable. A zero-rated export can still belong inside the VAT system. An exempt supply may sit outside output VAT charging and can affect input VAT recovery. If the supply classification is uncertain, the safest operational answer is to pause the invoice and check the underlying legal category before using any calculator.

Registration Threshold and Documents

TISEZA's current VAT page gives the registration threshold as TZS 100 million in a twelve-month period or TZS 50 million in a six-month period ending at the end of the previous month. That means a growing business should not wait for a calendar year to close before monitoring the threshold. The six-month test can bring the registration issue forward.

The same official page lists registration steps and supporting documents. It says the applicant fills the VAT registration form and submits company and business evidence. For a company, the practical document pack should be treated as a control list:

  • VAT registration application form
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association
  • Lease agreement or title deed for the premises
  • Business licence
  • Tax Identification Number certificate
  • Photos, where requested in the application process

TISEZA also says there is no fee for VAT registration. That does not mean registration is casual. TRA can still expect the business to show a real business location, consistent records, and invoices that match the registered taxpayer details.

Registration checkpoint: If taxable turnover is near TZS 50 million over six months, review VAT registration immediately. Waiting until the annual threshold is visible can leave the business behind the compliance clock.

How to Calculate VAT Inclusive and Exclusive Amounts

The standard Tanzania VAT math uses the 18% rate. The formula is easy when the price is VAT exclusive. It is more error-prone when the invoice amount already includes VAT.

Use case Formula Worked amount
Add VAT to a net price Net amount x 18% TZS 1,000,000 net gives TZS 180,000 VAT and TZS 1,180,000 total.
Extract VAT from a VAT-inclusive price Gross amount x 18 / 118 TZS 1,180,000 gross contains TZS 180,000 VAT.
Find the net amount from a gross price Gross amount / 1.18 TZS 1,180,000 gross gives TZS 1,000,000 net.

This is the same logic used by the AfroTools Tanzania VAT Calculator. Use "add VAT" when the commercial price is net. Use "remove VAT" or reverse VAT when the total already includes VAT. Then keep the invoice, EFD receipt, and payment evidence aligned with the number you used in the return.

EFD and Invoice Controls

TRA operates the EFDMS public portal for suppliers and manufacturers of Electronic Fiscal Devices. That portal is a small page, but it confirms that EFD infrastructure is an official TRA-controlled compliance surface rather than a private bookkeeping preference.

For a VAT-registered seller, electronic receipt controls should be part of the monthly VAT file. The minimum internal evidence pack should connect four things:

  • The customer invoice or receipt number
  • The EFD or electronic fiscal record
  • The taxable value, VAT amount, and gross amount
  • The payment record, especially where the transaction is bank or electronically paid

When those records do not reconcile, the tax risk is not only a calculation issue. It becomes an evidence issue. A finance team may know that the 18% output tax was calculated correctly, but the return is still weak if the EFD record, bank payment, and invoice do not tell the same story.

What the Finance Act 2025 Changed for VAT Workflows

The Finance Act 2025 came into operation on July 1, 2025 and amended the Value Added Tax Act. For ordinary businesses, the most useful parts are workflow signals rather than a new headline standard rate.

First, the Act amended the treatment of certain online intermediation and platform services. The wording includes accommodation, online marketplace, and payment services platform language. This matters for businesses that earn from platform fees, marketplace intermediation, app-enabled services, or payment facilitation. The practical advice is simple: do not assume a platform supply is outside Tanzania VAT merely because the transaction happens online.

Second, the Act added detail around VAT withholding and certificate evidence. It says a taxable person should not subtract withheld output tax unless, at the time of filing the VAT return for the relevant tax period, the person holds a valid VAT withholding certificate. It also introduces certificate details for withholding agents and advance VAT paid by assisted government entities.

Third, the Act references proof of bank payment or electronic payment for certain supplies taxed at 16% under the VAT Act. If your business uses a special reduced-rate treatment, the operational lesson is not just to pick the rate. You need the payment evidence that supports the treatment.

Monthly Tanzania VAT Control Checklist

A good monthly VAT process is boring by design. It should catch the same issues before every filing period.

Control Why it matters Tool or record
Turnover threshold review Six-month and twelve-month tests can trigger registration. Sales ledger and management accounts
Rate classification review Standard, zero-rated, exempt, import, and platform supplies need different treatment. Invoice coding file
VAT math check VAT-inclusive invoices are easy to overstate if the team multiplies the gross amount by 18%. Tanzania VAT Calculator
EFD and payment reconciliation Invoice, fiscal receipt, and bank or electronic payment evidence should line up. EFD report, bank statement, sales ledger
Withholding certificate file Finance Act 2025 makes certificate evidence important before subtracting withheld output tax. Withholding certificates and return workpaper

The calculator can solve the arithmetic, but it cannot decide whether a supply is taxable, zero-rated, exempt, or caught by a special platform rule. That classification should sit in the workpaper before the return is submitted.

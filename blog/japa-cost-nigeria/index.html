The True Cost of Relocating from Nigeria in 2026: A Complete Breakdown

By AfroTools TeamPublished Mar 19, 20269 min read
AfroTools TeamMar 19, 20269 min read

Japa - the Yoruba word that has become shorthand for leaving Nigeria - is not just a dream for millions of Nigerians. It is a concrete financial project that requires careful planning, a realistic budget, and a clear understanding of what every step actually costs. Relocation agents and social media influencers often quote incomplete figures that leave people underprepared. This guide lays out the full cost breakdown for the five most popular japa destinations in 2026.

Why Accurate Cost Estimates Matter

The most common reason japa plans fail is not visa denial - it is running out of money halfway through the process. Application fees are non-refundable. IELTS scores expire after two years. Exchange rate movements can add millions of naira to your budget overnight. Knowing the true total upfront lets you set a savings target that actually works.

As of March 2026, the naira trades around NGN 1,550-1,600 per USD in the parallel market. All naira figures in this guide use an approximate rate of NGN 1,550/USD for consistency, but you should check the current dollar-to-naira rate before finalizing your budget.

Canada: The Most Popular Destination

Canada remains the top japa destination for Nigerians due to Express Entry's points-based system, which does not require a job offer. Here is what a single applicant should budget:

Pre-Application Costs

  • IELTS General Training: NGN 310,000 – NGN 350,000 (approximately USD 225). Many applicants take it twice to improve scores.
  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA): NGN 470,000 – NGN 550,000 (WES fee of CAD 240 plus transcript and courier costs).
  • IELTS preparation course (optional): NGN 100,000 – NGN 300,000 for a structured prep program.

Application Fees

  • Express Entry application fee: CAD 1,365 (approximately NGN 1.55 million) for the principal applicant.
  • Biometrics: CAD 85 (NGN 97,000).
  • Medical examination: NGN 150,000 – NGN 200,000 at an approved panel physician in Lagos or Abuja.
  • Police clearance certificate: NGN 20,000 – NGN 50,000.

Proof of Funds

Canada requires you to show settlement funds in your bank account for at least three months before applying. For a single applicant, the minimum is approximately CAD 14,690 (about NGN 16.7 million). For a family of four, this rises to approximately CAD 27,000 (NGN 30.7 million). This money is not paid to anyone - it stays in your account - but you need to have it available.

Post-Approval Costs

  • Right of Permanent Residence Fee: CAD 515 (NGN 586,000).
  • One-way flight Lagos to Toronto: NGN 1.2 – NGN 2.5 million depending on airline and season.
  • First 3 months' rent (Toronto): CAD 5,400 – CAD 7,500 for a one-bedroom apartment (NGN 6.1 – NGN 8.5 million).
  • Winter clothing and essentials: CAD 500 – CAD 1,000 (NGN 570,000 – NGN 1.14 million).

Total Canada Budget (Single Applicant)

NGN 20 – NGN 30 million (CAD 17,500 – CAD 26,000), including proof of funds, flights, and three months of settling in. Without counting proof of funds (since you keep that money), the cash outflow is approximately NGN 5 – NGN 8 million.

United Kingdom: Skilled Worker Route

The UK requires a job offer from a licensed sponsor for most work visas, which adds a layer of complexity but can speed up the process significantly.

Cost Breakdown

  • IELTS UKVI: NGN 400,000 – NGN 430,000 (approximately GBP 200).
  • Skilled Worker visa application: GBP 719 – GBP 1,420 depending on job type (NGN 1.4 – NGN 2.8 million).
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS): GBP 1,035 per year, typically paid for the full visa duration upfront. For a 3-year visa: GBP 3,105 (NGN 6.1 million).
  • TB test: NGN 50,000 – NGN 80,000.
  • One-way flight Lagos to London: NGN 800,000 – NGN 1.8 million.
  • First month's rent + deposit (London): GBP 3,000 – GBP 4,500 (NGN 5.9 – NGN 8.9 million).

Total UK Budget (Single Applicant)

NGN 15 – NGN 22 million including the IHS, which is the single biggest expense. Living outside London can reduce rent costs by 40-60%.

Germany: The Budget-Friendly Option

Germany stands out for its low tuition fees (public universities charge only EUR 250-350 per semester) and a growing demand for skilled workers in engineering, IT, and healthcare.

Cost Breakdown

  • German language course (to B1 level): NGN 400,000 – NGN 1,200,000 at Goethe-Institut or private schools. This is often the longest and most expensive preparation step.
  • Visa application fee: EUR 75 (NGN 128,000).
  • Blocked account (Sperrkonto): EUR 11,904 for one year (NGN 20.3 million). Like Canada's proof of funds, this money is yours but must be in a German blocked account. You withdraw approximately EUR 992/month for living expenses.
  • Health insurance: EUR 110 – EUR 120/month (NGN 188,000 – NGN 205,000/month).
  • Flight Lagos to Frankfurt: NGN 700,000 – NGN 1.5 million.
  • First month's rent (shared flat in Berlin): EUR 400 – EUR 700 (NGN 682,000 – NGN 1.2 million).

Total Germany Budget (Single Applicant)

NGN 8 – NGN 15 million including the blocked account. Cash outflow excluding the blocked account is approximately NGN 2 – NGN 4 million, making Germany the most affordable major destination.

United States: Diversity Visa and Student Routes

The USA has no points-based immigration system like Canada. The main pathways for Nigerians are the Diversity Visa (DV) lottery, student visas (F-1), and employer-sponsored H-1B visas.

DV Lottery Route

  • Application: Free (online at dvprogram.state.gov). Beware of scam sites that charge fees.
  • If selected - immigrant visa processing: USD 330 (NGN 512,000).
  • Medical examination: NGN 200,000 – NGN 300,000.
  • Affidavit of support (if applicable): Requires a US-based sponsor meeting income thresholds.
  • Flight and settling-in: NGN 3 – NGN 8 million depending on destination city.

Student Visa (F-1) Route

  • University tuition: USD 15,000 – USD 55,000/year (NGN 23 – NGN 85 million/year). Community colleges start around USD 8,000/year.
  • SEVIS fee: USD 350 (NGN 543,000).
  • Visa application (DS-160): USD 185 (NGN 287,000).
  • Living expenses: USD 12,000 – USD 20,000/year depending on location.

Total USA Budget

Highly variable. DV lottery winners can relocate for NGN 5 – NGN 12 million. Student route costs NGN 30 – NGN 50+ million for a two-year master's program.

Australia: Skilled Migration

Australia's points-tested skilled migration (subclass 189/190) is similar to Canada's Express Entry but with higher income thresholds and a stronger emphasis on occupations on the Skills Priority List.

Cost Breakdown

  • Skills assessment: AUD 500 – AUD 1,200 depending on the assessing authority (NGN 508,000 – NGN 1.2 million).
  • IELTS or PTE Academic: NGN 310,000 – NGN 400,000.
  • Visa application (subclass 189): AUD 4,640 for the principal applicant (NGN 4.7 million). This is the highest visa fee among the five destinations.
  • Medical and police checks: NGN 250,000 – NGN 400,000.
  • Flight Lagos to Sydney: NGN 1.5 – NGN 3 million.
  • First month's rent (Sydney): AUD 2,000 – AUD 3,500 (NGN 2 – NGN 3.6 million).

Total Australia Budget (Single Applicant)

NGN 12 – NGN 20 million including flights and initial settlement. Australia's higher visa fees are offset by strong earning potential (minimum wage is approximately AUD 24/hour).

Saving Strategy for Japa

With these numbers in mind, here is a practical savings framework:

  1. Set a target amount using the Japa Calculator to model your specific situation.
  2. Save in foreign currency. Open a domiciliary account or use a fintech platform that holds USD, GBP, or EUR. The naira has depreciated by over 70% against the dollar since 2023; saving in naira alone means your target keeps moving.
  3. Create a timeline. Most pathways take 12-24 months. Work backward from your target departure date.
  4. Track expenses ruthlessly. Use the savings goal calculator to monitor your progress and adjust your monthly savings rate.
  5. Budget for the unexpected. Add 15-20% to your estimated total for exchange rate movements, retaken exams, and document re-submissions.

Hidden Costs People Forget

Beyond the line items above, budget for:

  • Document apostille and notarization: NGN 50,000 – NGN 200,000 for multiple documents.
  • Passport renewal: NGN 70,000 for a 64-page passport (plus potential expediting fees).
  • Storage or shipping of personal items: NGN 500,000 – NGN 2 million depending on volume.
  • Travel insurance: NGN 50,000 – NGN 150,000 for the first few months abroad.
  • Currency conversion losses: Parallel market spreads and transfer fees can eat 2-5% of your total.
  • Opportunity cost: Time spent preparing, attending interviews, and waiting for decisions is time not earning. Factor in potential income loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to japa from Nigeria in 2026?

The total cost depends on the destination. For Canada via Express Entry, budget NGN 15-25 million for a single applicant covering all fees, proof of funds, flights, and three months' settlement. For the UK on a Skilled Worker visa, expect NGN 8-15 million. Germany can be done for NGN 5-10 million excluding blocked account funds. Use the Japa Calculator to get a personalized estimate.

What is the cheapest country to relocate to from Nigeria?

Germany is often the most affordable option due to no tuition fees at public universities, lower visa costs, and cheaper rent compared to Canadian or British cities. Total out-of-pocket costs for the Germany route can be as low as NGN 5-10 million, though you will need to learn German to at least B1 level.

Can I relocate from Nigeria without a degree?

Yes, but options are more limited. Canada's Federal Skilled Trades program accepts tradespeople. Germany's vocational visa targets trades like plumbing, welding, and electrical work. The UK has some categories accepting relevant work experience over formal degrees. Nursing and healthcare pathways in several countries may accept diploma holders with relevant certifications.

How long does the japa process take?

From the decision to relocate to actually arriving abroad, expect 12-24 months for most pathways. This includes exam preparation, document gathering, application processing, and visa issuance. Express Entry to Canada averages 12-18 months total. UK Skilled Worker visas can be faster at 6-12 months if you already have a job offer.

Should I save in naira or dollars for relocation?

Given the naira's ongoing depreciation, saving in a stable currency (USD, GBP, EUR, or CAD) is strongly recommended. Options include domiciliary accounts at Nigerian banks, USD savings on fintech platforms, or stablecoins like USDT or USDC.

Mobile data is the lifeblood of internet access in Nigeria. With over 150 million active mobile subscriptions and fixed broadband still limited outside major cities, most Nigerians depend entirely on their SIM card for browsing, streaming, remote work, and social media. The problem is that data prices vary wildly between networks and plan types, and picking the wrong bundle can mean paying two or three times more per gigabyte than you need to.

This guide compares every major data plan from MTN, Airtel, Glo, and 9mobile as of March 2026. We break down prices, data allocations, validity periods, and, most importantly, the cost per GB so you can find the cheapest data plan in Nigeria for your actual usage pattern. Whether you are a light user checking WhatsApp and email, a medium user on social media and YouTube, or a heavy user working from home on Zoom calls, there is a table here for you.

Nigeria's Data Networks at a Glance

Nigeria has four major mobile network operators. Each has different strengths depending on where you live and how you use data.

MTN Nigeria

MTN is the largest network in Nigeria by subscriber count, with over 80 million users. It has the widest 4G LTE coverage across all 36 states and the FCT, making it the default choice in many areas, especially outside Tier-1 cities. MTN's data plans tend to be priced slightly higher than competitors, but the coverage advantage means you actually get to use the data you buy. MTN also offers data rollover on select plans and has a strong USSD menu system for quick plan purchases.

Airtel Nigeria

Airtel is the second-largest network and has been aggressively expanding its 4G footprint since 2023. Airtel is known for its SmartConnect bonus system, which gives new and migrating subscribers double or even triple data on certain plans. In Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, and other major cities, Airtel's speeds often match or exceed MTN. Airtel also tends to run more promotional offers than any other network.

Globacom (Glo)

Glo has historically been the budget-friendly network, offering more data for less money than any competitor. If you live in an area with strong Glo coverage, it is almost always the cheapest option per gigabyte. The trade-off is that Glo's 4G coverage is less extensive than MTN or Airtel, and speeds can be inconsistent in congested areas. Glo also offers popular night browsing plans that give very cheap data between 12 AM and 5 AM.

9mobile (formerly Etisalat)

9mobile has the smallest subscriber base of the big four but maintains decent coverage in major urban areas. Its MoreData plans offer competitive pricing for medium users, and the network is less congested than MTN or Airtel in many locations, which can mean faster actual speeds during peak hours. However, 9mobile's rural coverage is limited, so it works best as a primary SIM only if you are based in a city.

Tips to Save on Data in Nigeria

Beyond choosing the right plan, there are several practical ways to reduce your data consumption and stretch your bundle further.

1. Use USSD Codes for Cheaper Plans

Networks frequently offer hidden or promotional plans that only appear via USSD menus, not on their websites. Dial the main data USSD code (*131# for MTN, *141# for Airtel, *127# for Glo, *229# for 9mobile) and browse through all sub-menus. You may find plans that offer better value than what is advertised on the app or website. Some plans are also cheaper when purchased via USSD because there are no app processing fees.

2. Activate Night Browsing Plans

If you regularly download large files, update apps, or stream content you can watch later, night plans are an excellent way to save. Glo offers 1GB for just ₦100 between midnight and 5 AM. MTN and Airtel have similar late-night offers, though they change frequently. Schedule your downloads, many phones and apps let you set download times, and save your daytime data for browsing and communication.

3. Take Advantage of Data Rollover

MTN's data rollover feature lets you carry unused data into your next plan cycle, as long as you renew your plan before it expires. Airtel offers rollover on SmartConnect 2.0 plans. This means you do not lose data you have already paid for, but you must renew on time. Set a reminder to rebuy your plan a day before it expires to trigger the rollover.

4. Use Wi-Fi Whenever Available

This sounds obvious, but many people leave Wi-Fi off by default on their phones. If your workplace, school, or home has a Wi-Fi connection, make sure your phone connects automatically. Configure your phone to only download app updates and large files over Wi-Fi. On Android, go to Settings, then Network, then Data usage, and enable the restrict background data option for apps you do not need online at all times.

5. Enable Data-Saving Features in Your Browser

Chrome's Lite Mode (now called Data Saver) compresses web pages and can reduce data usage by 30% to 60% on text-heavy sites. Opera Mini has built-in compression that is even more aggressive. For social media, enable data-saving modes in the app settings: Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, and TikTok all have options to reduce image quality and stop auto-playing videos, which are the biggest data consumers.

6. Monitor Your Usage in Real Time

Use your network's balance check code regularly, not just when data runs out. MTN users can dial *461#, Airtel users *140#, Glo users *127*0#, and 9mobile users *228#. Knowing how much data you use daily helps you choose the right plan size. If you consistently use only 8GB of a 15GB plan, switch to a 10GB plan and save the difference.

7. Consider Dual-SIM Strategy

Most smartphones sold in Nigeria are dual-SIM. Use your primary SIM (with the best coverage) for calls and light browsing, and a secondary Glo SIM dedicated to data. This way you get MTN or Airtel's voice reliability with Glo's cheap data pricing. Many heavy users in Nigeria already do this, it is one of the most effective ways to reduce your monthly data bill without sacrificing connectivity.

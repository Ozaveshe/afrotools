A Ghana motor insurance claim is easier to manage when the accident record, policy wording, repair evidence and follow-up trail are treated as one file. The first question is not how much an insurer will pay. It is what happened, who was involved, what cover applied on that date, what evidence is still available and what the policy requires the claimant to do next.

This guide was verified on July 15, 2026 against the National Insurance Commission, or NIC, consumer-rights pages, complaints process, motor-insurance guidance, Motor Compensation Fund procedure and non-life claims-management guideline. The NIC says most policies require prompt notification but the exact period comes from the policy. This article therefore does not invent one national deadline for every motor claim.

What To Do First After A Motor Loss

Safety comes before the insurance file. Seek emergency help where anyone is injured, follow police instructions and avoid actions that create another road hazard. Once immediate safety duties are handled, preserve a factual record while details are still available.

  1. Record the time and location. Save the date, approximate time, road or landmark and direction of travel. If a phone provides coordinates, keep them as supporting information rather than replacing a readable location description.
  2. Identify the people and vehicles. Record driver names, contact details, registration numbers and insurer information where it is safe and lawful to do so. Keep witness contact details separately.
  3. Photograph the scene and damage. Capture wide views, vehicle positions, registration plates, impact areas, road conditions and relevant signs. Do not edit the original files. Preserve their timestamps.
  4. Report to the police where required. Motor accidents, injury, theft and other reportable events may need a police record. Ask the insurer which police document it needs and how to supply it.
  5. Notify the insurer promptly. Use the channel stated in the policy. Ask for a claim number, the assigned contact, the required-document list and any instruction about towing, inspection or repairs.

Do not admit legal liability, promise payment or sign a settlement for another party merely to end a roadside disagreement. Give truthful facts to the police and insurer. A claimant can describe what happened without making a legal conclusion about liability.

Check Which Cover Is Actually In Force

The NIC distinguishes comprehensive, third-party fire and theft, and third-party motor cover. The name of the cover matters because it defines the starting scope of the claim. Third-party cover is not the same as cover for damage to the policyholder's own vehicle. Comprehensive cover can include own damage, but payment still depends on the policy terms, exclusions, excess, insured value and facts of the loss.

Use the full policy wording and all endorsements, not only the sticker, certificate or sales summary. If the insurer relies on an exclusion, request the decision and the specific clause in writing.

Build The Motor Claim Document Packet

An insurer's own claim form and checklist remain controlling. The following packet is a preparation framework, not a claim that every document is mandatory in every case.

Identity, policy and vehicle records

  • Completed and signed claim form.
  • Policy schedule, motor certificate and relevant endorsements.
  • Claimant identification and contact details requested by the insurer.
  • Driver's licence and vehicle registration documents.
  • Premium receipt or other proof of the active cover where requested.
  • Claim number and the insurer's acknowledgement.

Incident and damage evidence

  • A clear written chronology based on known facts.
  • Original photos or video of the scene and damage.
  • Police accident report, theft report or other official record applicable to the event.
  • Witness details and any written statement requested through the proper process.
  • Towing, storage, recovery and emergency receipts.
  • Repair estimate, inspection notes and assessor communication.

Injury or third-party records

Where injury or death is involved, the documentary burden becomes more serious. NIC's motor-complaint procedure refers to police and medical reports and correspondence with the insurer. Its Motor Compensation Fund procedure lists original police and medical records, medical receipts and prescriptions, identity records and additional legal documents depending on whether the petition concerns injury, death, an adult or a minor. Follow the exact route given by the insurer, police, hospital and NIC. Do not publish medical or identity documents in a public share link.

Name files consistently, for example 01-policy-schedule.pdf, 02-claim-form.pdf, 03-police-report.pdf and 04-repair-estimate.pdf. Keep the originals unchanged and create a separate submission copy. A missing document log should state what is missing, who should provide it, the request date and the next follow-up date.

A Controlled Claim Workflow

1. Open the claim through the policy channel. Give a factual loss notice and request written acknowledgement. Record the claim number. If the insurer accepts reports through an app or messaging service, export or screenshot the acknowledgement without exposing it publicly.

2. Confirm the checklist. Ask the insurer to identify required documents, acceptable formats, the person handling the file and any deadline in the policy. Mark items as required, submitted, accepted, replaced or outstanding.

3. Preserve the vehicle for inspection. Follow written instructions on towing, storage, assessment and repair. Starting repairs before an insurer or assessor can inspect may complicate the evidence trail. If emergency work is needed to prevent further loss, record why it was necessary and preserve photographs, invoices and replaced parts where practical.

4. Submit one indexed packet. Use a cover page listing every attachment. Keep proof of delivery. If a large file must be split, use stable part numbers and state the total number of parts.

5. Reconcile requests. When the insurer asks for more information, link the response to the claim number and the original request. Do not resend a different version of a statement without explaining the correction.

6. Maintain a contact log. Record the date, channel, person, subject, promise and next action for every material contact. Keep email and letter copies. The NIC specifically advises policyholders to keep communication records and follow up in writing.

7. Review the decision. Compare any approval, partial settlement or rejection with the schedule, excess, valuation basis, assessor report and cited policy clause. Ask for the calculation in writing where it is not clear.

Assessment, Repairs And Settlement

An estimate from a workshop is evidence of proposed repair cost. It is not automatically the insurer's accepted settlement. The insurer may appoint an assessor, request another estimate, authorise specified work or negotiate a cash amount under the policy. Keep the estimate, assessment, authorisation and final invoice as separate records.

The excess is the amount or proportion the policy says the claimant bears. Betterment may arise when an old component is replaced with a new one, depending on the policy and settlement basis. The NIC's motor guidance warns that a policyholder may contribute because of an excess or because new parts and broader repair work improve the vehicle beyond the damaged component. Ask the insurer to separate each deduction and identify its basis.

For a vehicle treated as a total loss, request the valuation method, pre-loss value, salvage treatment, excess and other deductions in writing. Do not assume the purchase price, financing balance or advertised replacement price is automatically the insured settlement. The schedule and policy wording determine the starting contract position.

Before signing a discharge form, check that the claimant name, vehicle, claim number, settlement amount and conditions are correct. NIC's published non-life claims-management guideline says that, after all documents are submitted and liability is admissible, motor settlement should be negotiated and the discharge form arranged on the same day or within two working days. It also says payment should follow within five working days after receipt of the discharge form. Those guideline timings do not remove the earlier investigation, document-completion and admissibility stages.

How To Escalate An Unresolved Claim

The current NIC complaints page says to contact the insurer or intermediary first. If the issue remains unresolved, a consumer can use the official NIC Complaints Hub. The NIC asks for the policy or claim number, key dates, documents and correspondence. This makes a clean written trail more useful than repeated unrecorded calls.

  1. Write to the insurer's complaints desk with the claim number, a short chronology, the disputed decision or delay, the policy clause involved and the resolution requested.
  2. Attach only relevant documents and a numbered index. Redact unrelated personal data where the receiving process allows it.
  3. Give a reasonable date for a written response and preserve delivery evidence.
  4. If unresolved, file through the NIC Complaints Hub and retain the NIC reference number.
  5. Respond promptly to a request from the Commission for more information.

A complaint should distinguish the issue. Delay, disputed liability, disputed value, an excess calculation, an exclusion and poor communication are not the same problem. State the exact remedy sought, such as a written decision, a calculation, reconsideration with a missing document, or payment of an agreed settlement.

When The Motor Compensation Fund May Matter

Ghana's Motor Compensation Fund is not a general replacement for comprehensive insurance. The NIC says it can assist people injured in motor accidents, or dependants of people who die, when compensation cannot be obtained from an insurer in specified situations. The current statutory-funds page describes uninsured or untraced vehicles. The detailed NIC overview also identifies expired or false insurance, hit-and-run vehicles, certain driver-licence problems and other valid grounds on which an insurer repudiates a claim.

The published procedure says the victim must first claim from the insurer or vehicle owner. If that fails, the victim may petition the Fund in writing or through the NIC complaints system. The Fund then requires its form and supporting evidence. Injury and death petitions have different document lists, and the NIC labels awards as gratuitous rather than a contractual entitlement.

Do not treat an own-vehicle repair dispute as automatically eligible for this fund. Review the NIC eligibility description and contact the Commission for the specific case. Preserve the insurer's repudiation letter, police accident report and medical or legal documents that the procedure identifies.

Keep A Private, Verifiable Claim Pack

Motor claim records can contain national identity details, signatures, medical information, vehicle ownership records, addresses, phone numbers and bank information. Store them in a controlled folder. Do not put raw documents into analytics, public cloud links, search-indexed pages or screenshots used for support.

A strong folder has four sections: originals, submission copies, correspondence and decisions. Keep a read-only original photo set. Add a document index with filenames, dates and source. Track each submission by version and delivery proof. Export the AfroTools checklist as a working aid, but keep the insurer's official forms and acknowledgements as the authoritative claim record.

Review the file before each follow-up. The next action should be specific: obtain the police report, send the signed form, confirm assessor appointment, request the written calculation or escalate the unresolved complaint. A vague status such as "pending" hides the actual blocker.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which East African country has the highest PAYE tax rate?

Uganda tops out at 40% (above UGX 10,000,000/month). Kenya is 35%, Tanzania and Rwanda both cap at 30%.

How does social security compare across East Africa?

Tanzania's NSSF is 10% employee + 10% employer — the highest in the region. Kenya's combined NSSF/SHIF/AHL is lower in percentage terms. Rwanda's RSSB is the lightest at 3.5% employee (pension + medical).

Which country is cheapest for employers to hire in?

Rwanda generally has the lowest employer social security burden (5.5% total). Kenya is second (~4.5–5.5%). Tanzania and Uganda are higher due to NSSF structures.

Does Tanzania have higher take-home than Kenya?

Tanzania's top PAYE rate is lower (30% vs 35%), but Tanzania's NSSF (10%) is significantly higher. At equivalent purchasing power, results vary by salary band. Use both calculators to compare your specific salary.

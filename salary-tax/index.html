How PAYE Works

PAYE Tax Across Africa Explained

PAYE — Pay As You Earn — is the standard mechanism by which governments across Africa collect employment income tax. Instead of workers filing and paying tax at year-end, employers calculate, deduct and remit income tax on behalf of their employees each pay period. This makes income tax collection automatic and reduces the compliance burden on individual taxpayers.

Every African country applies PAYE differently. Tax bands, thresholds, social contribution rates and deductible items vary significantly from one jurisdiction to another. For example, Kenya's PAYE includes SHIF (2.75%), Affordable Housing Levy (1.5%) and NSSF Tier I and II — all of which must be correctly computed to arrive at accurate take-home pay. South Africa's PAYE includes Medical Tax Credits, primary rebates and UIF. Nigeria's PAYE has two regimes — PITA 2025 and NTA 2026 — that can be compared side by side.

AfroTools builds each calculator directly from the official legislation of each country's revenue authority. When tax laws change — like Kenya's Finance Act 2024 which repealed NHIF relief and introduced SHIF, or Ethiopia's Proclamation 1395/2025 which updated tax bands — AfroTools updates within days. Most generic calculators lag months or years behind these changes, resulting in incorrect take-home pay.

What is PAYE in Africa?
PAYE is the system by which employers deduct income tax from salaries before payment and remit it to the national tax authority monthly. Almost every African country uses PAYE as the primary income tax collection mechanism.

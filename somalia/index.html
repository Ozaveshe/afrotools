About Somalia

Somalia Tax & Finance Overview

Somalia's tax system is managed by the Federal Government of Somalia (FGS) tax authority and applies personal income tax rates from 0% to 20%, denominated in the Somali Shilling (SOS). The relatively low maximum rate of 20% reflects the country's evolving tax infrastructure and the ongoing efforts to establish a more comprehensive revenue collection framework. The progressive income tax bands provide exemptions for the lowest earners, with rates increasing to the top rate on higher income. Somalia's formal social security system is still in development, and mandatory social security contributions are not universally applied across the private sector, though government employees may be subject to pension contributions. The country does not currently levy a broad-based VAT, though sales taxes and customs duties apply to certain goods and services, particularly imported items. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers in the formal sector are expected to withhold income tax from employee salaries and remit it to the tax authority within established deadlines. Somalia's economy operates with significant informality, driven by livestock, agriculture, telecommunications, and remittances from the Somali diaspora, which represent a major source of income for many households. The US Dollar circulates widely alongside the Somali Shilling, and many formal sector salaries are denominated in USD. The country has been making significant progress in rebuilding its fiscal institutions, with support from international partners, to strengthen tax administration and broaden the revenue base.

Egypt VAT FAQ

Common VAT Questions

What is the standard VAT rate in Egypt?

The standard VAT rate in Egypt is 14%. This applies to most goods and services. A reduced rate of 5% applies to essential items like staple foods and medicines. Exports are zero-rated.

Which items have the reduced 5% VAT rate?

The 5% reduced rate applies to essential goods including: staple foods (grains, oils, sugar), medicines and medical devices, books and newspapers, and certain other basic necessities. A complete list is available from ETA.

What services are exempt from VAT in Egypt?

Exempt services include: unprocessed food supplies, medical and health services, education and training, financial services (banking, insurance), and certain government services. These do not charge VAT.

How do I calculate reverse VAT in Egypt?

To extract VAT from a total amount: Divide the total by 1.14 to get the net, then subtract net from total to get VAT amount. This calculator does this automatically when you select "Remove VAT" mode.

What is the VAT registration threshold in Egypt?

Businesses with annual turnover exceeding EGP 500,000 are required to register for VAT with the Egyptian Tax Authority. Smaller businesses can voluntarily register for VAT.

How often do VAT returns need to be filed?

VAT-registered businesses must file monthly VAT returns with the Egyptian Tax Authority and remit VAT monthly. The filing deadline is typically the 20th of the following month.

Egypt taxes employment income through seven progressive bands with a unique Bracket Exclusion Rule — where all taxable income falls within a single bracket, only that bracket's rate applies to the full amount. The ETA administers all income tax. The fiscal year runs July to June.

NOSI (National Organisation for Social Insurance) contributions are 11% employee and 18.75% employer on insurable salary, subject to an annual ceiling of EGP 174,000. NOSI provides retirement pension, disability benefits and survivors' pension for all formally employed workers.

Egypt is Africa's third-largest economy with over 105 million people. Cairo is Africa's largest city. Suez Canal revenues, tourism, remittances and hydrocarbons are key economic drivers. The ETA has been modernising through digital e-filing systems and expanded taxpayer registration.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Bracket Exclusion Rule in Egypt?
If all your taxable income falls within one bracket, the entire amount is taxed at that single rate — not split across lower bands. This significantly benefits lower-middle income earners.
What are NOSI rates in Egypt?
Employees contribute 11%; employers contribute 18.75% of insurable salary. Annual insurable salary is capped at EGP 174,000. NOSI provides pension, disability and survivors' benefits.
What is Egypt's fiscal year?
Egypt's fiscal year runs July to June. PAYE is withheld monthly but annual returns and reconciliations align with the July–June cycle.

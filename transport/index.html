Frequently Asked Questions

Why is logistics so expensive in Africa?
Logistics costs in Africa average 75% of product cost vs 15% in the US and 25% globally. Contributing factors include poor road infrastructure, multiple border crossings, high import duties on vehicles and parts, limited cold chain, fuel price volatility, and inefficient customs procedures. AfroTools helps quantify these costs so businesses and individuals can plan better.
What are vehicle import duties in African countries?
Rates vary dramatically. Nigeria charges up to 75% total (35% duty + 35% levy + 5% ECOWAS) on most vehicles, making car imports very expensive. Kenya charges 25% IDF + 14% VAT + 20% excise and bans vehicles over 8 years old. South Africa charges 25% ad valorem + 14% VAT. Our Vehicle Import Duty Calculator shows the full breakdown for each country.
How much do boda-boda riders actually earn per month?
A Nairobi boda-boda rider doing 20 trips/day at KSh 150 average earns about KSh 3,000/day gross. After fuel (~KSh 600), bike hire if not owned (~KSh 300/day), and maintenance allowance (~KSh 150/day), net income is roughly KSh 1,950/day or KSh 50,700/month. Our Boda-Boda Income Calculator shows country-specific breakdowns for Kenya, Nigeria, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, and Ghana.
What is volumetric weight in shipping?
Couriers charge based on whichever is greater — actual weight or volumetric (dimensional) weight. Volumetric weight = (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ 5000 for air freight, or ÷ 4000 for some couriers. A light but bulky package (e.g., styrofoam) may cost more than its actual weight suggests. Our Shipping Weight Calculator shows chargeable weight for all major couriers.
How long does customs clearance take in Nigeria vs Kenya?
Nigeria's Apapa Port is notoriously slow — clearance typically takes 15 to 45 days due to congestion and documentation requirements. Kenya's Mombasa Port is faster at 5-15 days, while Nairobi's ICD takes 3-7 days. Rwanda is the most efficient at 3-7 days. South Africa's Durban and Cape Town ports clear in 3-10 days. Our Customs Time Estimator shows estimates by country, goods type, and documentation completeness.
Should I buy a car with a loan or pay cash in Africa?
It depends on the interest rate, your alternative investment returns, and your cash flow needs. African car loan rates range from 12% (South Africa) to 25%+ (Nigeria, Kenya). If your business returns exceed the loan rate, taking a loan may make sense. Our Car Loan vs Cash Comparator shows total cost of each option with country-specific pre-filled rates and opportunity cost analysis.

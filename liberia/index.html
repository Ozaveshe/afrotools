Liberia taxes employment income through a progressive system with four bands rising to 25%, administered by the LRA. Both the Liberian Dollar (LRD) and US Dollar (USD) are accepted as legal tender, with USD widely preferred for large transactions. Employers withhold income tax monthly and remit to the LRA. The fiscal year follows the calendar year.

Social security is managed by NASSCORP — National Social Security and Welfare Corporation. Both employees and employers contribute 3% of gross salary each. NASSCORP provides retirement pension payments and work injury compensation for formally employed workers. NASSCORP has been undergoing modernisation to improve systems.

Liberia is a West African nation founded in 1847 by freed American and Caribbean slaves — one of Africa's oldest republics. English is the official language. Rubber, iron ore, gold and timber are key exports. The LRA has been expanding the tax base and improving voluntary compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is income tax calculated in Liberia?
Liberia uses 4 progressive bands with a top rate of 25%. Employers withhold monthly and remit to the LRA. Both LRD and USD are accepted legal tender.
What does NASSCORP provide in Liberia?
NASSCORP collects 3% from both employees and employers. Benefits include retirement pension and work injury compensation.
Why is USD used in Liberia?
USD has been used alongside the Liberian Dollar since the country's founding connections to the United States. Both currencies are official legal tender, with USD preferred for larger transactions.

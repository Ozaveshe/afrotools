Calculation methodology

The calculator adds 13% to a tax-exclusive amount or divides a tax-inclusive amount by 1.13. It permits 0% only after the user confirms an exact export of goods under Section 1000 and retained evidence. Telecommunications is deliberately not modeled because the enacted 13% plus 5% surtax conflicts with the LRA notice stating 15%.

Known limitations

  • Telecommunications is blocked because current official LRA sources conflict between 15% and an enacted 13% plus 5% surtax.
  • Zero-rating is limited to an exact export of goods with retained transaction evidence.
  • The tool does not infer exemptions, registration, deductions, invoice rules, filing or remittance.
  • Planning estimate only; confirm treatment with LRA or a qualified adviser.

Test-case examples

Input: Net amount: 1,000 at the displayed standard VAT rate.
Expected: VAT equals 1,000 multiplied by the displayed rate; total equals net amount plus VAT.
Why: Confirms the core VAT-exclusive calculation path and makes the rate dependency auditable.

Input: VAT-inclusive amount with the displayed standard VAT rate.
Expected: Net amount equals total divided by 1 plus the rate; VAT equals total minus net amount.
Why: Confirms reverse VAT handling for invoices that already include tax.

About Morocco's Tax System

Morocco taxes employment income through the IR — Impôt sur le Revenu — with six progressive bands. The annual tax-free threshold is MAD 30,000. Rates rise to 38% on the highest band. Professional deductions of 20% of gross salary (capped) reduce taxable income. Employers withhold IR monthly and remit to the DGI. Morocco has a well-established tax administration and is the second-largest economy in North Africa.

Social contributions are split between CNSS pension (4.48% employee, 8.6% employer) and AMO healthcare contributions applied separately. The combined system provides comprehensive pension and medical coverage for all formal sector workers. CNSS is one of North Africa's largest social security institutions.

Morocco is North Africa's second-largest economy. Phosphate exports, tourism, remittances and a growing manufacturing sector drive growth. The Dirham (MAD) is managed by Bank Al-Maghrib. Casablanca is the commercial capital. Morocco is investing heavily in renewables and positioning as a hub for European and African markets.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Morocco's IR tax bands?
Morocco's IR has 6 bands: 0% on income up to MAD 30,000/year, then 10%, 20%, 30%, 34% and 38% on the highest band. Professional deductions of 20% (capped) and family charges reduce taxable income.
What are CNSS and AMO rates in Morocco?
CNSS pension: 4.48% employee, 8.6% employer. AMO healthcare contributions apply separately. Combined employer costs represent a significant portion of total employment cost.
What is Morocco's annual tax-free threshold?
The annual tax-free threshold is MAD 30,000. Professional deductions (20% of employment income, capped) further reduce taxable income before IR bands are applied.

About Morocco

Morocco Tax & Finance Overview

Morocco's tax system is administered by the Direction Generale des Impots (DGI) and applies a progressive personal income tax known as the Impot sur le Revenu (IR) with rates from 0% to 38%, denominated in the Moroccan Dirham (MAD). The progressive structure features six tax bands, with the first MAD 30,000 of annual income exempt from tax, and rates increasing through 10%, 20%, 30%, 34%, to the top rate of 38% on annual income exceeding MAD 180,000. Employees in Morocco contribute to the Caisse Nationale de Securite Sociale (CNSS) at approximately 4.48% of gross salary (capped at MAD 6,000/month), while employers contribute approximately 8.98% plus additional charges for AMO health insurance, family allowances, and vocational training. The total employer social charge is approximately 21.09% of gross salary. Morocco applies a standard VAT rate of 20% on most goods and services, with reduced rates of 14%, 10%, and 7% on specific categories including basic necessities, water, electricity, and pharmaceutical products. The fiscal year follows the calendar year, and employers must withhold IR from employee salaries and remit it to the DGI monthly. Morocco's diversified economy spans manufacturing, tourism, agriculture, mining (phosphates), and a growing automotive and aerospace industry. The country's strategic position bridging Africa and Europe makes it a major hub for investment and trade.

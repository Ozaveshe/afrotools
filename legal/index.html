Frequently Asked Questions

Are documents generated by these tools legally binding?
The templates generated by these tools are starting points only. They are not legal advice and should not be relied upon as formal legal documents without review by a qualified lawyer in your jurisdiction. Always have important documents reviewed by a licensed attorney before signing or using them in legal proceedings.
Which African countries have data protection laws?
Many African countries now have data protection legislation, active regulators, or draft frameworks. Start with the regulator-linked tools for Nigeria NDPA, South Africa POPIA, Kenya DPA, Ghana Act 843, Rwanda Law 058/2021, Morocco Law 09-08, and the cross-border comparator for multi-country processing.
How does business registration work across OHADA countries?
OHADA (Organisation pour l'Harmonisation en Afrique du Droit des Affaires) is a uniform business law framework covering 17 mainly francophone African countries including Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Cameroon, and DR Congo. OHADA standardises company types (SARL, SA, GIE) and commercial law, making it easier to operate across member states. Companies formed under OHADA law are recognised across all member states.
What is ARIPO vs OAPI for trademark registration?
ARIPO (African Regional Intellectual Property Organization) covers 22 mainly anglophone countries (including Kenya, Zimbabwe, Zambia, Botswana, Uganda). OAPI (Organisation Africaine de la Propriété Intellectuelle) covers 17 mainly francophone countries (Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Morocco equivalent). A single ARIPO or OAPI application gives regional trademark protection — more cost-effective than filing in each country individually.
Do most African countries have inheritance tax?
Most African countries do NOT have inheritance or estate tax. Notable exceptions include South Africa (estate duty: 20% on estates over R3.5M), some francophone African countries (droits de succession: typically 5–20%), and some OHADA member states. Most anglophone African countries have abolished estate duty. However, probate fees and stamp duty typically apply during estate administration.
What is the difference between a statutory declaration and an affidavit?
Both are written sworn statements — but an affidavit is typically used in court proceedings and sworn before a commissioner of oaths or magistrate. A statutory declaration is used outside court (e.g., for administrative purposes like notifying a name change, replacing lost documents, or making official declarations). Both require the maker to declare the contents are true, and making a false statement is a criminal offence in most jurisdictions.

Legal Complexity in Africa — The Problem We're Solving

Africa's legal landscape is uniquely complex. The continent operates under at least six major legal traditions: English common law, French civil law, Portuguese civil law, customary law, Islamic (Sharia) law, and Roman-Dutch law in southern Africa. Many countries operate under dual or even triple legal systems simultaneously.

For businesses, this means a startup in Lagos faces a completely different registration environment than one in Nairobi, Abidjan, or Cairo. A will valid in Cape Town may be invalid in Kano. A data processing agreement compliant with POPIA may not satisfy Nigeria's NDPA requirements. AfroTools bridges this knowledge gap with country-specific, plain-language legal tools.

  • Business registration in Rwanda takes 6 hours — the same process in some other countries takes 60 days
  • Only ~5% of Nigerians have written wills, leaving estates subject to customary intestacy rules that can disinherit spouses and daughters
  • Data protection laws and regulators are expanding across Africa, so privacy tools now link to primary regulator checks where available
  • GDPR affects any African company serving EU customers — our comparator helps you understand exactly what applies
  • Missing annual returns deadlines can lead to company deregistration — our reminder tool ensures you stay compliant

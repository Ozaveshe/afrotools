What this snapshot means

AfroTools requests ranked market data from CoinGecko through a dedicated server function. Each returned row must include a parseable provider timestamp that is no more than 30 minutes old. Rows that fail that test are discarded. The page reports the number that remain, so “fresh rows” is a measured count—not a catalogue promise.

NGN and ZAR are the only African quote currencies exposed because they are the currencies currently verified against the provider’s supported-currency endpoint. AfroTools does not combine a USD crypto price with a separate foreign-exchange rate and present the result as provider data.

Before you act

  • These are indicative global market snapshots, not executable quotes.
  • Exchange, bank and P2P prices can include spreads, fees and local liquidity effects.
  • CSV and JSON downloads include the source, quote currency and freshness timestamps.
  • This is market information, not financial advice.

Crypto price snapshot FAQ

Why are only NGN and ZAR available?

They are the African quote currencies currently verified as supported by the primary provider. Unsupported conversions are not estimated or presented as live provider prices.

What happens when CoinGecko is unavailable or returns old rows?

The table closes safely. AfroTools shows an unavailable state instead of using an expired cache, the separate hourly scheduler snapshot or a fabricated conversion.

What does “oldest provider update” mean?

It is the oldest CoinGecko timestamp among all rows that survived the 30-minute freshness check. Individual row timestamps remain available in each coin’s details.
